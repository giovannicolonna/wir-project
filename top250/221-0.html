Thanks to mrbubbler for sharing this one at his tasting.

Canned 5/4/15.

Pours a murky golden-orange with a foamy beige head that settles to a partial film on top of the beer. Small dots of lace drip into the remaining beer on the drink down. Smell is of citrus fruit, citrus zest, berry, and earth aromas. Taste is much the same with citrus zest, berry, and earth flavors on the finish. There is a mild amount of hop bitterness on the palate with each sip. This beer has a lower level of carbonation with a slightly crisp mouthfeel. Overall, this is a very good beer with a great mix of citrus and berry hop characteristics all around.

Serving type: can.

Yellow Rose is my favorite beer. And that's without question. So when I found out one of my favorite breweries would be brewing a Mosaic IPA, I flew in (coincidentally), stood in line, and got a case of the stuff. I was not disappointed.

Aroma is spot on: mosaic front, mosaic finish. Taste: incredible. Very very good mosaic flavor without suffering from the mellowing or cardboarding that I feel most mosaic beers have suffered from on the finish.

This is wonderful. And the fact that I found three cans lurking at the back of my fridge five weeks later that were still good is just icing on the cake.

The beer comes in a golden yellow, with touches of orange, slightly hazy.

A dangerously drinkable IPA, although slightly restrained and a hint weaker than the Nelson and Citra.

4.15/5  rDev -7.8%
look: 3.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

8/15/15 canning date. 16oz. can into a Carton Boat Beer glass.

Pours a foamy, 2 finger, sticky white head with good retention. Beer is a slightly cloudy peach/mango. Lacing is stringy and patchy with good stick. Nice.

Nose is citrus, sweet mango, pine, resin and fresh cut grass. A dank hop presence is pervasive throughout. Deliciously aromatic!

Opens juicy mango, starfruit and citrus. Big, cane sugar backbone in the middle balances out a piney, sticky, dank hop bitterness. Ends grassy and resiny and bone dry. Sweet and lightly bitter pine cone in the aftertaste. Fantastic balance!

Light bodied with moderate carbonation. Smooth with some slight bubbles on the palate and goes down rather aggressive and quenching. Dry finish with a resiny, sticky aftertaste. Excellent!

Probably the best single hop IPA that Other Half does... which is saying a lot. Definitely worth trying!

Other Half makes a lot of IPAs, some tastier than others. To me, this guy is the king. I've had it in cans and on tap (these are observations from a recent growler pour), and it is always beautiful. Tingles the nostrils without an off note. The taste follows with a mosaic explosion of resin and citrus, followed by some malty quiet biscuit-bread notes. If it's in any way underwhelming, that's only because this beer smells like perfection. Medium light on the tongue. Totally refreshing. Mosaic is the absolute real deal.
