Poured from a 750 mL bottle into a Bruery tulip glass. Acquired in a trade with Rishi - thank you kind sir.

Pours a deep brown, nearly black opaque body with a 2 finger mocha brown head that reduces at a medium rate to a ring of foam. LIttle lacing.

Aromas are strong and potent but very inviting - chocolate, bourbon, vanilla, and booze with some coffee in the background.

On the tongue lots of complex and super delicious flavors - milk chocolate, vanilla, butterscotch, caramel upfront - follow through is roasted coffee and some booziness (expected at this abv), finish is toasty, roasty coffee and malt and semisweet chocolate. Mouthfeel is thick, rich, and full bodied with low carbonation, you just have to let this one warm up a little bit.

This was the last of the black tuesday variants that i had to try, and this was my favorite one thus far. Most complex of them, and the coffee/mocha character works well. Recommended.

Went to pick up my allocations at The Bruery last night and a kind Hoarders' Society member shared a bit of his pour with me. I couldn't be more thankful.

A: Very dark brown, almost black; no head, very little lacing; the thing I noticed first about this beer was that it had great "legs" like a fine wine. I've never seen beer with "legs" before.

S: Chocolate fudge, fresh espresso beans, caramel, oak, and a slight hint of coconut. Then more chocolate.

T: Where do I begin with this beauty? Starts out with an explosion of bourbon-chocolaty goodness, then mellows out along the middle of the tongue and transitions into warm flavors of vanilla, espresso, oak, coconut, and more chocolate. This is decadence incarnate. Just phenomenal.

M: Medium-light viscosity; oily, buttery, with a very slight boozy warmth as it goes down. Honestly, I'm amazed at how smooth this beer is considering how high of an ABV it carries.

O: I've tried well over a thousand beers in the past 4 years of my journey as a craft beer enthusiast, and this takes the cake as the absolute best beer I've ever had the pleasure of placing on my tongue. Incredible.

TboneRN, Sep 14, 2015
4.59/5  rDev +4.8%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 4.5

2015 Vintage

Appearance: Very dark brown with a modest beige head which dissipated fairly quickly. The head on this beer was larger and hung around longer than any of the Black Tuesday's or variants that I've had. This fixed my biggest gripe with Black Tuesday and/or it's variants. Considerable legs on this one.

Smell: Upfront coffee followed by Bourbon, brown sugar, chocolate/cocoa, mild vanilla, and a bit of coffee/roasted malt aroma. nice nose on this one.

Taste: As the nose suggested coffee, Bourbon, raisins, vanilla, brown sugar, chocolate, and some oak/roast malts appearing in the finish along with the coffee notes. The upfront coffee helps to subdue the sweetness of the base black Tuesday and helps the drink-ability. Very mild alcohol presence especially in light of the 18.6% ABV . Nice barrel presence and flavor complexity. Fairly sweet in the middle of the swallow but the finish is surprisingly dry finishing with the coffee notes which appear to be a smooth roast.

Mouth-feel: Medium bodied, fairly thin for such a big beer but the significant ethanol presence has something to do with that. The carbonation is moderate and spot on for the beer. It's scary the beer has such a lack of an alcohol presence for the ABV.

Overall: Great beer. The coffee is well integrated and along with the cacao nibs tame the sweetness from the base Black Tuesday. Nice complexity in the flavor profile. My favorite coffee stout at the moment.

The richness is fantastic, and the balance of bourbon and esperesso on the finish is lovely. Clean with just a touch of leftover sugar on the tongue.

M: Full bodied and heavy with medium-low carbonation, but that is to be expected. Slick, this beer coats your tongue and mouth. Not unpleasant though...and it grows on you.

O: Really wishing they bottled this in those 8 oz. bottles that Anchor used to use for Old Foghorn to justify a single serving. Very glad to have the opportunity to try this and I will be happy to share my remaining bottles with friends and family. Unique to be sure and though my taste falls to lambic, gueuze and saison of late, this is not to be missed.
