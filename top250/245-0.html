12/29/2014 Bottle

Very hazy yellow-orange, billowy head with good retention and lacing.

Smells like freshly cut pineapple, including the skin. Not the most aromatic IPA I've run into, but nice.

Very fruity. Peaches, lighter notes of pineapple.

A bit low on carbonation, but clean with a long fruity linger and minimal bitterness.

TBH, I've been a bit disappointed with most Trillium IPAs, finding them to have something of a vegetal off-flavor, particularly as they warm. This one, however, has none of that and is pure deliciousness. I don't know if it's just a matter of being fresher, or being kept nice and cold by being shipped through Polar Vortex 2015, but this is legit.

Really stoked about this one! Loved Mettle, so Heavy Mettle is a must try. Pours a murky yellow (just how I like it) with a thock, white crown. Blobs and globs of lace. The nose is magnificent. Puréed fruit juice hops. Zesty, tropical fruit hops, passion fruit some moss and a tad if green onion. Chunks of mango. Very impressive. The hoppiness is incredibly intense. Starts with a huge passion fruit zap along with melon and mango. Guava. I believe they used Galaxy hops and they shine! Hints of grass and moss. Citrus and nectarine. Dry, pale malt. Fruit juice elixir. Hops are stunningly intense. Flavor lasts for days. Surprisingly not ridiculously bitter. Hop-oil feel. No booze. Sneakily drinkable. Trillium is vaulting into legendary status and this may be their best one yet. What a brilliant, modern Imperial IPA.

Via bottle.

Bottle shared by Curran tonight.

Pours a bright, radiant orange, with a half inch head, and a good amount of lacing.

This smells so bright, pungent, and aromatic- orange, peach, grapefruit, and mango, with a bit of weed and stuff.

This is one of the brightest and most delicious IPAs I've ever had. It's so juicy and sunny. So much citrus and tropical fruits- orange, pineapple, and mango galore, with a little bit of dankness on the back end. There's a small amount of bitterness on the back end, with very little malt character, a little bit of pale cracker on the end.

This is pretty thick bodied, with a creamy and pulpy mouthfeel, really just thick and nice, a good aspect of the 9.3% ABV. The other good aspect of the ABV here is that the alcohol is not noticeable at all- there's no booziness, whatsoever.

I like to say that certain beers are "next level," but I feel like this might be the most next of level of all the things that are next level.

BMMillsy, Jan 17, 2015
4.4/5  rDev -3.1%

750ml bottle dated 8/16/2014. Poured into a Lawson's snifter 8/28/2014 at 40F.

A- Dark hazy peach-coloured body, three finger thick, frothy cappucino-like offwhite head with very good hang time. Wispy lacing covers the glass. Altogether, the head is a real winner here. [4.5]

S- Pine-forward aromas mix in with sweet caramel. Smells fairly resinous. There is also a blast of tropical fruit, mango and some pineapple. For the most part though, pine and caramel are the predominant contributors. [4]

F- Delicious tropical fruit hops up front. Sweet mango, pineapple juice-like flavours, which start to mix in with some sweet caramel and tapioca making for a substantial malt backbone which never quite overcome the hops, which is just the way I like it. More tropical fruit and a bitter pine kick on the backend. Very smooth and well-integrated between the hops and the malts. [4.5]

M- Medium bodied, smooth and silky whilst striking the balance between dry and resinous, very much like Congress Street does. Finish is a pine and caramel smoothness that isn't altogether too bitter to keep you from continuing to drink it. Very easy to drink, disturbingly so for a 9.3% DIPA- it goes down like a 6% IPA. [4.75]

O- I came into this one expecting a good DIPA, thinking that this brewery's hoppy offerings can't be as good as that of Tree House or Alchemist, but you know what, this is excellent stuff. As I mentioned in my review of Congress Street, there's just something in the water in New England that makes the mouthfeel with just the right amount of softness that just enables me to gobble down the hops. Still, it could use more vibrant burst of hop aromas on the nose; that would make the beer that much better. I look forward to trying other Trillium offerings. [4.5]

Final Score: 4.4

Poured from a 750 mL bottle into a willibecher. This was bottled yesterday, picked up from the brewery an hour ago.

Appearance: Pours a cloudy, near-opaque honey/amber and leaves a full, two-finger white head, that lingers for a long time, leaving full lacing down the glass. (4.5)

Smell: Big mango and citrus hops, grapefruit juice, citrus rinds, a bit of grass. Super hop forward, refreshing, big aroma. (4.75)

Taste: Matches the smell pretty well. Starts with big bitter hop flavors, moves to juicy citrus + tropical hops flavors. Some malts in there to round it out. Really big flavor. For such a big IPA, the finish is wonderfully smooth. (4.75)

Mouth-feel: Medium to high carbonation with a medium to heavy, creamy body. Quite a presence in the mouth. Finishes smooooth. (4.75)

Overall: This is a wonderful, big IPA. Really well crafted to give huge, smooth flavor. It's not syrupy or high in alcohol taste like some of the 9%+ IPA's out there. Definitely not for the new-to-IPA's drinkers. Tons of hops in here. (4.75)

pmarlowe, Jan 02, 2015

Drank from a 25.4 fl oz (750 ml) bottle acquired in an in-person trade with muzzy
081814
Served in a snifter

The cloudy, glowing, peach colored liquid was topped by an inch-tall, creamy and bubbly, bone white crown. Solid head retention. Thick, streaky lacing coated the glass and lasted until the beer was finished. With time, the chill haze eased up and the liquid turned a bit more transparent.

Citrus notes dominated in the aroma. Candy coated oranges came through strong. Lots of tropical fruits as well. Hefty dose of mango. Bit of guava. Really bright, fruity and intense. Slightly dank and weed-like. Some peach and apricot notes were also apparent. As the beer warmed, papaya and apricot notes really came forth.

The flavor profile was also super hoppy and citrusy. Juicy with fresh orange notes. Some orange peel came through as well. Loads of peaches and apricots. There was a subtle tropical fruit presence. Touch of mango. The beer finished with notes of pine sap, ruby red grapefruit and a faint, rind-like bitterness. A hint of alcohol was detectible initially but it soon dissipated. All in all, immensely flavorful and remarkably well balanced.

The mouthfeel was slick, rich, creamy and supple. A super fine and frothy effervescence gave the liquid a fluffy, tongue-blanketing quality.

As much as I loved this beer, it lacked some of the intensely bright, tropical aromatics and flavors that Mettle had the first time I sampled it on tap. Nevertheless, this is an outstanding double IPA that reminded me a lot of Heady Topper.
