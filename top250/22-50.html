Aroma: Loads of grapefruit, pineapple, mango, dank hops, and a little pine. Absolutely amazing smell to this beer!

Appearance: Super hazy yellow, resembles pineapple juice with a little bit of a glowing bright orange hue to it, extremely fluffy head is left on the pour that stays for a long time, leaves amazing lacing on the glass, one of the best looking beers I have seen!

T: I'm blown away by the taste of this beer, loads of mango, pineapple, some buttering grapefruit, a little bit of pine and the right amount of bitterness, and it is all so well balanced. You get a slam of hops then it begins to transition to a lovely crisp light finish that is also very smooth, all the flavors play their own role and none are overpowered by another, truly amazing flavor profile!

Palate: Light crisp, smooth, and refreshing, finishes a little dry and the flavor does linger too much leaving you craving for more!

Overall: This is the best DIPA out right now IMO

Taybeh, Aug 25, 2015
4.91/5  rDev +6.5%
look: 4.75 | smell: 5 | taste: 5 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.75

I got this from my new friend, Omensetter, who leaves in proximity to the brewery! I never thought I would get a chance at this beer and he seriously hooked me up! And I love the graphics on the label with the T-Rex I'm in love!

A - Bright honey-orange color. Incredibly opaque capturing all of the light in it's brilliantly dry hopped body with a HUGE, fine white head and crazy strong lacing.

S - Extremely strong hop fragrance with tons of tropical fruit that you would ever want all packed into one glass. Mostly a papaya, pineapple, and mango mixture that I can barely describe the awesomeness. Could smell it as I opened the bottle. Definitely one of the best smelling beers ever!

T - Amazing hop flavor with a full fruit bouquet hop character with lots of tropical fruit, citrus, some pine, and dank resin hops to round out this tremendous beer. There is some malt and ale yeast but very much like a mango, citrus hop soup. The bitterness seems quite mild for so many hops but still very good. This is clearly a Citra lovers feast and if you don't like the bite of some of the other Citra DIPAs out there this will probably be your new favorite.

M - Medium mouth-feel with medium carbonation. Sits very well on the palate. More like a regular IPA than a DIPA but it is very easy to drink the entire bomber and keep wanting more.

Overall, this beer is definitely one of the best DIPAs. I think I'm biased and my palate has already known too many amazing beers to really send this one to the very top of the list but I would definitely spend $15 a bottle again! This brew is absolutely amazing for every hop head out there that loves tropical flavored hops. This beer went down fast and I look forward to trying it again next year!

First bottled batch, bottled 1 day prior to review. (Note: I had this on tap twice previously and thought it was far better so perhaps the first bottled batch was a bit off…still great though)

Appearance: Very hazy/murky orange, yellow hue, absolutely the murkiest DIPA I’ve drank (almost makes Heady Topper seem clear). Good head and lacing.

Aroma: Great mango, tropical fruits and citrus layered over some slight oniony & vegetal aroma. Very similar to Pseudo Sue but a bit less fruit forward…a little more dank and slightly vegetal.

Taste: Very dry and bitter, possibly a little too dry and bitter. Fruity & citrusy but I get much less in the flavor than in the aroma. Flavor is good, but not near as impressive as the aroma

Mouthfeel: Pretty Good, sort of velvety/creamy….and nicely resinous…maybe a little over-carbonated.

Don’t get me wrong this is an excellent DIPA to be sure, but it’s not a world class beer like Pseudo Sue is. It’s also not quite on par with Heady or Pliney, but it’s definitely a top 20 DIPA. However, I definitely prefer the baby brother (Pseudo Sue) to this.
