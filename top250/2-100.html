Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: Kevincredible on 09-12-2014

Brewed with Monson’s own Maxwell Road Maple Grade A Dark Amber Maple syrup, Good Morning pours pitch black in the glass with a creamy mousse-like head. The bubbles give way to aromas of rich milk chocolate, cocoa powder, and dark amber maple syrup. The flavor starts as a blast of milk chocolate, sweet maple syrup, and rich fresh coffee as deeper complexities are uncovered as it warms. The crew here at Tree House tastes “chocolate covered maple candy”, “vanilla”, “chocolate cake”, “brown sugar”, and “fresh roasted coffee”. A super decadent treat, Good Morning begs to be shared and contemplated!

This was suppose to be an Imperial Double Shot, but it became something else. Something greater.

Pours a really dark amber with about half a finger head. Head is creamy with some really nice lacing.

The smell of this beer blew me away. Huge notes of Maple and Vanilla assault my nose. really great notes of fudge chocolate with coffee bitterness surprisingly making the backseat, but making their presence still known.

Wow! Maple and Vanilla sweetness getting a hold of my tongue. Once you think this beer will be completely sweet, the cocoa and coffee bitterness comes trough. Completely giving you a well balance Imperial Coffee Stout. What a dense beer, but in a good way.

Absolutely spectacular mouthfeel. Thick, creamy, rich, and chewy. Just what I love in a non BA Imperial Stout.

Overall, this became my favorite non BA Imperial Stout of all time. The real Maple syrup gave even more World Class status to this completely different version of Double Shot. Nose,Taste,Mouthfeel you name it, the Maple is king in this beer. The fact the sweetness of the Maple and Vanilla didn't overpower the beer and yet made it the most important factor, it makes this beer special.

Well done Tree House, well done.

look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.25

~8oz pour at Hop Jam in Bolton, VT--8/21/15.

Disclaimer: The refrigerated truck that this keg emerged from had the chiller cranked way high to compensate for what was predicted to be a very warm day on the mountain. I received my pour about three minutes after tapping. Consequentially, although I did my damnedest to allow it to warm properly, rather more of my pour than I would have liked was consumed at sub-optimal (literally) temperatures.

Pours a rich, viscous black, mocha highlights faintly visible when held up to the sun. In contrast to many other reviewers, a pleasant but rather tame and short-lived khaki heads sits atop my pour, dissipating after perhaps a minute.

In the nose, chocolate fudge, fresh maple syrup, Muscovado sugar, and medium roast--while the smell as a whole is more muted than I would have expected, when I am able to discern the flavors they are all distinctive and vibrant unto themselves.

Chocolate cake, toffee, cold-brewed coffee, maple syrup, and toasted brown sugar prominent also on the tongue. Although I am again somewhat surprised by the delicacy of the flavors, they are all distinct and fairly assertive in their own rights, and I am struck by how definitely fresh the maple tastes--it is definitely the smooth, slightly earthy sweetness of fresh maple syrup.

Mouthfeel is, to my palate, lovely--robust and pleasantly plump without feeling flabby. Svelte is perhaps an appropriate word here.

On the whole, delighted to have gotten to try this--now I know that, though I thoroughly enjoyed it, I need never brave the appalling lines that inevitably form around its sporadic releases. An exceptional stout, a superb beer--just not worth losing your head over.

750ml growler, filled two days ago at the brewery.

A: pours inky black, opaque with a 1 finger light tan head. Sticky lacing, looks frothy and thick in the glass.
S: huge amount of coffee, chocolate, vanilla, marshmallow and roastiness on the nose. Smells sweet but I don't get distinct maple syrup.
T: upfront lots of coffee and chocolate, slight roast. Sweet but not over done. Then you get maple syrup, wood, vanilla, pancake notes. In the mid palate there is a acidic coffee note that I found a little distracting. Get a hint of the alcohol without any alcohol burn.
M: medium to thick, the coffee and carbonation make the body very creamy.
O: tastes like a thick creamy iced coffee poured on pancakes (all in a good way) and one of the best coffee stouts I have ever had. Probably one of the best noses on a beer in recent memory for me, may have to go with how fresh the beer was. The maple syrup was a great addition and not overpowering. The acidic note, (I'm guessing from the coffee) was the only drawback for me and hard to get over.

Definitely a beer worth seeking out. Shared with a group of friends who ranged from casual beer drinkers to geeks and everyone enjoyed it.

Had this multiple times, both in growlers and at EBF. Rating the latest batch, growler filled on 8/15/2015, poured into a Tree House tulip glass.

Looks exactly how an imperial coffee stout is supposed to look. Black with about a half inch of brown head that sticks around a while, but doesn't linger.

The nose is huge with maple, dark coffee, and a hint of chocolate. I need a perfume like this.

Taste explodes with deep maple syrup up front, transitions into a deep roasted coffee, almost a Cuban coffee. Finishes with a smooth chocolate/caramel flavor. Slight bitterness from the dark coffee and roasted malt.

The feel is quite sugary, definitely due to the maple syrup, but it balances out the bitterness perfectly.

Overall, a fantastic beer. A must have for coffee and stout lovers alike.
