really and truly one of the best india pale ales ever. its murky and yeasty and pale almost beige, wallpaper looking, but super heady and fluffy and sexy, looks like I want it to, raw and fresh as they come. the nose is the best feature of this one. it smells like a bubbling cauldron of all my favorite hop strains, just more bold and fresh and alive and upbeat and forward than any other beer, even ones that use the same blend and quantity. the freshness needs to be mentioned, I got a bottle at the brewery, and it was bottled about two hours before I bought it, and les than four hours before I drank it, so you really cant get it any fresher than I did, which is just amazing. this place is a mecca, and even though you cant drink there, the beers they are turning out are among the best on the planet, I have no doubt. pineapple juice and passion fruit and pine and citrus and every half assed hop descriptor we all use all the time, times a million. very intense, and one thing about the flavor is that its also very bitter. a lot of these type ipas never resolve, they don't get bitter, and without a lot of grain flavor, its like a sentence without punctuation, and so the bitterness serves this well, even if its high. it was nicer and nicer as it warmed up, and I almost regret letting it chill as much as I did. the hop profile on this is entirely unique, not like any other beer, and its really hard to describe, almost iron rich like raw spinach or kale or something, lettuce juicy too, crunchy but light, not at all filling, and with the perfect alcohol content. I had a few from these guys so far, with a fair few to go, but this is easily the winner in the early going, one of the best india pales ive ever had.

Appearance: Pours a slightly hazy amber-tinged honey color capped by a small head that fades away rapidly though it does leave some lacing behind

Smell: Very citrusy and tropical, with orange, grapefruit and mango tones; a hint of sweet onion

Taste: Like all of Trillium's IPAs you can barely detect the malt presence as the beer is all about the interplay of the hops; grapefruit tones dominate, with the orange, mango and sweet onion tones playing a supporting role; slightly piney, in the finish

Mouthfeel: Medium body with moderate carbonation

Overall: Another great IPA from Trillium; I am not sure anybody out there is doing them as consistently good as Trillium - wish I spent more time in Boston if only to get more beers from the source

This is a great IPA, I love the hop flavor and bitterness level -I enjoy the way the hops chosen are utilized without an asringent qualities or harsh bitterness, yet retains a very intense hop flavor while continuing to be drinkable taste after taste.

A-Pours a mildly hazed orange. More chill haze than anything else. The head birthed from the pour is thin but dense and slightly frothy. It leaves sticky lacing.

S-Pretty spectacular aroma. I initially get a bunch of fruity, exotic hop-notes along with a noticeable green onion aspect. Mango, marijuana, garlic, melons. The hoppiness hits all ends of the spectrum but they seem to balance out well.

T-The specifics of the nose are all present yet they melt into one another. Quite a hop concoction. It has a very earthy flavor that I think began with that green onion/garlic portion I found in the nose, I love it. The fruity hoppiness is brimming with guava, mango, melon and papaya...yes, that's specific but these flavors are quite exotic and very concentrated. Citrus and grass as well.

M-Has plenty of body to it. Hop resins are present. Just a tickle of alcohol. Softer carbonation but that may be from the pouring median. Modest bitterness; it's clearly bitter but not excessive.

O-Ho Hum, another Trillium offering that tastes like it was consumed from the cupped hands of Zeus. Love grabbing growlers from these boys; the beer is always delicious and the employees are cool beans.

pmarlowe, Jan 03, 2015
4.57/5  rDev +2.9%
look: 4.5 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.5

Drank from a 25.4 fl oz (750 ml) bottle purchased at Trillium Brewing Company, Boston, MA
Bottled 121014
Served in a pint glass

The hazy, yellow ochre liquid was topped by a frothy, foamy, two finger tall, eggshell colored crown. Clumps of bubbly, sudsy looking lacing trickled along the glass and stuck around long after the beer was finished.

The aroma was simply amazing. Super punchy and bright. Tropical fruits dominated. Mango, papaya and guava were most noticeable. Some candied orange peel as well. There was also an intense weed-like quality to the nose. Somewhat dank and almost a touch musty, but in a good sort of way.

The flavor profile was definitely more dry and bitter than the aroma but fantastic nonetheless. Quite lemony and bright. Good dose of tropical fruits. Mango and papaya were noticeable. Citrus notes also came through strong. Orange peel was apparent. Lemon notes reappeared on the back end. Crisp and slightly tart. Dank, marijuana notes were detectible. The beer finished pithy and dry with loads of white grapefruit. Slightly bitter. As the beer warmed, the lemon notes intensified.

The mouthfeel was lighter bodied for the style and quite bubbly. Frothy and foamy on the palate. Perhaps a touch airy. The carbonation eventually settled down and the liquid turned super slick, creamy and smooth.

Trillium Brewing never disappoints with their hoppy beer offerings, and Melcher Street IPA is definitely another winner. The Morteuka hops lend a really interesting and dynamic flavor profile that is both tropical and lemony. I would love to see more breweries make use of this fantastic hop cultivar.
