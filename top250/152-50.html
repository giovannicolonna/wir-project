Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: jhartley on 12-24-2014

Up until this brew, none of Lagunitas’ barrel-aged adventures ever made it to shelves. They’ve been super-limited draft-only releases. This time we’ve made enough to actually bottle some … The 100% barrel-aged 2015 High West-ified Coffee Stout. Ale brewed with Coffee from our friends at Petaluma Coffee & Tea, then aged in Rye Whiskey barrels from our friends at High West Distillery in Park City, Utah … barrels that held High West’s Double Rye and American Prairie Bourbon, to be exact. Cheers!

Bomber served slightly chilled into a goblet.

High West-ified is a very dark brown color with good clarity seen on the pour. It has thin tan colored head that settles quickly to a skim layer.

Aroma has wonderful oak and bourbon character with vanilla, chocolate, and peat.

Flavor is intense but balanced. Milk chocolate and caramel sweetness come through at first. The vanilla and bourbon from the oak barrel come through next. And it finishes with roasted malts, bitterness, and a dryness.

Mouthfeel is full bodied and nicely carbonated for the style. It has good balance for a big bourbon stout. The alcohol is well hidden at first but begins to warm after a few sips.

Overall an excellent imperial stout and worth seeking out. I'll try to find a few more bottles before it's gone.

The beer pours a nice foamy brown head that dissipates quickly. The beer itself is an opaque black. The beer has a fairly nice texture.

The beer smell is very good. The beer has decent hop notes mixing with various barrel notes and a dark chocolate sweetness. The barrel notes mainly consist of vanilla and a little bit of the rye spices from the barrels as well as a tiny bit of oak. The notes mix very well and smells pretty balance without one note burying another.

The beer taste is very good as well. The barrel notes are much stronger than in the smell and include a slight alcohol note as well. The taste has a stronger hop note too in addition to a weaker chocolate note. Unlike the smell, the rye seems to have the advantage.

The mouthfeel is fairly smooth and prickly. The beer has a decent amount of carbonation in it. The beer is heavy bodied. Not bad but feels a little thin.

Overall, this is an objectively fantastic beer made even better by the great price. Essentially, you're getting KBS level of quality for a very cheap price.

From the bomber into an oversized tulip/snifter.

Medium to dark-khaki in color. Tons of lace scattered the nearly three to four inches that the head once sat. Still sits chewy, foamy and frothy in a film over the body. Body is the expected pitch black but shows some good viscosity. 5

Deep aromas of oak, tobacco and a tad of soy sauce. Vanilla and chocolate follow. Faint notes of bourbon or whiskey. I appreciate that restraint. 4.25

Weyerbacher Sunday Morning Stout than this. I feel like they've both captured the taste that a barrel-aged beer should have, meaning not a bourbon but a beer first. This one is pretty damn soy sauce heavy, but it's also oak-y, chocolatey, and a touch spicy with some whiskey. Vanilla does a good job of sweetening this up mid-palate too. As it warms it even develops some fruit character, offering cherries and other dark fruit while the finish tastes like after you've finished a quality cigar. The coffee flavors are there but much easier to overlook than other flavors. 4.5

The body on this is a tad under medium but still substantial enough to enjoy. The booze is felt but not offensive or overwhelming and it's got a good balance of sweet, malty, barrel and bitter. Drinks excellently. 4.5

I enjoyed Sunday Morning Stout a bit more but these are pretty neck-and-neck and both apparently more widely distributed than the more hyped beers that are a little less fun to obtain and drink. Great stuff.

look: 4.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

From a 650 ml bottle (dated 2015) to a snifter. Reviewed from notes dated May 23, 2015.

A-The beer is nearly black and opaque. My pour nets about one and one-half fingers of mocha colored head. The retention is good for the style and a beer with this much alcohol by volume.

S-The aroma is heavy on the bourbon with lots of caramel, vanilla, and good bit of sweetness to go with warming alcohol. I also sniff some dark berry fruit and chocolate, a bit of cream, and a light coffee aroma.

T-The taste is more coffee forward than the smell, but not as strong or bitter as many of the style. There is still a good bit of caramel and vanilla, as well as a raspy oaky bite from the barrel and the alcohol. Other flavors include cocoa powder, overripe fruit, and a bit of chocolate syrup sweetness.

M-The body is medium in heft with a nice, fine carbonation.

O-This is a pretty tasty beer that scores extra points for being on the cheap end of the style. This beer nicely balances coffee, barrel, and beer, so if you like your BA stouts to be all things to all people, this is a good option. If you want something more extreme, you might look elsewhere.

ArkyVaughan, Aug 10, 2015
3.76/5  rDev -14.4%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 3.25 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

Drank from a 1 pint 6 fluid oz bottle purchased at Liquor World, Cambridge, MA
2015
Served in a snifter

The beer poured a dark brown/black but rather thin for the style. A tight, frothy, one-finger tall, cappuccino colored crown topped the shimmery liquid. Drippy streaks of lacing coated the glass. The head faded to thick, bubbly, lasting ring.

The aroma was rich and robust and consisted of an equal mix of roasted malts, dark roast coffee and bourbon. Slightly sweet. Brown sugar was noticeable. Some caramel, burnt sugar and molasses came through as well. Interesting overall, but not as intense as I was hoping it would be, especially with regard to the coffee. As the beer warmed, the aroma really intensified. Powdered cocoa came forth. Very mocha-like. Loads of vanilla as well.

The flavor profile was super roasty and hot. Deep, dark, roasted malts and French roast coffee came through strong. There was a much stronger coffee presence here than in the aroma. Loads of roast and char. Touch of brown sugar. The beer finished with an intense, whiskey-like kick and lingering ashy notes. Slightly acrid. Earthy hops trailed in the aftertaste.

The body was quite thin for the style, yet the liquid was surprisingly frothy and fluffy. Pillowy soft on the palate. The beer contained a super fine, moderate effervescence.

High West-ified Imperial Coffee Stout tastes much more raw and unrefined than I typically like an imperial stout to be. Some aging might mellow the alcohol sharpness, but it will likely subdue the coffee notes as well. Fans of rye whiskey should perhaps give this a try, especially at its price point. For others, however, I would recommend passing on it.

Appearance - Pours dark, near black cola brown with a darkly tanned, silky looking finger width head. Great retention, breaking down to a thin, consistent layer of suds.

Overall - What a fantastic BA offering from Lagunitas. IMO, they killed it. Not heavy on the aggressive alcohol bite that can sometimes overrun BA offerings, which I can appreciate. 22oz bottle from Sprayberry Bottle Shop (Marietta, GA). 8.99. 2015 vintage with no discernable bottle date. Served in a Barrel and Barley teku.

Pours jet black with a full three fingers of fluffy pale tan froth. The head lingers before settling to a milky surface haze with a modest halo. Only a few meager splotches of lace remain.

A strong dose of whiskey kicks off the smell followed by roast, capucinno, cocoa and vanilla.

As in the nose, whiskey leads the flavors and remains consistent throughout. Dark cocoa, heavily kilned malts, creamed coffee, fudge, caramel, faint oaky vanilla and a fairly hefty bite of booze. It's a nice blend and despite the alcohol being noticeably strong it is never overpowering or distracting.

Rich, full, creamy feel with moderate carbonation. Whiskey heat, capucinno, chocolate and caramel linger in the long, sweet aftertaste.

Lagunitas has strayed outside of their typical box of hops with High Westified Coffee Stout. I've long been a fan of their Capuccino Stout and this takes it to a whole new level. When Lagunitas traditional pricing is factored in this becomes hands-down the highest quality, best valued barrel aged imperial stout that you can find. Outstanding work!
