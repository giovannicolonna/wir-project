Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: MichaelAllenVT on 04-03-2014

This lupulin-ladin India Pale Ale is packed with juicy tropical fruit character, bright floral aromas and delectable layers of hop flavor. Pour mindfully, inhale deeply and enjoy a tropical vacation in a glass. Always store cold, enjoy fresh and stay cool!
Sip of Sunshine IPA is brewed by Lawson’s Finest Liquids at Two Roads Brewing Co. in Stratford CT.

Had this on 2015-03-22 in Columbia, PA from a fresh, 16 oz can. Thanks Mike.

A - Mildly hazy, golden-orange with a 2.5-finger pillowy white head, a light touch of sediment and very nice lace. BTW, the can itself looks really refreshing.

S - Orange, pineapple, honeysuckle, fresh hop buds that are a little dank and very floral.

T - Fresh, juicy oranges, pineapple, grapefruit and papaya sweetness with a touch of light malt. The refreshing, outdoorsy flavors of pine and mint compliment the present-but-moderate bitterness delivered by the various hop flavors that ebb and flow on the tongue (and nose). The aftertaste continues these same flavors; and remains well balanced; slightly favoring hop bitterness.

M - Medium body and low-medium carbonation. Smooth, sticky, creamy, resinous and just lightly prickly.

O - A great DIPA; nearing the lofty heights of Double Sunshine.

n2185, Jun 26, 2015
4.89/5  rDev +7%

Pours cloudy gold color (darker gold for second pour of the bomber) with a lot of sediment floating in the brew, leading up to the 1-2 finger foamy white head that was full of bubbles and faded very slowly, leaving great thick foamy lacing coating the sides, rings as it went down and a layer on top.

The nose is bursting with citrus, and I could smell the hops from 6 inches away. Aroma is a potent combo of grapefruit, orange, pineapple, fresh juicy wet citrus hops, mango, graham cracker, malt, floral hops, grass, caramel, pine cone/piney hops, spices like pepper and faint coriander, as well as earthy notes like dried hay or dried hops, and some lemon zest at the end. A lot of tropical/citrus fruit notes and a lot of fresh hop goodness make this excellent smelling with just enough malt balance to keep it very close to perfection.

Tastes very similar to the aroma (thank goodness!) with a combo of grapefruit, orange, pineapple, fresh juicy wet citrus hops, lemon zest, mango, graham cracker, malt, floral hops, grass, caramel, pine cone/piney hops, toasted grains, pepper and other spices like tea leaf and faint coriander, either brown sugar or dry cake batter, earthy notes like dried hay, peat moss or dried hops, and a bready finish like a dry biscuit. The flavor was by no means dry, in fact it was extremely juicy and the hops were fresh and delicious with the malt backbone making this perfectly balanced and hiding the ABV completely. This brew is not sweet for long as the hops wash away any sweetness and then leave some bready/grainy after tastes swirling with the mild bitterness. The complexities are constantly changing the arrangement of the taste, which to me is a sign of great ingredients and a very well crafted beer.

Mouthfeel is buttery and slick, with a medium almost chewy body, mild/below average carbonation and a slightly bitter finish that trails off with grainy notes, floral hop notes like tea leaf, and of course some citrus hops. It’s almost a dry finish with minimal sweetness and a great aftertaste that brings out more grapefruit/orange/pineapple/lemon with each sip.

My first beer from Lawson’s and it lived up to the hype. This was a great DIPA, worth seeking out; hell, it’s worth the 10 hour drive to go up to Vermont to get some. A very high recommendation.

far333, Jan 01, 2015
4.34/5  rDev -5%

We crack a tallboy, pouring into our oversized tasting snifters. It shines the color of a ripe Georgia peach. A powdery, snowy two finger head of white sand colored bubbles adorns this liquid, standing tall and retaining well. It is also highly reproducible. Thick sheeted cheesecloth lacing is left around our glasses. Chill is definitely apparent, as are some tight dots of sediment with the dregs of the can. Carbonation appears to be mild. The aroma is bright and natural, with hoppy varietals spanning citric to densely piney, bittering and serious lemon zest, spicy pale and earthy amber maltiness, fresh tomato acidity, pineapple and pear fruity mealiness, tin, warmed Granny Smith apples, green acorns, tight juniper stickiness, mineral-laden clay water, succulents, dried spaghetti, peppery spiciness, celery salt, and fresh spring dew. The flavoring gives creamy pale and wheaty maltiness at the forefront of the sip, dried rye and soaked barley, intensely floral and citric hoppy wash, cantaloupe and bitter grapefruity pith, under ripe pear and white grapes, dusty wool, mineral and metallic simple ale yeastiness, dried willow bark and honeysuckle, organic well water, tangerine flesh, vanilla extract, fusel booze, and stale white chocolate peppermint bark. The body is medium, and the carbonation is lightly medium. Cream and froth are up there, and slurp and smack are nice as well, but leave little room for a crisp finishing pop. However, the fact that it finishes so cleanly to flavor makes us happy regardless. The mouth is cooled, coated, and creamed, with lingering bittering astringency to pull us into a mild pucker. The abv is appropriate, and the beer disappears at a nice pace.

Overall, this is an intensely serious use of hops right here. Through both the aroma and flavoring you get this wonderfully fresh, authentic, sappy, fruity, robust, and uniquely Vermont-garden quality to the hops. They seem unadulterated in their clean bitterness, with enough energy left in the end to offer a respite of sweetness to the ultimate balance. The finish is thus clean and easy to palate, all while remaining excitingly flavorful and organically bitter. This muddies subtly with warmth, so drink on the chillier side. Again, we would like to give a thankful shout out to Joe at CT Liquors for pulling this one out for us. Initially, we were kind of surprised to see it in his hand. Having had to drive up to Vermont to a random little store to even find a random bottle of their stuff in the past, we hadn’t previously been aware of their collaborative brewing partnership with Two Roads out of Connecticut. Thankfully, my upbringing in the state brought us home for the holidays, and thus, this beer to our possession, but it is always nice to be surprised in this wonderful world of craft beer.

ONovoMexicano, Jan 05, 2015
4.31/5  rDev -5.7%
look: 5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 5 | overall: 4.25

Beautiful golden pale ale; "sunshine" is a rather apt descriptor. Fairly clear with just a little haze, and topped by a beautiful snow white, light and fluffy head. Very good retention, and huge snowdrift chunks of lace.

Quite a bit in the aroma, but all balanced. The hops are there: juicy, floral, piney, slightly dank, and peppery. The malt is there: sweet and creamy.

Flavor matches. I don't get tons of specifically tropical hop flavors as promised by the description; rather, it's a blend of hop flavors, from dank to resinous to floral to tropical. Quite a noticeable malt backbone, as well, with a diacetyl-like tinge to the flavor.

Creamy yet somewhat lively mouthfeel; a really nice in-between of a slow DIPA sipper and a single IPA gulper. Lasting hop bitterness, with oily retention around an otherwise very slightly dry finish.

There's absolutely no indication this beer clocks in at 8% ABV! There's a heavy, sweet malt presence, but no alcohol presence whatsoever.

This is a tough beer to rate. Some things it does about perfectly (balance, appearance, mouthfeel). Others are very, very good (aroma, flavor) but perhaps not world-class. It's an excellent DIPA, thankfully available in cans.
