650ml bottle shared at the Malt House while waiting for Great Taste tickets.

Appearance is darker brown, nearly black body, one finger brown head with good retention time, nice lacing. Thick brown collar persists. Smells of dark coffee, milk chocolate, cocoa nibs, roasted malts, sweet bourbon. Nice smell but you don't get much of the coconut here. Flavour is outstanding; you get dark chocolate bitterness up front from the cocoa, with a nice bit of roasted coffee bean, transitioning quickly to a sweeter milk chocolate presence with a bit of sweet bourbon heat, that quickly is replaced by a strong coconut finish. The coconut finish is excellent and draws comparisons to Almond Joy, but I think of it as more of flaked coconuts on top of dark chocolate chip coffee cake. Nice complexity with the coconut being strong but not an overwhelming one note flavour like GI Proprietor's 2013. Mouthfeel is full bodied, lower in carbonation than usual, thick and slick, but the coconut lightens it all up on the backend. Very drinkable for a 13% ABV beer. Overall, this was a very good beer that integrates coconuts very nicely with the bourbon barrel and coffee treatments on a base beer that was otherwise forgettable; it suggests that the base beer is a nice canvas for barrel treatments.
