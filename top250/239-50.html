SplitsAtoms, Aug 07, 2015
4.58/5  rDev +5%
look: 5 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.75

Poured into a Duvel tulip. The liquid is a cloudy golden orange, with a thick nearly-white head that leaves a ton of lace. Exactly what I would want it to look like!

Aroma is a bomb of citrus - grapefruit, orange, tangerine, etc, with hints of mango and pineapple, and just a little bit of soap. There's some great undertones of dank pine, and a nice backbone of caramelly bread.

Flavor follows the nose, with tangerine (and soap) up front, bolstered by the other citrus flavors. The malt comes in mid-palate, adding just a tiny bit of sweetness. Holding it in my mouth gives me that great tongue-prickly feeling, like I'm sucking on a hop cone. The swallow is fairly bitter, but not as much as I would have expected given the flavors, and the aftertaste is the perfect blend of hop and malt sweetness. Only downside is that this might be the soapiest IPA I've ever tried, but the other flavors more than make up for it.

Mouthfeel is amazingly creamy, with a moderate amount of soft, mildly prickly carbonation. Medium to light-bodied.

Overall, a really incredible IPA! Great flavors, perfect balance, and just the right mix of everything. Between this and Transmigration of Souls, it seems like GA is finally coming around to brewing some truly great IPAs! Can't wait to see what's next from this brewery.

anteatr23, Sep 16, 2015
4.74/5  rDev +8.7%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 5 | overall: 5

Can packed on date 5-26-15

L: Hazy golden orange, small head, nice lacing.
S: Outstanding--subtle but very pleasant and unique mix of tropical fruit, pine, and something vaguely vegetal/herbal.
T: Follows nose; has superb, lengthy and slightly bitter finish.
F: Medium bodied, goes down smoothly, progresses beautifully from wet to dry.

Tropicalia is by far the hottest beer in GA now; its popularity is soaring, but supplies are limited and the scarcity is adding to its mystique. People are following the beer trucks around to be at the store when it's delivered.

CC really hit a grand slam with this one. It's more subtly than explosively flavorful, and it will appeal to both less and more adventurous palates, to fans of both balanced and aggressive beers. It is drinkable enough for a summer quaffer, but complex enough for all occasions. It manages to be simultaneously juicy and crisp/dry. Flavor-wise it hits a sweet spot right at the intersection of several ipa styles without feeling derivative of any in particular. With this kind of wide appeal, no wonder it's so popular. A tremendous achievement and a must try for all beer lovers. It will fly off the shelves if CC ever decides to produce it for wider distribution.

scottyboy1980, Jul 09, 2015
4.29/5  rDev -1.6%

Appearance – 90: Canned 11/19/2014, poured into a tulip glass. Color is a golden orange, almost amber. 1/4 inch of foam, with decent lacing and head retention.

Aroma – 99: Intense tropical fruit upfront, with immediate notes of grapefruit, then mango, navel orange and pineapple, complemented by some floral notes that are slightly more subtle.

Body and Texture – 90: Light mouthfeel and extremely drinkable. Very strong hop presence without being overly heavy or bitter. Medium body.

Taste 91: Not as intense as the smell, but still deliciously juicy and tropical. Very crisp from the carbonation, with a slightly sweet and dry, bitter aftertaste. Very well-balanced.

Overall – 93 Excellent: I initially stumbled upon this ale while making a stop at the Brickstore Pub in Decatur, GA. I was reluctant to try another IPA from a Georgia brewery, but decided to give it the old college try. After two pints, I still wanted more. This is by far the best IPA I have enjoyed from Georgia. It’s an absolutely terrific IPA, which rivals the likes of other east coast IPAs, including Jai Alai (Cigar City Brewing) and Another One (Maine Brewing Company).

Purchase Value – 88: Bought this as a 6 pack for $10.99. A little bit on the expensive side, but worth the buy. Currently, this ale is only available at local stores in Athens, GA, so unless you live close by it might be quite the hike. Through Twitter, I was able to confirm that the brewery will start distributing to other parts of Georgia, including Atlanta, starting in March of 2015. The wait begins!
