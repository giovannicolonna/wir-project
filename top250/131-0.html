STYLE American IPA | ABV 8.0% | AVAILABILITY Year-round

Juicy, Dank, Chewy, Citric

Yes, there is such a thing as an East Coast IPA ... it’s simply a beer from an East Coast brewery that aims to take over your palate with hops. Hoppy beer knows no borders. Foundation throws down oats, Pale malt, Pilsner malt, and a quintet of hops here, and from all of that this IPA is elevated to hyped-about status. Meaning this is an Epiphany you should be seeking out.

Review from BeerAdvocate magazine issue #103 (AUG 2015)

MakeMineADoubleJack, Sep 05, 2015
4.58/5  rDev +1.8%
look: 4.25 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

Drank from a 1 pint can purchased at Foundation Brewing Company, Portland, ME
Served in a pint glass

The slightly hazy, golden amber liquid was topped by a tight, creamy, one-finger tall, off white crown. The head quickly receded to a lasting, frothy cap. Streaky lacing stuck along the glass.

The aroma was outstanding. Amazingly hoppy and fruity. Super fragrant and bright. I could literally smell the beer from a few feet away. Tropical fruits dominated. Loads of mango, papaya, guava and passionfruit. Citrus notes also came through super strong. Orange peel was most noticeable. There was a nice fruity sweetness as well.

The flavor profile was also amazing, though not quite as complex as the aroma. There was a subtle, sweet maltiness on the front end. Just enough for balance but not so much as to distract from the hoppy goodness. Orange notes dominated. The citrus presence was definitely much stronger here than in the aroma. Orange peel was most noticeable. Somewhat candy-like and really juicy. Tangerine and clementine notes also came through big time. Some mango and dried apricot as well. Orange peel appeared again in the finish. Just a touch of bitterness. Grapefruit rind was also apparent. As the beer warmed, the finish turned slightly more dry and bitter. All in all, amazingly flavorful, smooth and drinkable.

The beer was creamy, frothy, slick and smooth on the palate. Medium bodied for the style. The liquid contained a fine, moderate effervescence.

Foundation Brewing's Epiphany IPA is a fantastic example of what this brewery refers to as a "Maine IPA." This beer has the intensity of a double IPA yet drinks like something half its strength. The longer I sipped on this, the more striking I found the similarities between it and Heady Topper. If you are into juicy, fruity, citrusy and tropical fruit-forward IPAs, you will definitely love this beer.

Assertive but not aggressive hop spiciness with some juicy citrus. Slightly dank. Warming ups the dank and sweet elements. Oily and resinous. Deep pine.

Taste: Spicy and floral right up front. Followed by a ton of grapefruit. Juicy citrus. Zest and a good punch of bitter pith. Dank, incredibly dank. Deep and resinous pine. Oily. Tangerine, nectarine, mango, peach, and some other generic tropical fruits. Solidly floral as well. A big aggressive hop bite. Lingering bitterness. Malts are ever so slightly sweet. Pilsner and pale malt make their appearance. Even a touch of oat. Decidedly spicy hop character. A touch of alcohol runs through the back of each sip. A hint of aspirin. Herbal in a sweet and floral way. A touch of green wood and sap. Soft hint of cracker as well. Toasted edge.

Mouthfeel: Dry, very dry and crisp. Moderate carbonation and a mediumish body.

Overall: Ding!

It's another New England IPA with a focus on Cascade instead of New Zealand. Loaded with oily pine and grapefruit. Almost perfect if a bit too dry.
