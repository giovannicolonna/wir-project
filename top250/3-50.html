Had on tap Friday evening , day before the K-Brunch release, which, by the way, was one of the best run beer releases I have ever been to, and I've been to most of the "biggies".
Awesome beer-goddess Chelsea served this top-notch nectar in the 11 oz. TG chalice, for an amazing $8. Give the beer a quick swirl and you get a great coffee aroma that I haven't had the pleasure of inhaling off a beer ever. Fantastic nose appeal, really hard to stop inhaling the incredible aroma of this beer. Top notch.

The appearance has to wait until you get a few sips in, because at Toppling Goliath, your cup over floweth ! Your glass is REALLY filled to the rim, every time ! But once you get down a bit this baby really coats the glass, just the way you want, and it is every bit as black as the other big coffee stouts.

Your mouthfeel will be as expected once you sip it, and smell it, you know it will be thick and very smooth, velvety, as it most certainly is.

When you take your first taste , you almost immediately want to scream out " Oh My God " but you resist until you have given it a good tour around your mouth and palate, so as to take in this incredible explosion of taste, then you can let out your high praise of the great flavor of a not-barrel-aged beer. This is the undoubtably going to continue to be a top want of mine forever, if you haven't or can't get to taste it, just keep trying, these guys will be expanding and continuing to make more of this, keep it on your wants - totally way worth it.

Overall - I think you get my point. My top 4 beers ever tasted, in no order, Rare , Grey Monday, Mornin' Delight, Bourbon Barrel Dark Lord.

Served on draught into a MD Snifter on release day. Consumed over 2.5 hours to allow the beer to warm to ambient temperature.

A: Midnight black with a 1 finger mocha head. The beer is completely opaque.

S. Initial smell is huge freshly roasted coffee with maple lingering behind. It's been overdone: but this smells like pancakes on a Sunday Morning.

T: Immediate coffee up front, completely dominated the initial taste. Eventually gives way to a sweet maple flavor that is anything but artifical. I feel that allowing the coffee to mellow for a few months will do this beer wonders.

M. Thick as can be. Coats the glass with beautiful alcohol legs the same way it covers the mouth. Impossibly big mouthfeel.

O: this is great. Having had b1 aged, but not fresh, I urge you to hold onto the bottle for a month or so to let the coffee not be so overwhelming (not that it's a terrible thing)

dc55110, May 06, 2013
4.91/5  rDev +3.2%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 5 | feel: 5 | overall: 5

I've never reviewed a beer on BA before, and this seems as good a time as any to start! Received this bronze-waxed beauty last week, and it's been sitting in our fridge and enticing me every since! I will preface this by saying that I love Toppling Goliath and everything they've done so far, and I believe they are one of two or three breweries to watch in the Midwest over the next couple years. So, here goes:

Poured from a 12 ounce bottle into a Bruery tulip glass. Very oily looking pour that slowly slides into my glass.

A: First off, the bottle is really baller looking. Cool label, awesome wax, it's already grabbed my attention. When I pour, it looks like most stouts, very opaque, dark chocolate color, a very small amount of khaki-colored head present, but it mostly dissipates.

S: I knew going into this that the beer had a big maple influence, and the label says it's a coffee stout. I took a few big whiffs of the glass before I consumed any of the nectar inside. And I smelled some maple (that increased as the beer warmed), more coffee, burnt grains, a little chocolate. Maple and coffee dominated the smell for me. Other people have said that it smells like a pancake house, and I most definitely concur.

T: I took a sip, then a couple more. WOW. I had high expectations for this beer, but I wasn't prepared for how great it would actually be. Waves and waves of fresh, delicious maple syrup hit my tongue. So much maple syrup, that it tastes like someone squeezed a fresh pancake out over my glass! And it's the best kind of maple syrup too, not that sugary junk you'd use at IHOP. I can only imagine it compares to CBS at its best. Beyond that delicious flavor, coffee and chocolate sneak in for a subtle balancing act. There is so much flavor in this beer, but nothing is overpowering and nothing tastes fake, which is an extraordinary feat. It's certainly a sweeter stout, but I would not call it sugary at all. As it warms, more and more maple comes out, and I shake my head in wonder. Now I see why they can only make such limited batches of this! This is the best-tasting non BA stout I've ever had, and potentially the best-tasting stout I've had, period.

M: Toppling Goliath knocks it out of the park with mouthfeel and stouts. This one has the characteristic creamy silkiness that I remembered from trying SR-71, and it really adds to the drinking experience. I feel like a lot of stouts focus so much on flavor that they lose something in the mouthfeel category, and you end up with a thin-bodied beer. Not so here -- it's everything I'd want in the stout category.

O: This beer has an insane amount of flavor, especially considering it was not barrel-aged. It is the perfect breakfast beer (or anytime beer, let's be honest), and the alcohol is well-hidden. It is also incredibly filling, which might be a small knock against it in the drinkability category. Even with help, it was hard for me to finish the bottle. I've had FBS and KBS, as well as many other coffee stouts, and this is a step above them all. I have a feeling this will be one of the most memorable beers I have all year, and I'm certainly glad I was able to try it. As a postscript, TG does not encourage you to cellar their beers. I'm happy that I took their advice. The maple really took center stage, and I think that would only fade over time (much like CBS). Fresh is best! Well done TG!

'13 Mornin' Delight from the bottle still drinking great two years out.
