RyanJohnCrain, Aug 16, 2015
4.94/5  rDev +3.8%
look: 5 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 5 | feel: 5 | overall: 5

Had it on tap at the TG release on Saturday 8/29/15.

The look is just what you'd expect from a TG stout, amazing. Just looking at it you can tell the mouth feel is on point. The head is minimal and presents a deep tan color. The way this beer coats the glass is like no other beer I've seen. The entire glass is coated in sweet maple stout by the time you're through.

The smell is very coffee forward but you begin to pick up the maple as it warms. In my opinion this is the only area where this beer slightly lacked. We had Good Morning the Friday before and the nose on that beer had a lot more maple. But once MD warms up a bit, it honestly smelled like pancakes...

The taste...heavenly. You get a bunch of coffee upfront and subtle maple notes towards the end. Once you let this bad boy warm up for a bit, it completely changes the complexity of the beer. The maple becomes much more pronounced. They say to drink fresh, I'm not saying to age it for two years...but I feel six months to a year will only add to the character of this stout.

The feel of the beer is hands down one of the best I've had. It's super thick and viscious, leaves almost a resin type feel in the back of your throat. This is where MD blows GM out the water. Again we had GM the evening prior, and everyone agreed it was a great tasting beer. But after having MD, it was a unamious vote for Toppling Goliath.

Overall, this was the best non-BA stout I've ever had the pleasure of trying. Frankly I never write reviews, if I do, they're typically a few sentences long. I'm not one to over-hype a particular beer, but I'd definitely put this in my top 5...maybe top 3 best beers I've ever had. Maybe I'm a bit biased because I'm a big fan of maple coffee stouts. But you will not be disappointed. I had every intention on trading one of these bad boys prior to the release...after having it, I don't think I could trade for another beer (despite KBBS) that would be on the same level as this.

TG keep doing what you're doing. Thanks for giving me the opportunity to try this amazing beer.

10 Oz pour at TG yesterday into the MD chalice. Thanks again to the Hop Partol and Clark for running a fantastic release. Here's the notes.

L: Motor-oil black. Coats the inside of the glass which I LOVE seeing in stouts. Dark brown head that sticks to the edges mostly.

S: Coffee. Straight up roast and some chocolate I think. It's hard for me to tell because the coffee is quite strong. You can tell it's going to be sweet though.

T: Strong roasty coffee. Hits like a truck and then fades as it warms in your mouth. This is when the maple comes through. Sweet. Very well balanced. There is a slight booziness here as well that's quite warming.

F: Thick. Perfectly carbonated and smooth.

Overall the taste reminds me a lot of my grandfather's maple syrup. While definitely a sipper it's hard to believe this is 12%. This is everything a coffee stout should be and more. Definitely stands up to the hype. Mike, you made one helluva beer!

Let me preface, I don't give many perfect scores out, but this is just ridiculous stuff.

On Tap at Toppling Goliath Mornin' Delight Day, multiple pours.

Look: Oily, viscous, borderline sludgy. Head foams up with a mocha-dark khaki head. Lacing and legs for days.

Smell: Maple. Maple. Maple, coffee, waves of chocolate. This is a beer I could smell for days.

Taste: Sweet sticky maple doused chocolate covered coffee beans. Pure decadence. Absolutely mind blowing. There's even a buttery note (not diacetyl) that comes through almost like creme-brulee.

Feel: Viscous, creamy, thick. Words don't describe this mouthfeel. 2nd only to SR-71 for Stout mouthfeel IMHO.

Overall: Many folks have said this isn't as good as 2013, they're wrong, it's better. More maple, more coffee, just completely delicious. Worth every penny. Would I give up a 12 bottle shipper for one? Doubtful. No beer is worth that.

STRAIGHT #HUNNITS/100.

On tap at Dexter's in Madison and at Toppling Goliath at release.

Minimal tan head which dissipates quickly. THICK, and sticks to the sides of the glass for an eternity.

The smell. My god, the smell. Absolute perfection. Rich, sweet and chocolate, with a pronounced coffee note.

Taste - A perfect blend of sweet coffee goodness, with vanilla. Given the high ABV, it was surprising that there was virtually no heat to the beer. Coffee flavor is much softer than one would expect from the nose, and continues to become less prevalent as it warms.

Mouthfeel is SMOOTH and THICK. Take your pick of a viscous liquid to compare it too -- motor oil is probably the closest, however. Perfectly chewy, it clings to your hard palate in a wonderful way, letting you hang on to the flavors a few seconds more.

After having Good Morning the night prior to my second time with MD, it is clear that MD is in a category all its own with this one. The only beer I've had that tops this is was 2013 KBBS, and that was an exceptionally close call.
