etmur007, Jan 07, 2015
4.74/5  rDev +5.3%
look: 4.75 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Poured from a can filled on 9/9 into a tulip:

Appearance: vibrant orange with a frothy, bubblicious head. Big off-white bubbles and tons of little bubbles rising up the sides of the glass. Somewhat hazy, but clear enough to be visible through the glass.

Aroma: INSANE amount of hop aromatics. Tons of fruity bitterness, like scraping the inside of a grapefruit rind. Some freshly cut grass, but mostly fruity dankness. Incredible smell; not understated at all.

Taste: in line with the smell, with layer upon layer of fruit juice and just a twinge of bitterness. I get little to no malt, which for a DIPA advertised as being a straight up hop bomb, is what I expected. Grapefruit, orange, pineapple......they're all present and tasting delicious.

Feel: Medium-soft body with not a whole lot of carbonation present. Lots of lingering stickiness from the whopping five-hop recipe.

Overall: probably the juiciest beer I've had next to Trillium's Headroom, and probably juicier. I get that this one is out of the box in it's lack of malt and balance, but if you are looking to drink a hop banger of an IPA, it is perfect.

kammij, Jul 11, 2015
5/5  rDev +11.1%
look: 5 | smell: 5 | taste: 5 | feel: 5 | overall: 5

I was fortunate that a coworker went to Maine and was generous enough to get me a couple of cans. I was told they are $4 per 16oz can.

Look: I did not pour overly vigorously, so a single finger thick off-white head was the result. It settled to a consistant, thin foam along the surface of the beer. A bit of lacing stuck on the glass as it settled out. The color of the beer itself is a nice golden color that you'd expect with an IPA. The beer is not entirely clear, but there are no floaters of any sort (not that I would deduct for sediment).

Smell: Oooo boy, that's nice! A lively aroma greets your nostrils. I would say this smells like Heady Topper. I'm not smart enough to pick out the individual smells, but the aroma is abundant.

Taste: The bite of the carbonation hits the tip of the tongue. You might confuse it for bitterness, but for me, that comes in at the end as you're swallowing. Again, not smart enough to pick out individual flavors, but I would say you get some of that dankness that you'll have with Enjoy By, and it is very juicy and sweet. With a FG of 1.016, you'd expect it to be somewhat sweet with a meaty malt backbone to carry all of these hops. As you swallow and then breathe out through your nose, you'll get an aftershock of that delicious aroma that your brain will translate into taste. The hops and bitterness completely mask any booziness for me.

Feel: As I mentioned above, the carbonation bites the tip of your tongue. I would say it is appropriately carbonated. With the strong malt backbone, this beer is ...I don't want to say it is syrupy, but...it's getting there. I think the hop bitterness makes it appear thicker in your mouth than it actually is. Completely appropriate for this *almost double* IPA.

Overall: This is delicious. I don't know what the distribution is, but if they were able to get their name out there a bit more, I think it would shoot to the top of everyone's Want List. If you get the opportunity, try this. If you have to execute a somewhat lop-sided trade to get it, do it.

macleodad, Sep 11, 2015
4.69/5  rDev +4.2%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.75

I'm giving this beer the score that I am because it is easily among the two or three most intensely potent hoppy beers I've ever had. Think Lawson's sip of sunshine level aromatics, but with nectary mosaic and spicy Ella bursting out rather than straight citra. For me, it's actually a little too intense. The level of spice and the mosaic character make you want to suck on your tongue after you've swallowed it, which I love, but it is bone dry at 1.009 and for me, I could use a bit more body to back up the incredible hop profile. I'm Giving it the high score that I am bc it really is a stand out beverage and it deserves attention, but personally I wish they'd take the hopping schedule they've come up with and make something with a bit more character in the yeast/malt department. Great beer though if you like to get smacked in the face with peaches and mangoes. I've had it from cans that were a couple days old as well as on draft and I found the draft to be a bit more balanced and drinkable (this was also an earlier batch). Either way though, great beer and worth hunting for. More in the stone dryness/clarity department than in the tree house/trillium/HF cloudy, juicy New England trend. I'd love to put it next to Dinner as they both finish very dry and I feel like they have similar hop profiles.
