WTKeene, Mar 07, 2015
4.66/5  rDev +6.9%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Pours like a big stout should, thick and black with a small, 1 finger head. Good lacing and head retention overall. The smell is incredibly unique for a stout. Big, semi-acidic notes from the apple brandy up front with good fruit forward flavors. The booze of the barrel comes in next and in fairly strong on the nose. The fruitiness moves from initial apples to dark fruit in the middle of the scent and then the roasts of the stout kick in. At the back end the apples ramp back up to mix with the chocolate and dark roasts of the stout. Leaves you thinking fresh picked apple.

The taste of this is mellow and not as segmented as the scent was. Smooth dark flavors with just a touch of the apple hit the tongue right up front. The dark roasts and barrel mix quickly after the initial apple flavor and kind of give a caramel-vanilla-apple flavor impression. At the end of the drink the green apple flavor returns and lingers on the palate with the dark and burnt roasts intermingling a little. This beer is rather heavy on the tongue, like it should be, and smooth. The alcohol is only mildly detectable, and mostly in the warmth of the drink.

Overall, this is a superb beer. The apple brandy barrel adds a dimension that the Noir base needed. Compared to the red wine and vanilla versions of this beer, the apple brandy is a winner by a mile.

Feel- As with some of prairie stouts this is on the lighter side of full bodied, very low in carbonation, and pretty clean in the finish for a stout.

Overall- The brandy barrel flavor on this beer is really big in the nose and taste. The finish and body definitely leave something to be had though, so i can't quite put it up with the best barrel aged stouts I've had. But I really wish I had gotten more than one of these as it's definitely one of prairie best stouts and it needs to be made again. Highly recommend this, and it's well worth the $10 price tag.

I was lucky enough to score a few bottles of PAA Apple Brandy Noir in my little home town of Ponca City, Oklahoma thanks to Sharon, owner of Plaza Wine and Spirits.

Visually, ABN is similar to the other variants of the Noir line - deep black/brown capped with a single finger of mocha colored foam that simmers down to a ring at the edge of the tulip glass. Lace is very drawn out with large gaping holes down to the last drop.

The aroma - wow! Sweet apple brandy heaven! Like a fresh dipped hot caramel apple with a ton of buttery vanilla and sweet booze backed by tinges of light chocolate. An easy 5.

The flavors of the brandy are more predominant when chilled while the chocolate and vanilla are more striking after warming with alcohol coming up to strength. The apple remains from start to finish. Very nice.

Silky smooth decadent feel with full body as expected.

Overall, this is great. Maybe the best Noir yet. Can't wait to see what it's like at the 6 and 12 month marks. Cheers!
