Since Born Yesterday is bottled and shipped the very same day—probably the freshest Fresh Hop ale you'll get your paws on—the arrival date is kinda in the air 'til things get closer and nature says so. Kinda like a fresh lil' newborn, only this puppy is made from whole-cone, un-kilned Mosaic, Equinox, Citra, Simcoe and Amarillo hops from our farmer friends in Yakima, WA. It's borning soon. Wanna be the first to know when? You've gotta guess first …

As soon as I heard of this I thought, "They're making a move on their neighbors 20-minutes up the freeway..." Tried some last week at my favorite local brew-pub Tustin Brewing Company, from a freshly tapped keg. Sure enough, first thing that hit my nose was the summer fruit, floral bouquet of their famous neighbor. This tastes very different from Lagunitas' other pale ales. The malt is way toned down compared to the familiar Lagunitas profile. I drank 3 pints and enjoyed them all. I passed up a freshly tapped (as I sat there) keg of Pliny to continue on with this fine brew.

Tonight I'm setting down with a bottle but I'm not getting quite the same experience. There are hints of greatness here, but there is some room for refinement and fine-tuning. I'm getting a slightly sulfurous flavor here and that's not my favorite hoppy ale attribute. Still, I rate this very high and I hope the folks at Lagunitas continue in this direction. I'm a big Lag-Dog fan and I think their success is well-deserved. I look forward to variations/refinements of this recipe. This one is pretty Doggone Good already!

I'm a little puzzled by the BA score of 99 world-class rating; I don't think this belongs in the same category as Pliny or Heady. Yet.

unitypunk, Oct 27, 2014
4.75/5  rDev +6%

I have to admit that when I heard about this impending release, I thought it would be more gimmick than substance, but boy was I mistaken! Now first things first: this is not a well balanced beer. But who cares!? it's all about the hops, and how glorious they are in this beer. It's almost like drinking a fresh hop soda.
I crack the bottle and immediately there's the floral fruity aroma blasting my nose. I pour it into a glass and those aromas only grow more intense. It is a beautiful dark golden with a tinge of orange. First sip: now here's the thing - it's not insanely hoppy, or really all that bitter for that matter. But those fresh hop flavors really shine through - Apricot, Peach, Mango, even some pleasant citrus tartness. Almost none of the typical herbal piney characteristics that I love so much. But that's ok. That's what makes this beer unique. And perhaps is why they call it a pale rather than an IPA. And try as I may, I don't detect a hint of malt. Clearly though, that is what is buttressing all these hops. It's just that I can't taste them over all these awesome hop flavors.
This is when I'm really pleased to be living where I do, right down the road from Lagunitas.
And I'm really hoping that this is a trend (much like Stone's "Enjoy By") that continues, and showcases the fresh characteristics of lots of different hop varietals.

Lagunitas- Born yesterday. Tasted on draft 10/28 (24 hrs after release)

Appearance (9.5/10): Clear, light straw colored. Thin silky head with light lacing in glass.

Aroma (10/10): explosion of tropical fruit and citrus. Undifferentiated at first but very strong and pleasant aroma.

Mouthfeel (9/10): very light body. Slightly less carbonation than anticipated, but later I believe it helps accentuate the bold hop flavor. Lingering bitterness that coats the tongue and demands another sip. Medium resin bitterness that is not overly drying. Very mild note of warmth on tongue and back of palate but never overwhelming.

Taste (10/10): strong grapefruit with intense tropical fruit (guava, pineapple). As it warms, slight notes of grass and hay. Very little sweetness.

Overall: (38.5/40) fantastic. Explosion of fresh hop flavor that consumes the senses. Light body and crispness entice me to keep the beer in mouth as long as possible before swallowing. I feel that I could sit and drink this all day, although at 7.5%, I'd have a bit of trouble walking out of the bar and getting home. Thank god they are releasing six packs! 9.6/10!
