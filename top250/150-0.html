Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: American_Hawk on 11-13-2014

First brewed in celebration of the first anniversary of Row 34, our favorite Fort Point oyster bar and craft beer purveyor, Vicinity highlights a neighborhood of passion and vision. Plenty of spicy, earthy, tropical fruit aromas followed by complex flavors of grape juice, light citrus and lychee on the palate. The pilsner malt provides a crisp, clean, minerally finish. Soft mouthfeel, a restrained bitterness on the finish.

Bottle. Pours a hazy, pale orange color. Glowing. Head is a ripple froth congregation. Not much lace. Fruit juice hop liquid. Very reminiscent to Trillium's Mettle and Heavy Mettle. Pineapple, citrus, some bubblegum, peach and mango hops. Blasphemous amounts of aromatic hops. The flavor is such a concentrated influx of hoppiness that all flavors border on excess. That's a compliment in my book. I expected a brine of pure hop and I got it. All those fruity descriptors can be found here and in plentiful quantities. Rich, dank and superbly fruity. Malt? Provided a surprising balance that leads to a finish that is spookily mellow. Bitterness is low and I actually am jiving with it. The body is also stunningly light. This brew is another hop cauldron from Trillium. They have a very specific flavor and it reminds me a lot of some of their previous offerings. I have also absolutely loved those previous offerings. Did they do it again? Yes, comrade, yes.

Thanks Kevin for sharing a bottle of this last night. Notes taken at the time.

A- Poured into a wine glass with a murky, turbid appearance; almost milky look to the golden body. The cap raises to over a finger with good retention dropping webs of lacing as it dies and I sip.

S- A massive blast of fresh hops; reaches into the higher tiers of pungency for sure. Juicy brightness with notes of ripe mango, tangerine, oranges, pineapple, dankness and citrus zest. A bit of yeast ester and bread but the hops are strong with this one.

T- Pretty much summed up in the nose with more pale grains and a hint of sweetness. Oranges, passionfruit, mangoes, apricot, tangerines, floral and zesty citrus notes. Hop nectar.

MF- Right around medium bodied with a consistency that is creamy and foamy but not chewy; really smooth. A touch of warmth and moderately bitter finish.

Very thankful to my brother-in-law for sharing some Boston-only brews lately with me. Trillium has been up there on my list for quite some time and all of their brews have delivered.

Bottled 1/28/15, two weeks old or so.

A: Pours a very cloudy light orange. Tall, frothy offwhite head. Head retention is top notch and there are heavy walls of lacing as it slowly recedes.

S: The nose on this is fantastic. Pungent, sharp grapefruit and orange peel. Hints of tropical fruit like pineapple primarily. A hint of some dank, resinous underbelly. Wonderful.

T: More of the same. Loads of wonderful sweet and bitter citrus. Orange and grapefruit and pineapple all stand out once again. Sharp, biting flavors. A really bitter piney resin is in there too, mainly towards the finish. No real sign of malt, maybe some breadiness under all the hops but it's barely noticeable. Get hints of garlic/onion in the aftertaste, I'm guessing there's plenty of citra in here.

M: Medium bodied, fairly smooth with a big hop bitterness punch. Lightly dry in the aftertaste.

O: Pretty serious DIPA. Fantastic if you like the fruitier hop varieties. Lots of hop complexity, lots of pungent flavors and aromas but the bitterness is contained well enough. Not teeth-chatteringly bitter but still plenty strong. Nice.
