Pours black into my stout glass, with a rich body that appears luscious despite the faded tan head. Nice lacing.
Smells terrific despite alcohol being present on the nose. The milk chocolate licorice note is spot-on for me. Hints of apple are buried within the burn. Can't wait to sip this.
Taste is like the smell, but not as sweet as I'd expect. This really kicks so hard though is what strikes me most, in a pretty good way, but almost as if this was beyond 16% abv. and not the 12% it states to be. It's an acidic/ anise apple-barrel-bite that is inviting, although I'd more prefer if the profile was more mellowed-out. But for what this is, it's quite a good sipper. It is what it is.
Feels medium thick and medium-low carbonated, whiskey-like burn. Balanced, nice feel.
This is a big beer, a terrific stout considering the apple, which is done with barrel-burning precision as opposed to being a cloy-bomb.

BrewUSA, May 23, 2015
4.78/5  rDev +9.6%
look: 4.75 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.75

Poured too cold. Let warm and open at room temp.

A. Black with a small fine-bubble amber head that retreats to small rim. The fine carb leaves small spotty laces. Moderate viscosity.

S. The nose gets much more vibrant as it warms and the first notes that become apparent are cider, apples, raisins, and brandy. Next come raisin bread, cinnamon, brown sugar, and a hint of coco.

T. The taste is different than the nose and somehow an improvement. Very complex. Starts similar to a charred smoore. Then come waves of bread pudding, raisins, caramel, and coffee with 1/2 and 1/2. At the end comes a hoppy bitterness that balances the sweet notes to create a mildly bitter beer with great flavors.

M. Very smooth, silky, thick, medium carbonation, and a very full body. I throw booziness into feel, because it is not a taste, but more of a feel that sits in my cheeks after I take a sip.

O. Damn they did it again. Coming off the heels of the Pirate BOMB! they bring a barrel-aged stout with a different base, but with all the complexities and dynamic flavors of it's predecessor. While the flavors are certainly different than BOMB!, they very much work. Not knowing much about this before I had it, I was overly impressed and I fully stand behind my high rating.

March 15, 2015

12 oz bottle, snifter

A – Jet black, but pours fairly quick, without much thickness or viscosity. A half inch of brown, deep chocolate, head, somewhat bubbly with low retention.

S – Caramel, apples, chocolate, and vanilla. The apple is of the candy variety (though not SOUR), and the caramel is sweet but very rich. Malt is black but not roasty, just contributing dark or bittersweet chocolate aroma with a slight milkiness. Vanillin from the barrel and some alcohol fruitiness are evident.

T – Vanilla and semi-sweet chocolate lead here, with some smooth, rich caramel following. Apple flavor is very mild here, contributing a touch of fruitiness along with what you’re getting from the alcohol. Some high bitterness in the finish and aftertaste, not particularly pleasant.

M – Low carbonation and medium-bodied. A lot of sweetness up front with bitterness high on the end, and a bit of astringency. Moderate alcohol warmth.

O – Fantastic aroma profile and some nice flavors up front but the bitterness is somewhat unpleasant and mouthfeel does not measure up. Good, but not great.

Bottled 01.20.15 and enjoyed from a snifter:

A: Slightly translucent black with a thin, rust-colored head

S: Fantastic aromas of vanilla, Hershey's chocolate syrup, brandy, and a blend of mildly tart apples and cherries. Loved the muted wafts of brandy, which come across almost like a creme brûlée-ish sugary vanilla.

T: Delicious. A lot like those chocolate candies with a booze-soaked cherry inside. Lots of cocoa, vanilla, cherries, and brandy. Nice, mild roasted cocoa or coffee beans on the finish. Not getting much in the way of apple; the main flavor is milky, brandy-soaked cocoa.

M: The mouthfeel isn't "thin," but I think a bit more heft would send this beer to the next level. As is, however, the mouthfeel is light, slightly syrupy, and a great conveyor belt for the flavors.

O: A delicious beer that I'd be glad to drink on a regular basis. That said, I think this beer would benefit from a slightly more substantial mouthfeel and a more pronounced apple-brandy flavor. The brandy really shines on the nose and in the taste, but I was left wondering where the apple in the apple-brandy barrel was.
