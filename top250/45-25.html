Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: mothman on 03-11-2015

A different beer from previous draft iterations of SR-71. This beer has been confirmed to be the equivalent of Assassin (Barrel Aged Norseman’s Wrath) with more time in the barrels. The fill date mirrors the 2014 Assassin release that was aged in Pappy Van Winkle barrels and is most likely drawn from the exact same batch of base beer.

This is an incredible beer and it came out as stealth as many other whale releases. I feel like this one went under the radar a bit. From what I have conjured.. this an extended BA Assassin. The aroma of this beer is intoxicatingly good. huge wafts of sweet and dark roasted chocolate. The vanilla and bourbon notes help add a little tight fruit quality to the sweetness. The appearance is gorgeous. Motor oil black with fantastic lacing. The taste is perhaps one, if not the best damn Imperial I have ever had. This beer is so big and complex that it is walking the wire of just blowing up into a mess. However, Toppling Goliath once again proved their mastery. This beer is brownie and fudge with roasted qualities that are balanced by the vanilla. It is a massive chewy beer, but in a good way. Share this and sip it slowly with more than yourself, or even two people. Just an amazing beer.

Look: pours like thick black oil into a snifter, minimal head. Almost non existent

Smell: bourbon, bourbon and more bourbon. Did I say bourbon? Slight hints of chocolate and sweet coffee as it warms, but mostly overpowered by the whisky smell. My wife asked if I spilt bourbon on the ground from the other room it was so powerful.

Taste: pretty straight forward but perfect. A lot of fine whisky/bourbon flavor, mixed with fudgy chocolate and caramelized sugar/vanilla touch later on. As perfect as a stout can get to me.

Feel: very full bodied and thick, by far the heaviest stout I have ever drank. This will definitely be a long sipper for most people. Finishes sweet and dry.

Overall: this is one heck of a big beer. Smooth, bourbon forward, and wonderful. Any fan of big stouts or a lot of barrel aging will love this stout.
