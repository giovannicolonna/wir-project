12 oz draft, served in a small goblet.
Inky black with a thin layer of mocha foam that had a certain creamy texture to it. Minimal spotty lacing around the edges, retention was average. Foam faded after the first couple sips.

Aroma was fairly reserved, fudgy chocolate and light vanilla marshmallow, not much whiskey heat.

Taste was rich and big on the dark chocolate and marshmallow notes, sticky though not overly sweet. Nice drying finish with the rye whiskey coming through in the aftertaste, a lingering peppery, prickly heat. Creamy and smooth, a potent full bodied sipper. This was a nice one, though one and done for me.

cpetrone84, Dec 11, 2014
4.75/5  rDev +5.8%

Hnnnng. I’ve drunk my body weight of this elixir of happiness on tap, so I suppose it’s time to crack a bottle & do a proper review. 750ML cracked open with my wife.

Unlike Eight, the red wax is like bloody rubber, & takes some thumb-endangering effort to saw through. Worth the effort, the beer comes out black as a harpy’s soul & with a lot more carbonation. Nose is gorgeously smooth, redolent with vanilla, booze, whiskey, & a healthy helping of chocolate. I like chocolate. I’d still go trick-or-treating if I could get away with it.

Darkness – High West Whiskey Barrel Aged makes me want to drink until I wake up in a pool of my own urine in a stranger’s yard. The High West Barrels were a stroke of genius & I would like to personally thank Surly for the Rye Whiskey input. Fantastic barrel influence, spicy rye, sweet malts, vanilla, sticky marshmallow that makes me pine for a bonfire. Mid-palate the booze kicks in: much more alcoholistic than regoolar old Darkness from former yields. Chocolate, molasses add to the Party Down South. The finish is pure integrated goodness. I can, and have, drunk this until I’ve forgotten the question. Yes.

The quality of Darkness has varied over the years, from the godly nectars of ’07, ’09, & ’13, to the sweet mess of ’08 & the weirdly porter-like, uh, whatever the hell year that was. This? A bloody masterpiece that’s a hot wedding of great barrel & great stout. An elite-level beer.

Poured from a waxed 750 ml bottle into a Surly Darkness chalice.

Appearance: Pours pitch black with a moderate amount of bubbles. About one finger of dark brown head which settles into a thin creamy layer.

Smell: A very rich, roasty, and sweet dark malt driven aroma with big hints of dark chocolate, coffee, dark fruit, and whiskey. Dark roasted barley malt with big upfront hints of dark chocolate, coffee beans, burnt toast, caramel, toffee, biscuit, and yeast. Sweet hints of molasses, syrup, and soy sauce. Good barrel presence with hints of rye whiskey, vanilla, and oak. Dark fruit hints of raisin, cherries, and fig. Subtle hints of earthy hops. Smells considerably sweeter than the regular Darkness.

Taste: Like it smells, a very rich, roasty, and fairly sweet dark malt driven taste with lots of dark chocolate, coffee, fruit, and whiskey. Upfront dark roasted barley malt with roasty notes of dark chocolate, coffee, toasted bread, biscuit, caramel, toffee, and yeast. Sweet taste of molasses and syrup. Barrel notes of rye whiskey, vanilla, oak, and wood. Slight presence of anise spice. Dark fruit undertones with notes of cherry, raisin, and fig. Bitter taste of earthy hops come through to help cut the sweetness. A very good tasting stout.

Mouthfeel: Full bodied with a moderate amount of carbonation. Rich, chewy, and fairly syrupy with a drying finish. Alcohol heat is minimal.

Overall: A really nice twist on an already great stout. The barrel aging adds some nice rye notes and sweetness without overwhelming the base notes. Sweeter and more syrupy than the regular Darkness but the hops help keep it in check.

A huge thanks to Jeff for this gem. 750ml waxed and capped bottle into a Cigar City snifter. Shared with schen9303.

I've always felt like Darkness was a beer that could really benefit from some barrel-aging. Still, I'm shocked at how different and how much better this beer is than the regular version. One of the best I've had in a loaded style.

Completely black brew (clear around the edges, if you really look), topped with a nice dark head. Slightly fizzy head, with OK retention (pretty good for the style).

Huge and complex aroma, featuring whiskey: coconut, mocha, and vanilla show prominently, while a hint of banana (from the yeast, likely) makes an appearance.

Flavor follows. Complex, thick, and delicious brew, with an absolutely smooth whiskey profile, amid notes of vanilla and coconut, chocolate and cream, and banana.

Thick and smooth feel; very potent but not overpowering. Phenomenal balance. Must be a high ABV beer, but it comes through so incredibly drinkable. One of the smoothest, most approachable (fresh) BBA stouts.

This is a truly fantastic BA stout; among the very best I've ever had. It has a thickness rivaled perhaps only by BCBS, with a complexity and barrel presence reminiscent of Firestone Walker - high praise, indeed. Beautiful beer.

larryi86, Mar 01, 2015
4.59/5  rDev +2.2%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.75

Served on draft at Local Option in Chicago, 10oz snifter. Reviewed from notes.

The beer pours a deep jet black, very opaque and somewhat viscous with a thin, finger high khaki head, moderate retention and a few swatches of soapy lace. The aroma is big on chocolate, charred oak, whiskey, toasted coconut with a bit of vanilla and some pitted stone fruits. The barrel is prominent, but somehow mild and approachable. The flavors mirror the nose well, but with more vigor and oopmh, huge chocolate with a touch of espresso, whiskey and charred oak barrel soon take over and bring out the toasted coconut with a strong caramel finish that showcases a bit of stone fruit and raisin. The mouthfeel is nice and thick, coats very well though it does have a bit of prickliness from the carbonation and finishes lightly dry, though warming from the ABV.

Verdict: An absolutely delicious RIS from Surly, perhaps the best version they've made of this beer yet, though the barrel treatment is a major departure from past batches. I'll concede that the bottles of the 2013 vintage sold at Binnys post release must've been special because those were out of this world, but this follows that batch up so incredibly well, wonderful barrel, excellent base and tons of depth and complexity. Darkness has evolved perfectly to fit the "modern world" where stouts seemingly have to be barrel aged. It set the standard for a non barrel aged stout and could easily be the benchmark for a barrel aged stout moving forward. Cheers to Surly.
