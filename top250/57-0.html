Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: Nav71292 on 06-11-2015

A beer brewed in the spirit of experimentation and the celebration of our 3rd anniversary… ! What we did here is we took a whole bunch of Mosaic and Amarillo hops, a classic punch of citrus purveying goodness, and added them to the Julius dry hop. The base beer is exactly the same, but the dry hop is altered tremendously. The result is a beer with all the deliciousness of the base beer with a layer of added citrusy complexity. Like devil Julius or something. A real treat!

Drank from a 1 pint can purchased at Tree House Brewing Company, Monson, MA
09 23 15
Served in a pint glass

The completely opaque, yellow ochre body was topped by a tight, foamy, inch tall crown. Typical appearance for a New England-style IPA. Excellent head retention. Drippy rings of lacing stuck along the glass.

The nose was solid but not as fragrant as I anticipated. Citrus notes dominated. Loads of orange and orange peel. Juicy. Hint of pine. There was a subtle, dry yeasty quality. Tropical notes were also detectible. Mango came forth as the beer warmed. Peach notes appeared as well.

The flavor profile was also super juicy and citrusy. Again, orange notes dominated. Touch of peach. Orange peel appeared on the back end. The beer finished dry and moderately bitter with strong notes of grapefruit grind. Commendable, just not mind-blowing like so many other Tree House beers.

On the palate, the liquid was medium bodied and contained a fairly standard, moderate effervescent. Nowhere near as soft and frothy as a number of their other beers. With time, the carbonation settled and the liquid turned softer and and smoother. Somewhat creamy.

If this beer was made by almost any craft brewery, I would probably be quite impressed. However, coming from one that routinely produces some of the best hoppy beers in the world, it was somewhat disappointing. Both the aroma and the flavor profile seemed a bit one-note, and the mouthfeel lacked the suppleness typically found in their other offerings. This is worth a try for sure but definitely a far cry from their best work.

Poured from a 16 oz can straight from the brewery.

The pour is a murky, deep orange color with a medium off-white head. Some mild lacing around the rim of the glass.

The aroma is fruity and extremely sweet, yet slightly piney. It's like Orange Fruit Punch with grass clippings. Very citrusy, perhaps moreso than Julius. A lot of orange and tangerine up front with the piney hop bitterness lingering in the back.

The taste is a lot more piney than the nose indicates, however it is still very citrus forward. I get some sweet, candied-orange flavors up front, followed by spicy pine and even a sticky sap-like flavor on the end. The aftertaste is a perfect balance of sweet and bitter.

The mouth is well-carbonated, a bit sticky, and full-bodied. Drinks more like a DIPA than a regular IPA.

Overall, another fantastic IPA offering from Tree House. Not as good as Julius or Haze, in my humble opinion, but it definitely holds its own.

look: 4.75 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.75

Pours a murky deep orange color with a two finger off-white head. Typical New England IPA appearance.

Aroma is much danker and has more grapefruit than it's counterpart Julius, which I really like. The Mosaic dry-hop accounts for that. Along with the herbaceous dank quality and grapefruit, there is peach, mango, papaya, and tangerine, all of which are very juicy. Some floral too. Extremely light bready malt and sweetness from the pale malts.

Just as the aroma indicated, the taste is much more dank. The herbaceous dank quality is there throughout. Lots of grapefruit come through, as well as light peach, juicy mango, papaya, passionfruit, and tangerine. Get some light breadiness and sugary sweetness from the malt. A grapefruit peel and light pine resin bitterness emerge on the finish to go along with the aforementioned juicy flavors.

Medium body. Very soft, smooth, and creamy. Very clean drinking beer.

I'm not sure if I like this or it's counterpart Julius more. I think this one wins by a hair because I love Mosaic hops and this one really shows off the dank, grapefruity quality that it can provide. So much vibrant, complex hop character, yet it is balanced perfectly.
