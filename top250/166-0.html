being an absolute lush for anything maple, this is one of the more compelling maple beers i have encountered, although it is in fact incredibly well integrated and not over the top on the flavor. its a matte dark black beer, dense but soft looking, with a lasting three quarters of an inch of tan foam. the nose is roasted barley first, tons of it, intense dark malt and warming boose, sort of blurring the stout/porter line more than it already is. the maple here adds a wooded character and a moderate sweetness, but its more much vibrant in the flavor than in the nose. in the flavor, its short of decadent, but still rather pronounced, deep amber maple, very nice, but also very well absorbed by the base beer, with its dark chocolate and chicory notes, this is one of the more earthy big dark beers going. sweetness rises up in the finish, but its cut by the booze that keeps getting more intense as i let my glass warm up a little. over the top delicious, regardless of whether or not i wish for more maple, things here are in perfect harmony. jackie o's now has to stand for me as one of the best and most intriguing breweries out there. have yet to have even a B from them, everything is A level for me, especially this one.

Bottle shared by Spycow. Thanks! Served in a tulip.

A - Tan foam settles to a collar, a few wisps, and minimal lace. Near-black body that seems appropriate for the name and style.

S - Smells like barrel-aged maple syrup. Bourbon, oak, vanilla, maple. There's a bit of dark bread and nutty malt in the background. Perhaps a hint of booze as it warms, but reasonably well-hidden.

T - The taste is a bit more heavier on base beer's malt character, with dark bread, nuts, and chocolate fudge. Less bourbon and maple, which is actually the detriment of the beer. Still pretty good; just not as great as the aroma.

M - Big and chewy body, with lingering stickiness. Minimal alcohol heat and a surprisingly soft texture. Minimal tannins present.

D - Quite good, although it's a tad too sweet and heavy to drink it large quantities. Still, really good brew that is reminiscent of the barrel-aged maple syrup Brad let us try at Jackie O's. Still, nice to see this brewery back on track after a stretch of middling brews.

This is some next level sh*t. Amazing.

Thanks to fiver29, a giant among traders, for sending me this weird little 375ML bottle. I’ve been thinking about it longingly all day; now I get to touch it. Happy.

The pour is dead-dirt black with slightly more carbonation, just a 75 year old man fringe around the edges. The nose is sweet & sweeter: big malts, rich chocolate, & those lovely maple notes, perhaps stronger than in any ‘maple’ beer I’ve had. I’m always stopping at far-flung roadside stands to buy local maple syrup. This is making me hungry for Belgian waffles…

Anyways, Black Maple – Bourbon Barrel Aged is a chameleon of a beer. First impression is all about the vanilla & bourbon mixing it up with the maple, with little hint of beer in this beer – not that I’m complaining, because %these are a few of my favorite things%. Then I read the label, & my brain starts reporting in about nuts & chocolate, & it’s all almond Hershey’s inside my skull. As it warms, the bourbon barrel straight-up takes over, & the other tastes recede into the background. As it warms some more, the maple returns in a lovely manner. Smooth as a red silk nightie, there’s no alcohol sizzle here, & it drinks like a much lighter beer.

A lovely, belly-warming jaunt, & I’m very glad I had almost seventeen ounces of this, because it’s gone faster than most brews. Very good beer.

TWStandley, Sep 14, 2014
4.73/5  rDev +6.1%
look: 4.75 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.75

Poured from the 375 ml bottle into a tulip. Appearance is completely black, with a thin brown head. The head dissipates fairly quickly, leaving a layer of film on top, as well as a ring around the edge of the glass.

The nose is loads of bourbon, some chocolate, and maple. As it warms, the bourbon becomes more intertwined with everything else, and it really begins to come together. The taste follows the nose in many ways, but it is a touch harsh at cooler temperatures. As it warms, the complexities come through and the chocolate, maple and bourbon play together wonderfully. A touch of roastiness comes through here as well. The bourbon is the focal point at the end of each taste. I had this side by side with a 2014 and the differences were quite interesting. The bourbon had taken a back-seat in the 2014 (as expected) and the maple and chocolate were the main players. It was by far the more enjoyable beer at cooler temperatures, but as it warms, I missed the complexities the bourbon presence offered. Either way, they were both great brews.

The mouthfeel is medium to full bodied, with just the right touch of carbonation. It goes down smoothly, and each sip leaves you going back for more. Overall, this is an absolutely wonderful beer, and is everything you're looking for in this style. The flavor profile as a whole is exceptional, and the complexities continually draw you in looking for more. Jackie O's continues to amaze.
