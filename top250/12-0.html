Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: ThatsThatDude on 03-10-2014

Our first Double IPA – dry, refreshing and hoppy. We really focused on hop flavor and aroma here. To maximize hop character, we dry hopped Dinner twice with over 6 lbs. of hops per barrel. For the best experience, please enjoy fresh as possible.

rudzud, May 08, 2015
4.97/5  rDev +6.7%
look: 4.5 | smell: 5 | taste: 5 | feel: 5 | overall: 5

A-Dinner time. Pours a hazy orange with yellow hues that emanate from its center. A strong swirl of my glass only aids the beer's pillowy head. The head settles down noticeably as a thin blanket of white fuzz. Lacing is quite sporadic.

S-The hops are resoundingly aromatic. I pick up lots of onion and garlic but it's not the predominant hop aroma. Dinner is also loaded with specifics such as passion fruit, pineapple, mango, peach and apricot. A supple layer of malty, bready sweetness helps out as well.

T-The hops are so aggressive yet so classy all at the same time. Again, I get some hefty green onion and garlic. Then comes the ridiculous onslaught of tropical flavors. Mangoes, melons and papayas abound! 'Dank', marijuana flavor as well. Pick a hoppy flavor descriptor and it's there - each sip brings forth more unique hoppy flavors. Caramel and bready maltiness pops up slightly. In the end, it's all about the hops and the flavors here are spectacular.

M-Creamy mouthfeel. Medium bodied, softer carbonation. The bitterness is sneakily potent; it hides behind the flavor and proceeds to constrict my taste buds. Alcohol is ever so noticeable.

O-Dinner is an unbelievable beer with plenty of grit and muscle. Yet, the balance between power and complexity is what makes this beer elite. I have always been a Maine Beer fan but this beer just made everything different. Dinner is one of the best beers I have ever tasted.

The beer comes in a dark peachy orange.

The aroma offers lots of peach, passion fruit, sweet mangoes, and pineapple. There is also a underlying kumquat and tangerine peel. Some rock candy sweetness. Very reminiscent of a sugary lollipop or candy.

On the flavor, up front is pink grapefruit, oranges, tangerine, citrus peel, it transitions to some mangoes and pineapple. There are some lacking parts on the palate, but it is still a clean hop bomb. It drinks under its ABV. An underlying note of grassiness offers bitterness and drying. There is a medium bitterness on the back end and a dank quality as well.

The mouthfeel is creamy at first, but then flies off the palate a bit. Not as chewing and creamy as some Hill Farmstead or Tree House brews.

By far Maine Beer's best beer and puts them on level with some of the top hoppy breweries, but this offering is one point below some of the elite.

99/100

HUGE thanks to Bill for this...

A. Pours a hazy, brilliant orange. Half finger of puffy, soapy white head with very good persistence. Lacing is consistent, coating, and sticky.

S. Heavily tropical, candied oranges, pineapple, and tangerine explode on the nose. Lush, dank hops creep in on the back end and provide a bit of spice to the affair, but overall this has a luscious, tropical, gorgeous bouquet. Top marks.

T. Just as amazing as the nose, perhaps moreso. Orange zest, pineapple, a little bit of grainy, biscuity malt, then a perfect resolution of strawberry, watermelon, peach juice, and bitter pine needle. This is incredibly tropical and incredibly complex. There's not much else to say other than that it is perfection.

M. Light to moderate carb, with a thick and creamy mouthfeel. Balance is impeccable...it's big and tropical and dank while not being bitter, sticky sweet, or boozy. Outstanding.

O. One of the best IPAs I have ever had. It's got one of the biggest tropical notes I have found and somehow avoids the "sticky sweet" trap and keeps things hoppy and dank throughout. Super complex on the nose and the palate, with an impressive number of citrus and tropical fruit notes popping up seemingly everywhere. And a great mouthfeel, like peaches and cream. Maine has been producing excellent hoppy beers for awhile now...Lunch was an absolute revelation. But Dinner may be their best work yet. Do whatever it takes to try this.
