Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: Kevincredible on 09-12-2014

Brewed with Monson’s own Maxwell Road Maple Grade A Dark Amber Maple syrup, Good Morning pours pitch black in the glass with a creamy mousse-like head. The bubbles give way to aromas of rich milk chocolate, cocoa powder, and dark amber maple syrup. The flavor starts as a blast of milk chocolate, sweet maple syrup, and rich fresh coffee as deeper complexities are uncovered as it warms. The crew here at Tree House tastes “chocolate covered maple candy”, “vanilla”, “chocolate cake”, “brown sugar”, and “fresh roasted coffee”. A super decadent treat, Good Morning begs to be shared and contemplated!

Reviewing Good Morning from Treehouse Brewing Company out of Monson, Massachusetts.

Score: 97

Six week old growler (my bad) served in a Bourbon County snifter and enjoyed on 11/06/14 after being stored in a low-temp fridge continuously. Thank you immensely Rudzud for sending me this one!

Appearance: Pours an inky black color with a half finger of tan head that settles to a bubbly ring around the glass. Excellent lacing and lengthy retention with a high cling factor on the residuals. Gorgeous stout. 5/5

Smell: The aroma is a perfect trifecta of coffee, chocolate and maple syrup. Lovely roastiness too. This one rivals Morning Delight, through Morning Delight has a bolder chocolate character, if memory serves. While there is no mistaking this one for a kitchen cooking up fresh chocolate chip pancakes, it is still excellent. 5/5

Taste: Bold mix of coffee and maple syrup, with lots of maple flavor without much sweetness. The coffee character is stronger on the taste than it was present in the nose. There is not a big chocolate presence in the flavor. Coffee upfront with a long syrupy finish. Has a great balance between bitterness and sweetness, no booziness. I am frankly shocked by how much maple is present without this brew being sweet. 4.75/5

Mouthfeel: Medium bodied, appropriately low level of carbonation. Lovely bittersweet balance, lasting and coating flavors. 4.75/5

Overall: This is an amazing beer. I cannot imagine how much better this is even fresher, at which point I am sure it rivals Toppling Goliath's Morning Delight even moreso. My only knock here is the muted chocolate on the taste, but that is splitting hairs since there is a lovely 1-2 punch of coffee followed by maple, with a great mouthfeel and balance to boot. Seek this one out, you will not be disappointed. This was one of the best new beers I encountered in 2014.

Cost: $12 for a 750 ml flip-top growler (+$6 for the glass).

Drank from a 1 pint, 9.4 fluid ounces growler purchased at Tree House Brewing Company, Monson, MA
Served in a snifter

The beer poured an inky jet black and looked shimmery in the glass. A frothy, one-finger tall, tan colored crown topped the liquid and slowly faded to a thin, bubbly ring. Spotty lacing clung to the glass.

The aroma was outstanding. Intense espresso notes dominated. Ground espresso beans particularly came to mind. Rich and fragrant with loads of fresh vanilla. Chocolate and maple syrup notes came through as well. Rather sweet overall, but quite amazing.

Like the nose, the flavor profile was super rich and robust with immense amounts of French roast coffee, espresso and roasted malts. Dark amber maple syrup and vanilla came through strong in the center. Some semisweet chocolate as well. Bitter roast and crushed espresso beans appeared in the finish. The bitterness on the back end actually helped keep the sweetness in check. A touch of ashiness lingered on the palate.

The mouthfeel was rich and creamy. A super fine, slick carbonation turned the beer frothy and fluffy on the palate. After each sip, the liquid left a slightly sticky film on my lips.

I really loved Tree House's Double Shot, and Good Morning takes that beer and cranks it up to eleven. Although this is an intensely flavored imperial stout, its rather modest ABV means that you can drink a fair amount of it in a sitting. Now, if only they would do a bourbon barrel aged version of this.

Batch 1: From notes- my first review, after hundreds of great beers. This beer demanded review.

Enjoyed over brunch with my girlfriend out of Tree house Teku glassware. Not our first rodeo with this beer.

Nose-Brownie Crust- roasted notes and rich chocolate smells blend. Breathing deeper adds some maple syrup to the mix, and a small vanilla wafer smell. Some coffee beans on the tail end, with more coffee presence as this baby warms.

Look-Molasses head with a small light biscuit colored ring, rich dark brown near black beer as thick as can be. Some large and some small bubbles that stick around and look like chunky real hot chocolate foam. Light legs. Mixed light and dark spots on the foam.

Mouthfeel-This is where the beer excels- it is the most luscious mouthfeel of any beer I have ever had. Similar to some good bottles of Birth of Tragedy-creamy, rich, thick, like a light chocolate shake. Coats the tongue.

Taste- progression starts with sweet marsh-mellow flavor, with some vanilla bean aspects. Quickly some coffee flavor, which starts like an over roasted black coffee and then tastes sweetened with cream somehow. As the flavor closes, a real cocoa chocolate flavor comes on that when combined with the light alcohol presence almost tastes like cinnamon or spice of some kind.

General consensus is this would crush barrel aged, although I don't necessarily see a ton of room for improvement. The beer works so well and is an amazing treat. Needs to be bottled so that I can break this out during a snowstorm.
Edit: Did drink this during a snowfall, it did rule.
