22 oz. bottle, Batch # 744 - 745. The latest DIPA creation from the masters at Pipeworks, this one brewed with honey, passion fruit puree, and citra hops. Sounds absolutely delicious. Expectations are high considering the source.

Poured into a clear balloon snifter.

A - It pours a juicy golden orange body. One finger of soapy white head with good froth and thickness. Leaves good white bubbles and lots of sticky thin scattered lacing. Looks excellent.

S - A most ridiculously fresh, fruity, & floral aroma. Tons of fresh beautiful Citra hop goodness, and a sweetened honey balance.

T - Taste follows the nose: extremely juicy, fruity, & floral. I wouldn't know this was brewed with passion fruit just by flavor alone, but it is very clearly heavily imparted with juicy stone fruit goodness. Drinks like a nice glass of juice.

M - Extremely smooth, fruity, and juicy. Medium bodied with excellent carbonation. Super smooth drinking for a 9.50% abv beer. No alcohol presence is noted at all.

Overall, an amazing DIPA from Pipeworks. One of the best I've had from them yet, which is definitely saying something at this point. An excellent beer in a swelling line up of excellent beers.

Pipeworks Spotted Puffer -----4.5/5

wolfpac255, Aug 08, 2015
4.8/5  rDev +4.6%
look: 4.5 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.75

Serving: 22 oz. bomber poured into a mason jar. Bottled on 6/17/15, making this just over 2 weeks old.

A: Pours a pretty cloudy dark orange color. Soft white 1/4-inch head.

S: Tropical fruit explosion up front. As you make your way past the pineapple, orange, tangerine, and papaya, you land square on the passion fruit. Very strong, fresh and clean.

T: Oh yeah. Passion fruit hits first here. Tart, fresh, sweet and so tasty. Balanced in the finish is the rest of the tropical bouquet of tangerine, pineapple, tangerine. Hops all the way, no noticeable malt balance, and not needed really.

M: Full, rich. Great carbonation. Like the other "fish" beers, alcohol is really well hidden again.

O: Now this is how you do a Square Grouper variant. The passion fruit could not be implemented any better. It doesn't really dominate the beer, but rather enhances the amazing base beer. Super balance, super drinkable, tons of flavor. I thought Crimson Snapper was the best variant thus far, but this is definitely better. I think Crimson was crafted nearly as well, but I think passion fruit just works a bit better with the Citra hop than blood orange does. Not sure if it's better than the original Square Grouper, I guess I'd have to do a side by side to be sure. Nonetheless, it's a close 1-2.

Recommendation: Probably snatched up by now in Chicago, but absolutely worth an IP trade. Top 3-5 Pipeworks beer right here.
