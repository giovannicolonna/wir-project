From a can, pours a just a slightly hazy golden orange color with a big full white head. The head lasts the whole time and laces the glass as it goes down.

The nose is really big and bright. Great malt backbone, a little sweet tropical fruit. It also has a fair amount of orange citrus and pineapple. It smells great. I can't really smell much alcohol on the nose.

The flavor is sweeter, caramel malt, citrus and tea leaves, sweet tropical fruit. I would like a little more bitter hop content, but it goes down really easy. Zero bitterness, all sweet malt on the back end. Not dry, moderately wet and juicy.

Overall, this is dangerously drinkable and tasty.

Appearance: Pours a clear amber with an impressively sticky head; leaves lacing all over the side of the glass

Smell: Exceedingly floral and sugary, with pith and tropical fruit tones

Taste: A lot of sugary shortbread, up front, with the 100 IBUs and six hop varieties assaulting the tongue in short order; tropical fruits galore, with guava, pineapple, kumquat, lychee...everything but grapefruit; finishes bitter but the residual sugar sticks to the teeth

Mouthfeel: Full bodied with moderate carbonation

Overall: I was excited to try this beer, even though I heard it was very sweet and I am not a fan of a lot of sweetness in my IPAs and DIPAs; still, I was unprepared for how sweet this beer drinks; clearly, a lot of BAs love it but I prefer my beers in this genre to be dryer

Can sent to me by spinaltapped, thanks my man.

Pours a golden slightly dark yellow, with a half inch head, and a little bit of lacing.

Smells very sweetly hoppy, tons of tangerine, orange, grapefruit, peach, and mango. Just insanely juicy.

This is really, really sweet, but it still works. I get some sugar-covered fruits, and that's the extent of the malt, but it's very overpowering, but very delicious. So much juicy fruity hop flavor, as expected with a beer hopped with Cascade, Centennial, Chinook, Amarillo, Galaxy, and Citra. I get a ton of varying fruits- they're all listed in the aroma. Mildly bitter, mostly sweet, and very, very dank.

This is medium bodied, with a very sticky and juicy mouthfeel. It's got a moderate amount of carbonation.

This is absolutely amazing. I could drink a ton of these, and it probably wouldn't be pretty, since it's 10% and it hides that fact so well.

Canned on 10/7/14. 12oz can poured into a pint glass. Pours golden with nice clarity; fluffy eggshell white head that fades slowly leaving great lacing and retention. Looks nice. The aromas are full of tropical and citrus notes. Lots of pungent mango, pineapple and orange zest. There's some sweet caramel malts in the mix. It also smells like a sugar cookie. The flavors are bittersweet. There's a huge blast of hops followed by a nice sweetness. The malts aren't shy, but the hops still shine. Lots of grass. Tons of pineapple. There's a subtle warmth in the finish, but it's not hot or boozy. Mouthfeel is full-bodied with moderate carbonation. It's very smooth and easy drinking. This is dangerously drinkable despite having a subtle alcoholic kick.

This is one of those DIPAs that is among the best out there. Is it the best DIPA I've ever had? Probably not, but it's in the ballpark. This is a homerun for Revolution. Recommended.

Poured from the can dated 10/7/14 into a Spiegelau IPA glass. Appears a beautiful clear orange color with a nice thick off-white head. The head dissipates slowly and leaves great lacing, as well as rings around the glass after each sip.

The nose is full of citrus. Tangerines, mangos and grapefruit dominate this. There's a touch of sweetness to it, but the hops are definitely the key. The taste follows suit here as well. The sweetness comes through first, with touches of the tangerines, mangoes and oranges. This is followed by a slight grapefruit note and a nicely bitter finish.

The mouthfeel is medium to heavy bodied; slightly sticky finish. The carbonation is spot on. Overall, this is an excellent new brew. The citrus focus makes it a refreshing, enjoyable beer, and it is surprisingly drinkable (despite the name) even at 10%. Definitely worth seeking out, and I hope to find a few more in distribution.

look: 4.25 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 5 | overall: 4.5

12oz can, dated 10.7.2014, poured into a Pipeworks snifter.


The beer pours a slightly hazy copper color, but with a few bubbles rising to the top of the glass where a finger of thick, white, soapy head that has nice retention and yields a few webs of lace. The aroma is big, plenty of that 10% ABV shows, bitter hops that are clean, crisp, floral, citrusy, juicy with a bit of pine, malt is an afterthought, but likely is noticeable as sweetness behind the alcohol. The flavors really shine, big hops, bitter but juicy, very balanced yet intense, big grapefruit, peach, mango, orange zest, pine, wildflower while the malt is smooth and rich, lightly sweet and brings out the alcohol, which is hidden really well on the tongue. The mouthfeel is nice, very rich, full bodied, coats extremely well and the carbonation is perfect while the finish is dry, but not overly so.


Verdict: A really superb DIPA from Revolution, their best showcase of hops to date and arguably the best IPA to come out of Chicago that isn't from Chicago's honorary annex of Munster, Indiana. Big, bold, bitter but balanced and not too biting, just what one asks for in a DIPA. The potent alcohol on the nose is the only thing that is holding this back from being a truly world class DIPA, kudos to Rev, this is excellent!
