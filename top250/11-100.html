Big and rich, completely black but in some very dark brown at the thick edges, there's a certain oily texture to this big stout. The head is a brown-colored one about a finger tall. Retention is good, and it manages some good lacing despite the oils.
Coffee! Lots of fresh coffee, just-roasted grounds all in the nose and mouth. It's the forefront, but it lets the stout express itself all the same. There's lots of dark cocoa, and the bourbon barrel makes its way in softly but firmly with notes of vanilla and wood while offering just a little heat. The wood is dry and oaky, not a soft wood by any means. The coffee is just so rich and full, especially in the nose. It's like drinking a shot of espresso while sniffing a cup of fresh grounds right next to it after putting a little of very rich chocolate sauce, fresh made from cocoa beans, while having a snifter of bourbon right next to it.
The body is full, rich and thick, but it evens and lightens out in a surprising way, certainly more than I expect. I also can feel a certain oiliness to the grounds on the tongue. It's a good stout, and plenty rich, but it could be cleaner.

I hate to join the stampede, but hell, this is a great beer. Harnessing the bourbon of the original, cutting it with just the right amount of evidently quality coffee, & doing who knows what else to it. Darn near perfect. I will seek out more of this, tomorrow.

Poured into a little, fancy shaped whiskey glass that's kind of like the buttercup part of a buttercup. It's as opaque and lightly fizzy as regular BCS.

The beer smells and tastes like a much less abrasive version of regular BCS. Or like a BCS that's aged 4-5 years. It's one of the least coffeeish coffee stouts I've ever had, like somehow the black coffee took a rough, bitter RIS and made it sweet and smooth.

The whiskeyness is barely there, and while it makes the beer more drinkable and more appealing (I assume) to a general audience, it turns out that I actually really enjoy the pain of a regular BCS.

But judging the beer on its own, it's a pretty remarkable achievement to make a beer this big tastes this smooth. Starts out with a tiny hint of whiskey, with a small burn that lasts throughout the sip. This moves into a milky, chocolately mocha, moving into the rough and lightly sour grain of a medium-strength porter, and this porter aftertaste is all that's left once the beer is swallowed.

Very good, and definitely worth trying, but I'd honestly prefer a regular BCS.

Pours a motor oil black color with a short brown head that quickly dissipates into a light ring.

The aroma is of freshing ground coffee beans and vanilla, thus a french vanilla coffee scent. Beautiful caramel and bourbon aroma. When cold, it smells very boozy but as it warms, it really breaks out. Roasted barley and chocolate round out the aroma.

The taste is more similar to the base bourbon county stout. Bourbon, vanilla, and caramel really stand out. The roast and coffee play a secondary role. Perhaps not as sweet as the base BCS, but still very sweet. There seems to be a fruity flavor as well.

Full bodied with soft carbonation. Very sweet on the palate and it finishes sticky and syrupy. It is incrediably smooth and the ABV is somewhat balanced out.

Very good stout but I must say I am a bit disappointed. I was expecting much more coffee flavor. This seems to be much closer to the base beer than the other variations. Definitely had other porters or stouts with more coffee flavor.
