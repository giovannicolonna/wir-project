On tap (too cold) at Eureka Santa Barbara.

Look: pitch black and viscous, faint ring of cinnamon-colored head.

Smell: vanilla and bourbon. Don't get a lot of other classic BA stout notes (roast, coffee, chocolate, oak).

Taste: follows the nose, but with more coconut, milk chocolate, coffee. The booze is smooth, not at all harsh.

Feel: medium, but surprisingly light for the style. Silky, and pretty dry finish.

Overall: it's good and I'm glad to have tried it, but the people trading away their firstborn for it could just splash a little nice bourbon and some good vanilla extract a into a solid stout and get 90% of the experience with way less trouble.
