As others have said, this beer is so hazy that if you were otherwise unfamiliar with Trillium you just might think there was something wrong with it. The unbelievable smell quickly kicks that notion to the curb though. This is right in the sweet zone of that "Vermont Nose" that is just about my favorite thing going in beer at the moment. Lots of fruit (mango/pineapple is how I think of this - though you could probably find a whiff of just about any tropical fruit if worked at it a bit) as well as very fresh, green, cannibis tinged hops. The taste is much more the latter than the former.

I don't think I like this quite as much straight Congress Street, which is just about perfect as far as I'm concerned, but it's absolutely delicious just the same.

meefmoff, Apr 22, 2015
4.52/5  rDev -2.2%

750 mL bottle purchased at Trillium. Poured into a HF wine glass at 39F

Reviewed using the old scoring system.

A: 4.25 Foamy white head 1.5" thick with full carbonation. Cloudy to murky straw colored body with fine carbonation. Head regenerates with gentle swirling. Fine lacing remains on the glass.

S: 4.50 Overwhelming dank hops with notes of pine and resin. Citrus fruits so present in the standard Congress Street are less obvious here. Notes of peach and papaya. Malt backbone remains muted.

T: 4.50 Juicy, piney hops all the way. This screams West Coast IPA. Loving that n east coast brewery can make this. Pineapple and mango flavors areresent but subservient to the hops. Lingering bitter aftertaste, but pleasant. Missing the dryness of standard Congree Street.

M: 4.00 Very sticky and oily. This coats the mouth and lingers. Good carbonation balances the richness. Nrmally, I prefer a clean finish, but the hoppy remnants work here

O: 4.75 Glad to see the east coast representing a West Coast IPA style so well. This is a nice improvement on an already great beer. Highly recommended.

Appearance - Hazy golden brew with tangerine tint. Frothy head, and just looks hoppy

Smell - Lots of tropical and citrus fruits. Mango and orange dominated my nostrils. Slight pine in my final whiffs

Taste - very smooth and slight graininess with sweet and bitter fruits. Actually get a little nectarine and virtually no intense bitterness although it grows with each sip

Mouthfeel - Nice and dry with a touch of sweetness from the hops. It has just enough body to hold up to the obvious late boil aand dry-hop additons.

Overall - great version of a ubiquitous style. Very delightfully drinkable. Extremely hoppy without being a palate destroyer. Look forward to more beers from this gem of a brewery
