Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: ThatsThatDude on 03-10-2014

Our first Double IPA – dry, refreshing and hoppy. We really focused on hop flavor and aroma here. To maximize hop character, we dry hopped Dinner twice with over 6 lbs. of hops per barrel. For the best experience, please enjoy fresh as possible.

16.9 oz. bottle poured into a tulip.

Appearance: Pours a hazy, glowing orange with a huge white head that shows good retention. Some dotty lacing is observable. Lots of carbonation going on in there. Okay, let's see if this is the real deal ...

Aroma: Huge, strong, very attractive nose. Lots of tropical fruit. Kind of smells like apricots and candy. Also peach, mango. Pine is there. Definitely one of the best beers I've ever smelled.

Taste: Wow. The taste fulfills the nose. Bursting with big tropical fruit flavors. The mango comes forward a bit as it warms. Peach, apricot still there. That pine note is still there. Little bit of sap. Impressively dry despite the sugarish aroma in the nose. Everything you've heard about this beer is true.

Mouthfeel: Remarkably smooth. Not abrasive at all despite super hops and pretty high alcohol by volume. Alcohol, by the way, is deep in the background. Deceptively, almost dangerously, drinkable.

Overall: A powerhouse. Everything is true. Terrific beer.

look: 5 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.75

My good friend brought over a bottle of Dinner to share, and I'm SO GLAD he did. This is an unbelievably good beer. The bottle was dated 7/31/14, so about a week old.

Poured from the bottle into large tulip glasses, the beer is a nearly opaque orange-yellow (apricot?) with a thin tight white head.

The aroma is just HUGE fruity hops - tropical fruit and citrus mainly, with a hint of resin and pine.

Surprisingly, the first thing that hits the taste palette is grapefruit - I did not pick up grapefruit in the nose. This is followed up with grapefruit peel, mango, orange, passion fruit, and more tropical deliciousness, ending with pine, resin, dank (cannabis?) and then just the most beautifully balanced bitterness I've ever experienced in a beer.

Full bodied, slightly dry, smooth and totally luscious.

Overall, this is one of the best, perhaps the single best, beer I've ever tasted.

Medium bodied w/ adequate carbonation and middle of the road dryness. I drank the balls off this one in a minute! The smoothness and absence of bitterness make this one dangerous to endulge in for sure.

Overall, worthy of the hype and accolades, an awesome and unique animal. This was one of the tougher reviews I have written in some time as this new school DIPA was a beer all it's own and different from mostly anything else I have sampled in the category. The closest beer I can describe it to is Fiddlehead Mastermind kicked up a notch. Anyway, the 3rd beer to beat out Heady in my book and only slightly trailing Double Sunshine and the mighty Sosus. Great work Maine Brewing Co.!
