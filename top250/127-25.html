T: Vanilla and cinnamon come first before some dark, nearly raw cocoa, and moderate nuts, while the finish brings up just enough chili to live things up.
M: probably a bit too carbed (maybe the keg?) and feels a bit lighter than it should, but that also makes it easier drinking than it has any business being.
O: a fine chili stout: better than all but Cake IMHO.

KSOZE, Sep 02, 2015

look: 4.25 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

Bottled July 2015 enjoyed August 24th, 2015 in an Indians tulip/snifter.

Look - Head was slow to develop with this one. It did dissipate quickly from a half finger of tan head. I don't even want to say there is a ring left, nothing. No lacing except for a few errant bubbles. Oily black body.

Smell - Ginger bread. Chocolate. Cinnamon. Chocolate. Coffee.

Taste - Nutty. Gingerbread. Vanilla. Chocolate. Spicy finish. Cinnamon more so than peppers.

Feel - Heavy. Spiciness on tongue. Carbonation is accented with spice on swallow.

Overall - I finally have it, a stout with enough spice that is not overpowering. I still want Evil Twin Jesus with Peppers. Cinnamon over peppers in this one. Gingerbread stout.

Poured from a bottle from the recent release. Pours dark brown with minimal head. Smell is a gorgeous mix of chocolate and spice. Flavor is insanely complex, with lots of chocolate, and hints of vanilla, cinnamon, molasses, and oak. The heat is not overpowering, but certainly present, which allows it to complement the other flavors, rather than dominate the experience, as is the case with many other beers in this style. The mouthfeel is medium carbonation, and a hint of a bite provided by the spice. Overall, this is almost a perfect beer, and one of my favorites of all time.
