Thanks to my buddy Chris for bringing me a six-pack!

Pours golden in color with a tight white head that reduces to a layer in my tulip glass.

Fantastic aroma of tropical fruit: guava, pineapple, grapefruit.

The flavor is also tropical with flavors of guava and pineapple mixing with herbal hop notes and a hint of malt sweetness.

A tropical beer from Georgia? Believe it. This is a great beer. It reminds me of the excellent Cigar City Jai Alai. If you like juicy IPAs, you have to try this one.

Growler from 5 Points Bottle Shop in Athens, GA

Poured into a Founders Snifter.

Pours an attractive cloudy orange with a fluffy head. Looks raw, but not off putting.

Aroma is mostly citrus. Has that classic AIPA citrus hop aroma. A touch of funk from the Citra hops - just enough to give it character.

Flavor is really nice. The bitterness is substantial, but not so much that it will scare anyone away. The citrus flavor from the hop aroma shines through nicely. The bitterness lingers briefly.

Mouthfeel is spot on for the style. Medium dry, with enough malt character to keep it interesting.

Overall, this is the best AIPA I've had from Athens. I think it could use a little refinement, but this could easily make it into the class of something like Jai Alai with a little work. I'm glad Creature Comforts is finally pouring beer, and I hope they are pouring killer beer in Athens for a long, long time.

A big thanks to HopDawg03 for getting this beer for me.

12 oz. can into a snifter. Pours a cloudy, but vibrant orange when held to light. About 1/2 a finger of white, sticky foam. Tropical fruit nose, which is amazing. Powerful notes of grapefruit, mango, tangerine, pineapple, banana...Medium weight, and has a pretty crisp feel. Lots of tart fruit up front, which surprised me. Reading into it, though, I see this beer is brewed with passion fruit, which makes sense. A slight bitterness, but I'd guess pretty low IBU - perfect for a session IPA. I get some grapefruit and pineapple on the back end, as well as on the aftertaste. This beer finishes smooth and leaves a little bit of a sour note behind.

Overall, this beer isn't your run-of-the-mill IPA. It's neither a pine-bomb, nor is it overly grapefruit-forward. The use of an unconventional fruit as the main flavor sets this one apart, and in a good way. It's not what I was expecting, but damn, it's good.

Pours an opaque deep gold/pale amber producing 2 fingers of variously bubbled, frothy, beige, moderately lasting head that leaves a nice webby coating of lace. Aroma while not robust is wet & as touted on the can "juicy" being "Hawaiian Punch" tropical fruity without the perfuminess, particularly mango, pineapple & passion fruit, with citrus notes of orange & tangerine & floral/leafy hints backed by caramel malt tones. Taste is similar but the hoppiness is less tropical & more balanced with the stronger citrus, resinous & leafy/floral notes backed by sweet caramel providing a better hop/malt balance than the nose. Starts hoppy but slightly sweet, balancing toward bittersweet hops/sweet malt in the middle, gravitating slowly towards bitter again in the finish, slowly transitioning to mostly bitter in the aftertaste until the sweet is replaced by thin astringency. Just enough hop bittering & malt sweetness here to call this brew balanced. Mouthfeel is medium bodied with moderate carbonation, wet, juicy & slightly prickly throughout becoming faintly drawing in the aftertaste.
