The beer comes in an insane pineapple/fresh squeezed orange juice color.

The aroma jumps of kumquats, passionfruit, guava, orange, yellow grapefruit, lemon, dank Mosaic, pineapple. Just an insane melange of fruit from Galaxy, balanced by Mosaic's complexity.

The beer upfront drinks of passionfruit, pineapple, pulpy orange juice, lemon, a light crackery malt, which is almost nonexistent in the middle, hints of "gummy" yeast esters. The finish goes on of yellow grapefruit pith and more dank from the Mosaic, along with lots of fresh mint. The beer drinks as if hints of tropical fruit juice were poured into it.

The carbonation is light on this, reminiscent of a Hill Farmstead brew, but still very much Trillium, the best Trillium.

Poured from a 750ml bottle into a Trillium Artaic glass (noted so I can show off my cool glassware like the other reviewers on this site):

Appearance: hazy and very turbid pinkish orange color. Frothy white head comprised of tons of little bubbles that lingered for a while. Sticky, persistent lacing throughout drinking.

Aroma: one of the most pungent smells of any beer I've ever tried. Placed it down on the counter after I poured it and I could smell it from the other room. Crazy strong scent of grapefruit juice especially, with some pineapple and grapefruit rind mixed in. Easily the most refreshing smelling DIPA I've had.

Taste: Lots of floral, dank hops at the beginning, immediately followed by tons of grapefruit. Quite tropical in flavor, and really matches the smell. Bitter finish, that to me perfectly matched the aroma I got from the beer. Lots of tropical fruit flavor, followed by the rind from the fruit on the finish. Wow!

Feel: Medium-bodied and soft, with a super-long, lingering finish of stickiness and bitter hops.

Overall: it has been a while since I have had a beer this good. One of the best double IPAs I've ever had, and I wouldn't expect any less from Trillium.
