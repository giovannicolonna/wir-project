Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: Robo123 on 09-09-2015

In 2014, I brewed an American Pale Ale - Beneficiaries of Chance - in celebration of marrying the love of my life, Lauren (aka Tree House L-Dog). On the eve of our first anniversary, I brewed another beer for Lauren, In Perpetuity. It hopes to celebrate foreverness - in love, passion, and regardless of circumstance - the notion that what is good and beloved will last forever. In Perpetuity is brewed with a simple malt bill and two of my favorite hops - Citra, and Nelson. It pours a hazy bright orange in the glass and mixes notes of tropical fruit with a strong citrus backbone. Soft, and eminently drinkable. I hope you enjoy it.

Notes on the can design: Artist and friend Dean McKeever has created a memorable piece of artwork displaying Sand Beach in Acadia National Park at night - a magical place that everyone in New England should visit... especially at night.

A: Bright orange, looks to be almost glowing in the light. Hazy with a thick, foamy white head that dissipates very slowly and sticks to the glass.
S: Melon, bubblegum sweetness, white wine, tropical citrus, sticky hop dankness. Sweetness combined with tropical notes really seem to be the dominant aromas.
T: Upon sipping, it reminds me of "Julius" with the orange like citrus that can be detected, but then it quickly transitions into "Green" with the strong notes of melon. Doesn't taste as sweet as I detected initially in the aroma. Juicy, citrus hops burst in the mouth. Definitely a lot closer in taste to "Julius".
M: Soft, pillowy mouthfeel. Hops are slightly prickly to the tongue. Incredibly smooth, balanced and crisp. Slight bitterness, but almost unnoticeable and there is no dryness. Medium carbonation. Body is reminiscent to Tree House "Green".
O: Another amazing beer by Tree House…up there with "Green", "Alter Ego" and "Julius". Exceptional cross between "Julius" and "Green". On 9/18/15 I did a taste test between Julius and IP and I have to give the nod to IP as it seems to be a tad more complex taste and mouthfeel wise.

Poured from a 32oz growler on 9/23/15, which was filled on 9/17/15 and delivered in an absolute porch bomb courtesy of SDRedDevil, an amazing BA and great guy! Looks like many other TH beers that I've had, which is to say great. Very non-aggressive pour still led to about 3+ fingers of very fluffy white head (may very well be due to being sent in mail). Very hazy orange in appearance, reminiscent of Alter Ego.

Aroma was a little bit disappointing for a TH hoppy beer. Smelled more citra than nelson on the nose, but the aroma just wasn't very strong. Taste was very juicy and tropical fruit up front and mid palate with both orange, peaches and mango from Citra along with some melon and lemon in there showcasing the Nelson. There is a tiny bit of bitterness on the back end, but only a hint, and it just finishes very crisp, dry and smooth.

I've never even heard of another Citra/Nelson beer before but this makes me wonder why not. Although it may just be that Nate is able to balance the two so well because that's just what he excels at more than almost any other brewer out there. As is always the case with TH beers, this one is so amazingly balanced and just smooth. Definitely not as creamy or as in your face juice bomb as many of their other offerings, but its complex and very well balanced. Definitely a fantastic beer....that being said, I still don't fully understand the totally over the top ratings this beer is getting. It is amazing no doubt, and it is better than their average offering (which is already just incredible), however, I was still expecting more given the intense hype behind this beer. That however is not the fault of the beer or of TH...
