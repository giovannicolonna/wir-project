Thanks to GRG1313 for sharing this one at his tasting.

Pours a murky yellow-orange with a foamy bone head that settles to a partial film on top of the beer. Foamy patches of lace form around the glass on the drink down. Smell is of citrus fruit, citrus zest, tropical fruit, and earth aromas. Taste is much the same with citrus zest, tropical fruit, and earth flavors on the finish. There is a mild amount of hop bitterness on the palate with each sip. This beer has a lower level of carbonation with a slightly crisp mouthfeel. Overall, this is a very good beer that is tasty, but the hop aspects come through slightly better in the smell to me.

Serving type: bottle.

this phenomenal and rare beer comes to me by great generous courtesy of philbolt, a fellow BA from across the country that was far too kind with his access to cool beer that before now only existed in my dreams. thanks new friend! this is a hectic brew, totally east coast. looks like a vermont one with its hazy beige color, thick and yeasty looking, lovely head on it, but man, this is cloudy and dense beyond the level of even the other east coast monsters, almost looks less refined, intense, no light passing through this milky brew. the smell is its best feature, i wish they said which hops were used, but i can only stab at that, its super floral and citrusy both, short of tropical, but i get fresh cut pineapple, tangerine, even lemongrass in here. the malt base is odd. seems like it almost has to be wheat centered, but the texture isnt there all the way, and its hard to pick out what else is in here, maybe oats with all this density. it has a remarkable hop flavor to it too, scaled back just a little from the smell, but its pungent and different, and taste like something hill farmstead would make, or has made. the process here is very cool, and i immediately want to know more. i think it came through just a bit heavy for perfection, yeasty and really viscous for the style, and where it flatters some of its peers, its overdone here by a small margin that makes it a slower drinker and a weird one all in all. very awesome to taste this beer ahead of my trip to boston next week where i will surely get my hands on some more of their stuff, and its really exciting to get to taste beer like this out here. thanks again philbolt, looking forward to sharing a beer with you in real life!

Double Dry hopped Congress Street...can it improve upon an exceptional base beer? It pours a turbid yellowish color. Well-set head is white and frothy. Head leaves bubbly lacing remnants. Nose is dominated by tropical fruit hops and I am far from surprised. Fruit-hop perfume. Mango, pineapple and peaches in the nose. Some mild green onion (which I pick up a lot in Trillium's beer and which I love). Sweet citrus flesh, mango and even some pear. The flavor is dominated, again, by wet, ripe, juicy, fruity, hoppiness. Squishy pineapple and juicy melons. Some grass and soft green onion. Peach and apricot. Fruit smoothie. Pale malt cushion. Dry, not overly bitter finish, yet the bitterness, albeit it modest does linger. Soft carbonation. Hop oils. Almost a cask-esque feel. Trillium's new releases have been an exhilarating experience. Very happy I bought four of these. Long live the brewers in Fort Point!

Enjoyed from a bottle.

woosterbill, Jun 29, 2015
4.49/5  rDev -2.8%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

750ml bottle. Poured into a Cigar City snifter 9/5/2014.

A- Very hazy straw-yellow body. When I say hazy, I mean it. Two finger white head with great hang time. Drops eventually into a thick collar with a thin film on top. Lacing is left throughout. [4.25]

S- Hops galore. It's almost overwhelmingly resinous. At the same time, there is plenty of tropical fruit juiciness on the nose; almost overwhelming the base beer's citrus. Sweet caramel in the background. This smells like a Hill Farmstead beer in the sense that it's clearly hopped up and very resinous with the malt backbone being barely just enough to balance out the hop aromas. [4.5]

F- Excellently hop-forward with a lot of peach and mango up front, citrus in the middle, and a quite bitter pineapple finish. Throughout all of this, the malt backbone offers up a small bit of resistance- sweet caramel and biscuits. [4.5]

M- Medium bodied, soft yet substantial mouthfeel that starts out incredibly bitter and resinous and oily; making you think it was overhopped, if that were possible. Then after a few sips, the bitterness recedes, the finish becomes drier and the juicy hops come forward. [4.5]

O- Excellent IPA that is obviously hopped up but surprisingly seems to evolve over the short time it spends in my glass. Reminds me a lot of a Hill Farmstead pale ale- tons of hop aromas and juiciness with as little pale malt the brewer can get away with and a soft, velvety mouthfeel to boot. Highly recommended. [4.5]

Final score: 4.4875

Stevedore, Sep 06, 2014

Poured from a 750 mL bottle into a willibecher. This was bottled yesterday, and picked up from the brewery an hour ago.

Appearance: Pours a very, hazy, opaque peachy amber, and leaves a couple fingers of white head that fade at a medium pace to a white layer atop the beer (4.6)

Smell: The smell is dominated by big peach, apricot, pineapple, mango and citrus. There is also just a hint of some grassy aromas. (4.9)

Taste: Big citrus (especially grapefruit) take the lead in this one. It's all hops, but is surprisingly un-bitter. There is a bit of pineapple and mango as well, along with a dash of peach. Really juicy and really refreshing. Amazingly smooth finish. (4.9)

Mouth-feel: Medium to well carbonated with a medium, creamy, smooth body. You can almost feel the pulp (semi-joking, because there is no pulp). (4.6)

Overall: This is a unique IPA for me - this captures all the great fruity characteristics of the hops without being overly bitter or sweet. It is refreshing, delicious and very well crafted. (4.9)

Roguer, Mar 25, 2015
4.55/5  rDev -1.5%

Drank from a 25.4 fl oz (750 ml) bottle purchased at Trillium Brewing Company, Boston, MA
Served in a pint glass

Super turbid appearance. The opaque, dull straw yellow liquid was topped by an inch-tall, creamy, frothy, off-white crown. The head quickly faded to a thin, resilient cap. Spotty drops of lacing clung to the glass.

The aroma was outstanding. Super fruity and bright. Very tropical with intense passionfruit notes. Guava was noticeable. Loads of peaches and apricots. Good amount of ruby red grapefruit. Moderately sweet.

The flavor profile was amazingly hoppy and fruity as well, but a bit more pithy and dry than the nose. Super citrusy. Grapefruit dominated. Lots of orange peel. Passionfruit and mango appeared up front. Touch of pinesap on the back end and in the finish. Some bitter, grapefruit rind-like notes came through in the finish as well.

Fantastic mouthfeel. Fluffy, soft and slick on the palate. Ultra creamy and smooth. The liquid contained a super fine, plush effervescence.

Double Dry Hopped Congress Street is a terrific IPA that shows a noticeable improvement over the base beer. Of the recent Trillium limited release bottles (Galaxy Fort Point Pale Ale, Heavy Mettle and this beer), I would probably rate this as my least favorite of the three. That said, it is still better than 99% of IPAs I have ever tried.
