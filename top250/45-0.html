Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: mothman on 03-11-2015

A different beer from previous draft iterations of SR-71. This beer has been confirmed to be the equivalent of Assassin (Barrel Aged Norseman’s Wrath) with more time in the barrels. The fill date mirrors the 2014 Assassin release that was aged in Pappy Van Winkle barrels and is most likely drawn from the exact same batch of base beer.

Thanks to drewone for sharing this one at GRG1313's tasting.

Pours an opaque black with a foamy dark khaki head that settles to wisps of film on top of the beer. Small dots of lace form around the glass on the drink down. Smell is of dark roasted malt, cocoa powder, caramel, toffee, bourbon, and wood aromas. Taste is much the same with cocoa powder, chocolate syrup, earth, char, bourbon, and wood flavors on the finish. There is a mild amount of roast and wood bitterness on the palate with each sip. This beer has a lower level of carbonation with a slightly crisp and medium bodied mouthfeel. Overall, this is a very good beer with a great bourbon barrel aged presence that comes through especially well in the taste.

Serving type: bottle.

My review will mean little in the flurry of ever escalating stout raves and costs of entry for the perceived "top tier." But here's my riff on this beer. Yes, it's excellent. It doesn't quite get the head I'd like, little retention, but it sheets the glass. It's that heavy appearance but doesn't take it to next level pudding solidity like, say, Life is Like. Personally, I like a beer with some head that sticks around. Smell is deep fudge, like distilled brownie batter. Is there really wood presence, barrel, bourbon? Not that I can tell. The nose unifies into one megalithic wave of infinitely dense fudge. Dark enough to almost be savory, a hint of umami / soy that's so subsumed in CHOCOLATE it never becomes off-putting. The flavor? Exactly the same. EXACTLY. What you smell is what you get. No surprises, new nuances, subtlety. It's Platonically true from look to smell to taste to dense / creamy / mouth coating batter feel. Thankfully, it's not intensely sweet. To get this density without tipping into syrup terrain is an accomplishment. So, my critique; it's one note. One very loud, resonant, pretty awesome note ... but it wears you out. It doesn't develop / transform over the glass ... it just bangs the gong over and over again, relentlessly. 6 ounces is enough, 8 is still doable. 10 is too damned much. It's the beer equivalent of classic heavy metal's massive wall of of Marshall amps and a thunderous power chord. It has a power, a purity but at the end of the day there's no real composition. It's just pounding your sense organs and I don't know that it says anything unique. It's not fascinating. Like stealth there's nothing to discover.

cosmicevan, Apr 15, 2015
4.29/5  rDev -8.7%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

Pours a dark black body, much like its namesake; a thick foamy one finger brown head rises quickly, slowly fading into a thin film and collar that retains well. No lacing.

Smells quite a bit like 2014 Assassin did, which is not surprising. Initially, it has a good amount of chocolate fudge and whiskey to it. Sweet bourbon, oak, a fair bit of vanilla. Not a complicated beer, but the chocolate and whiskey aromas are fairly strong and initially are nicely balanced, but as the beer warms up to room temperature, it becomes quite a bit harsh, with more of a charred, dry wood and paint thinner aroma to accompany the whiskey.

Taste starts off with the chocolate fudge, brownie batter, dark chocolate, a touch of dark fruit. The whiskey and vanilla are most present later in the palate, towards the end with a moderate heat on it. Straight-forward barrel-aged stout, a lot of whiskey on it, which isn't too surprising. The whiskey is a bit more subdued and mellow, rather than harsh like Assassin was. There is a bit of dry paint thinner here as well on the finish, which is fairly harsh. I find that I preferred drinking it close to cellar temperature.

Mouthfeel is full bodied, appropriate lower carbonation. Full and quite a bit slick and sticky. Finish is drying dark chocolate and a mellow, but not insubstantial bourbon heat.

This is certainly a very good barrel-aged stout. It's in that conversation after a number of BA stouts, including 2013 Assassin. That being said, it's not as decadent or chocolatey as I would have expected based on 2013 Assassin. It's much closer to 2014 Assassin, so if you loved that one, I don't see any reason you shouldn't love this one.
