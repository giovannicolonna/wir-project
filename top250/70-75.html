Got a bottle of this prior to release about a week ago. I opened it with a group of friends and split it up between a set of 4oz flight flutes after being held around 45 degrees. It poured a dark brown with minimal head. Strong vanilla oak on the nose and possibly maple. Flavor was delicious, surprisingly heavy for a porter, and had similar tasting properties to what was experienced from the scent. This was aged in the same Buffalo Trace PVW barrels that held their Kill Em All Imperial Stout previously.
Definitely worth trying.

Look: Bronzed black.

Smell: Bourbon, Vanilla, Cinnamon, Maple, Chocolate and Earthy Coconut.

Taste: Vanilla and cinnamon up front with maple bourbon warmth, chocolate in the middle and finishes with coconut. Alcohol is well masked for 15%. A bit sweet but not too much.

Feel: A little thin, but it is a porter. The big flavor tricks you into expecting a stout type mouth feel.

Overall: Perrin's best beer. This tops the Malted Milk Ball Porter which I would still drink if it was still around. This one is crazy good right now, but can't wait to get a year on it. It's a special beer that will be remembered for years to come. Too bad distro is so small.
