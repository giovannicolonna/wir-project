On tap at the Surly taproom. 10 oz. pour in a chalice.

Appearance: Color is a bright golden orange. Moderate amount of bubbles and about one finger of white head that settles into a thin creamy layer. Leaves some good lacing.

Smell: A juicy and dank hop forward smell with a big scent of citrus fruits. Big upfront presence of citra hops with hints of passion fruit, orange peel, grapefruit, mango, lemon zest, pineapple, and apricot. Also some floral and earthy hints behind the citrus. Soft pale malt presence with hints of grains, cracker, and yeast. A very good juicy smell.

Taste: Like the nose indicates, a very ripe and juicy hop forward taste with a big presence of citrus fruits. Upfront juicy taste of citra hops with notes of grapefruit, passion fruit, mango, orange peel, lemon zest, pineapple, and apricot. Earthy and floral notes with some pine. Backing of pale malt with notes of biscuit, cracker, and yeast. A really good hoppy taste.

Mouthfeel: Medium bodied with a moderate amount of carbonation. Crisp, creamy, and juicy with a drying finish. Very smooth with no sign of the alcohol.

Overall: Another top notch IPA from Surly. Very citrus dominant with a nice smooth body for such a hoppy brew.

Thanks to Jeff for keeping the hits coming! 7/21/15 can date. 16oz. can into a Carton Boat glass.

Pours a messy, coarse, 1.5 finger white head with moderate retention. Beer is a slightly cloudy orange mango. Lacing is spotty, patchy and thick with good stick. A solid looking beer.

Nose has hints of papaya, mango, citrus and cane sugar. Tropical fruit hop bitterness wafts out of the glass. Really juicy and appetizing!

Opens citrus, papaya and mango. Juicy and very tropical fruit forward in the middle with a touch of cane sugar balanced by a nice, dry, pine hop bitterness. A touch of resin mixed with a grassiness and more fruit in the end and finish. Hoppy, tropical fruit aftertaste. Astounding!

Light bodied with moderate carbonation. Smooth and quenching in the mouth and slightly bubbly and dry going down. Finishes dry and filmy with a long, lingering, bone dry aftertaste. Really nice!

This is certainly the best single-IPA that Surly makes, supplanting Furious as a go-to for sure!

gatornation, Oct 12, 2014
4.29/5  rDev -5.1%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

This brew was served on tap from the upstairs walk-up bar, at the new Surly taproom in Minneapolis, Minnesota. They poured into Surly tulips, with the beer showing through a glowing golden pumpkin coloring. A one finger head of chunky, soapy white bubbles topped the liquid, showing solid retention. Lacing was spotty, and a cheesecloth film formed across the surface of the beer. Heavy haze reduced light and clarity, but no sediment was found. Carbonation appeared to be minimal. Aromatics included saltine crackers, heavy grapefruity and citric hops, evergreen piney hops, sweet caramel and honey sugars, pale, crystal, and wheaty malts, tropical pineapple and passion fruit fruitiness, chlorine, green onion airs, concrete dust, lactic creaminess, raw barley dust, soft amber and wheaty inclusions, light white sugars, dill weed, glue adhesive, and aluminum. With warmth came spicier dried herbal and grassy hop pellets. The flavor followed with cucumber skins, wheaty and Rice Krispie maltiness, big herbal, piney, and hearty citric hoppy blend, green bell peppers, fresh green twigginess and wet mossiness, vegetal celery stalk and parsnip root, potato starchiness, raw organic oregano spiciness, corny malty vibe, medicinal phenols, clean pale malt toast, simple raw caramel graininess, and clay-like mineral. The body was medium, and carbonation was a bit lighter. Nice slurp, smack, cream, froth, and crisp finishing pop enhanced each sip. The mouth was left chalked and astringently dried following a flash flood of wet and drippy creamed coating. The abv was appropriate, and the beer disappeared super easily.

Overall, this is advertised as their “west coast” bitter IPA. We felt this to be a bit of a stretch, according to the humble opinions of our collective palate. Much of that dense, labelling bitterness of the hops was subdued and balanced by booze, and what were nearly cloyingly sweet, residually sugared graininess. That said, this was one of the most pine-forward hoppy beers we’ve had in a long time. If you’re not sure if the hops you’re tasting are piney, compare to this beer, and you’ll have your answer. This was enjoyed immediately following our first glasses of Abrasive, so as a direct comparison, Todd was not as robust, but did show some similarities in their skeletal makeups. We try not to do side-by-sides like that per se, but we were just so excited to have all these heavy hitting Surly beverages at our fingertips. Regardless, this was a very pleasurable experience, and we are glad it came back onto the taplist for our return visit to Minneapolis.
