Poured on tap into a snifter

Appearance: This brew was a beautiful hazy golden orange color with a lingering white head. Nice sticky lacing clung to the side to the glass as I drank.

Smell: Loved the aroma on this one. Tons of ripe fruit, citrus, some pine, and some earthy dankness. I absolutely love the Citra and Mosaic combo, they go very well together. Faint sweet malt and yeast esters linger just under the wall of hops.

Taste: Absolutely awesome! There's a nice bitterness up front, but not aggressive. Tons of citrus flavor, more orange to me than grapefruit. Some pineapple and mango too. The Golden Promise malt lends a nice sweetness to underlie the hop flavors.

Mouthfeel. Medium body and carbonation. This beer has an almost creamy texture to it.

Overall: One of the best standard IPAs I've ever had, reminded me a bit of Focal Banger, though its been a while since I've had that. IPAs just don't really get any better than this. It's everything I want in the style.

16oz can from IAmJacksHopTongue (thanks!). Canned 7/31/15. Served in a goblet.

Pours cloudy orange with two and a half fingers pale head. Pale floaties hang in suspension throughout the body. The wide, persistent collar leaves a good deal of clumpy lacing.

Bright citrus aromas. Orange, tangerine, peach, grapefruit, melon and a hint of sweet malt. Outstanding.

Bittersweet citrus and tropical hops rule the flavor. Grapefruit, orange peel, mango, peach, pine, pepper and a little cracker. Delightfully balanced and incredibly easy to drink.

Slick, medium feel with moderate carbonation. Finishes with a bitter yet flavorful blend of citrus and tropical hops.

Todd The Axe Man is a damned tasty, distinctive IPA with a good mix of hop flavor and bitterness. I'm happy to have finally had the chance to try this much lauded IPA.

10 oz goblet at the brewery.
Served with a finger of white head that recedes to a thin layer on top of a clear golden brew. Doesn't have the orange hue that so many of these IPAs do.
Aroma is more mosaic than citra-not as dank. Plenty of tropical notes-passion fruit and pineapple with some orange on top.
Taste is beautiful. Hops for sure but the malt is present and carries the tropical and citrus flavors of the hops so nicely.
Mouthfeel is where this shines. Creamy not resiny. Clean yet hoppy finish. With an ABV over 7% this isn't boozy at all.
Overall this reminds me of Deschutes Fresh Squeezed with more malt body and a cleaner finish. I love fresh squeezed but if Surly canned this I would have this in my beer fridge a lot. Well done!

7/31/15 canning date. 16 oz. can.

Pours a cloudy dark orange color with about a finger of bright white head. Some yeast floaties in the beer.

Smells of extremely dank hops. One of the dankest beers I've ever smelled. Also get pineapple, mango, papaya, orange juice, and grapefruit. All the fruits are very sweet and juicy. Also get a bit of pine and chive.

Taste is a complex assortment of juicy tropical and citrus fruits and dankness. Some of that chive makes an appearance as well. A wonderful dry finish with a significant citrus pith and pine resin bitterness.

Medium body. Slightly crisp and creamy. No carbonation issues here.

This beer is extremely hyped up and for the most part, it lives up to the hype. Really shows what intensity and complexity Citra and Mosaic can have. One of the better, if not the best, single IPA's coming out of the Midwest right now.
