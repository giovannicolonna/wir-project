Had on draft and out of a growler.

S: Smells like coffee, chocolate and toasted coconut, all well-balanced and working in near-perfect unison.

T: Taste is a delicious blend of coffe, toast, chocolate and toasted coconut, reminiscent of any one of a dozen amazing coconut and chocolate desserts. Toasted coconut lingers pleasantly on the palate after each pull and for a while after finishing this beer.

M: A typical heft for an American porter, medium carbonation helps those amazing flavors dance around.

O: If you don't like coconut flavor this isn't the beer for you, but this is a fantastic-tasting beer that I could drink for dessert over and over. Manageable alcohol, delicious flavors, heady aromas and a nice body make this one of the most enjoyable porters I've had. Time to stock up!

1L growler courtesy of buddhabelly. Served into a HF wine glass at 40F.

reviewed with the old scoring system.

A: 4.75. dark brown body with 1.5" mocha head that is smooth and full. Fine carbonation with minor lacing on the glass. Gentle swirling reinvigorates about half of the head.

S: 4.75 Coconut. Cracking the growler, you can smell this 4-5 feet away easily. Amazing rich chocolate, coffee and caramel notes. No hint of hops or malt. One of the bet smelling beers I've ever had the privilege to taste.

T: 4.75 Just like the smell. Wafting tones of roasted coffee, fresh coconut and chocolate. Clean with no lingering aftertaste aside from coconut...which is fantastic. Like a liquid Mounds candy bar. Slightly acidic, but otherwise amazingly well balanced.

M: 4.50 A touch heavy. Slightly oily, but still pleasant. Carbonation is correct, but the nature of adjuncts in this beer will always leave a bit or a rough finish.

O: 4.75 One of the most interesting beers I've tried, but Funky Buddha aspires to just that. Glad my wife was here to share as more than a glass may be too much flavor, but this is definitely worth seeking out.

cubanbeerguy, Apr 23, 2013
4.59/5  rDev +2.7%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.75

Opened the bottle and immediately received a wisp of the aroma of coconut and the sweet smell of vanilla.
This bottle is a couple of months old – probably five from the spring 2015 release of last snow. The coconut, vanilla and coffee still had staying power.

Visually it fits the beer profile for an American Porter although slightly thinner on the pour than I have seen of other porters. Once in the glass it is beautiful to behold. Pours thin buts sits stoutly in the glass exhibiting a malty, dark
character which does not allow you to see through the glass. Looking overtop the coloring is black, dark brown with ruby highlights. The aroma prefaces the complexity of this beer with coffee, toffee, and light chocolate, sweet vanilla and coconut flavors. This beer is complex and emanates these aromas so take your time with it. Smell it and look it at to indulge your senses. Mouthfeel is thin but makes up for it with the complexity of the flavors matching the aroma. Moderately sweet balanced with a slightly bitter coffee flavor. Sharp in taste, just slightly dry with a bready finish and coconut taste that lingers on the back of the tongue. What an outstanding balance and contrast of flavors at the same time (sweet vanilla against the coffee, dry to malty flavoring, bready against the cocoanut fruit finish). My notes read that the coffee could have been slightly stronger. Someone will have to send me a bottle to validate that. Wait, further in my notes the coffee flavor seemed to become more pronounced as the beer warmed. 6.4 % ABV is deceiving for this beer as it is a very smooth and easy beer to drink.

T- SMOOTH, coconut heavy but not overpowering. Balances perfectly with chocolate, coffee and toffee notes. Very sweet on the finish

M - lightly carbonated, just right for its ABV (many porters 5-6 % abv tend to be thin.). This is just right. About as good of a consistency and viscosity as you can get for the style.
