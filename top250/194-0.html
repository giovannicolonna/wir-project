Bottle. Ahhh, another take on what is one of my favorite beers ever. Pours a hazy, dull orange hue. A frothy collar of tinted white head. Lots of lacing speckles. The excellent aroma of Fort Point meets the distinctness of Mosaic hops. Tropical fruits du jour. Peach, pineapple, tangerine, and the like. Mosaic adds extra fruit and a herbal, softly oniony and piney element. He best part is that it's still Fort Point at heart. Flavor explodes with hop-fruit-juice. Juicy and juicy! About 7 different types of citrus, peach, nectarine, apricot, nectarine, and hop-herb. Pale, pastry malt brings the balance that makes this beer one of my favorite before all the fancy dry-hopping. Bitterness isn't overwhelming. Feel has some hop resin and a very full, creaminess for its size. Softer bubbles. Fort Point, regardless of if it's quintuple dry-hopped or just plain and basic is a fantastic beer. I refuse to stop imbibing this beer in all its forms.

The beer pours a tarnished brass color topped by a sticky, foamy white head that lingers throughout drinking. There is some sediment, but no large flocs. The nose is a mix of pineapple, pine needles, grain, and some booze - there's a huge hop presence, but it's not a shovel full of hops to the face. The taste is similar with juicy hops upfront, but the grain is definitely starting to creep up on this 1/17 bottling. At this point, the yeast and grain presence plays well with the tropical fruit hop profile. Additionally, the beer stays well carbonated throughout drinking and has appropriate level of bitterness to balance out the malt base. I'd describe this beer has having a medium body, slight chewiness from the yeast, but minimal alcohol, and a semi-sweet and bitter finish. It's definitely a tasty APA, worth trying and sharing, but didn't quite have the crushability I was hoping for.

pmarlowe, May 01, 2015
4.59/5  rDev +0.7%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Drank from a 25.4 fl oz (750 ml) bottle purchased at Trillium Brewing Company, Boston, MA
Bottled 011715
Served in a pint glass

Super cloudy, yellow ochre appearance. An inch-tall, tight and bubbly, off white crown topped the liquid and faded to a thick, frothy cap. Drippy rings of lacing coated the top portion of the glass.

The aroma was super hoppy and fruity. Tropical fruits were quite apparent. Loads of passionfruit and guava. Dank, herbal, resiny, weed-like notes came through strong. Orange zest was noticeable. Perhaps some fresh orange or tangerine juice as well. Really crisp and bright. Subtle peach and apricot notes were also detectible.

Intense juicy and fruity hops dominated in the flavor profile as well. Peach, apricot and mango came through strong. Touch of pineapple. Zippy and spicy. Tart, zesty, lemony notes appeared mid-palate, lending the beer a nice brightness. Candied orange peel was noticeable in the ever-so-slightly bitter finish. Crisp, lemon notes lingered in the aftertaste.

The mouthfeel was super slick and silky smooth. Creamy and fluffy on the palate. A fine, subtle effervesce gave the liquid a nice suppleness.

It seems like every other week Trillium Brewing puts out another variation of their soon-to-be iconic pale ales, IPAs and double IPAs. Although this Mosaic Dry Hopped Fort Point release was incredibly flavorful and dangerously drinkabke, it is definitely a half step behind their galaxy and double dry hopped versions. Nevertheless, all of these beers are exceptional and worth seeking out.
