Look-definitely the beer's least impressive feature, color is a very cloudy orangish yellow, with a brown tinge. Pours two nice fingers of white, frothy head that retains well, leaving nice chunks of lacing on the sides of the glass.
Smell-Wow. A burst of complex, hoppy goodness. Grapefruit, tangerines, nectarines, mango, and a strong, distinct, tropical presence that i thought to be very similar to guava. Then comes a punch of floral and earthier hop notes, Definitely a little malt in there on the back end, supporting the bright hops.
Taste-Like the aroma, waves of hops, the tropical and citrusy notes are balanced by more hop notes of pine and grass, the malt is particularly sweet, almost caramel like, but very subtle, just cutting off the hops to crossing over from intensely floral and citrusy to bitter. Amazing Balance.
Feel-Body is very heavy, but not oily or even creamy, the intense carbonation keeps things refreshing, and the beer finishes juicy.
Overall-Review says it all, this one to me is right up there with Heady Topper and Pliney Elder in the the league of world class hoppy brews.cheers
