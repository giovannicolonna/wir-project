Perrin No Rules Vietnamese Porter

Serious appreciation to Beergorilla for the opportunity to try this beer.

A: Thick, opaque dark brown to a lighter brown cola color as my glass empties. A light brown head that disappears. Minor amount of lacing, but what's there is syrupy.

S: Vanilla, coffee, bourbon, dates and a touch of cinnamon.

T: Vanilla, coffee, bourbon, with sweet coconut on the finish. A bit of woody oak and nut on the finish. Spectacular.

M: Low carbonation. Slick. Makes my lips sticky.

D: At 15% you could not find yourself a more drinkable beer. It's super smooth, there's no unwanted bitterness, but it's not overly sweet. Big bold flavors.

Lazhal, Aug 07, 2015
5/5  rDev +10.4%
look: 5 | smell: 5 | taste: 5 | feel: 5 | overall: 5

From 22oz bottle in a snifter.

L: extremely dark brown with thin khaki head. Minimal lacing, no carbonation.

S: strong bourbon, cinnamon and vanilla. Little nutty, maybe some carmalized brown sugar. There some whiff of sweet cacao in there. You can expect this going to be very sweet. Once I warms up the maple becomes dominate.

T: sweet but not too sweet. Perrin did a great job here on balancing all the flavors together. This is a very complex and strong beer that's hard not to drink fast. Everything goes together very well. The vanilla and cinnamon taste is stronger than the coconut which is very hidden and only detectable when it warms up.

F: surprisingly thin, but makes sense for a porter, which in this case is kind of arguably misleading. Heats show up in the finish after this beer warms up.

O: This is a magnificent beer. One of the best BA beers I had. Completely understand the hype when an already great brewery makes a world class stout, which brings me to my next point on the classification of this beer. First of all, calling this an imperial porter is like calling an ipa an imperial pale ale. Historically there was a style called "stout porter" for strong porters which became known now as stouts. I think the reason behind perrin naming this a porter is because it's easier to be called the best porter than the best stout, especially in a state that makes kbs, cbs, black note and plead the 5th. Nevertheless, this beer is going to be one of the most sought after beer in the few next year. It's already hard to get and sold out at the brewery under one hour.

No Rules Vietnamese Porter. Imperial porter aged in Heaven Hill and Four Roses bourbon barrels for nine months with coconut, cinnamon, and raw cane sugar, then blended with maple syrup post-barrel. Have had this on draft and out of the bottles as well, there's no discernible difference that I'm able to pick up.

Appearance: Black as black can be.

Smell: Sweet coconut, booze, a bit of cinnamon.

Taste: Coconut, cinnamon, and sticky maple syrup. Booze comes through in flavor only, there's no burn to this beast.

Mouthfeel: My only criticism is it's a tad thinner than I typically like my BA stouts (yes, I know it's a "porter", but Imperial Porter = Stout, if we're being honest), but it's a personal preference thing and nothing against the way it was made.

Overall: A stunningly complex beer with layers upon layers of flavor that finishes soft, smooth, and zero alcohol burn for being 15%. Wow.

Take a back seat, coffee, because this beer is driven by strong, distinct notes of cinnamon hearts, bourbon, candied pecans, maple, brown sugar, gingerbread, cookie dough, raisin, Fig Newtons and coconut. And no - I'm not referring to subtle nuances, nor am I throwing around fanciful descriptor words just to beef up this review or hype up this beer. This beer deserves any and all hype it is given, and those flavours are all there.

Were I to dream up "the perfect beer", it would be this one - though perhaps with a more full-bodied, sticky mouthfeel. That said, this is a porter, not an imperial stout, and so its lighter body might be expected.

If you're looking for a thick, creamy beer, move along. It's the complex, layered flavour profile that makes this masterful offering from Perrin a standout beer that is well worth seeking out.

I guarantee you will not be disappointed.

After Perrins opening I had high hopes but their beers just didn't deliver. The complete package is something really unique that I don't think I've had before in a BA stout. The mentioning of this in the same league as CBS and BCBS doesn't seem like overkill to me. Really hoping this is a sign of more great things to come at Perrin.
