BoldRulerVT, Jun 21, 2014
4.77/5  rDev +8.4%
look: 4.5 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 5 | overall: 5

Thanks to Zach for the trade! Poured from the growler into my brand new FFF Teku tulip.

A - Pours a cloudy yellow-orange, with a half a fingers worth of pale white head. The head dissolves somewhat quickly into a wisp of white dust on the top of the glass along with a ring around the outer edges of the glass. Head retention is nice from this point, and it seems to cling to the sides of the glass after every sip.

S - WHAM HOPS! Citrus and grass dominate the nose in an exceptionally pungent blend. Incredible nose from the moment I popped the swingtop. Nice vanilla notes in the backdrop as well, complimenting the citrus with a clean finish. Really an exceptional scent to it.

T - Follows nicely from the nose. Very tropical fruit/citrus hop up front, with a nice vanilla malt background flavor. My only complaint is that it is not quite as intense as the nose, but it is still an incredibly well done beer.

M - Dry enough finish to clean the palate, but not too dry to leave me thirsty with every sip. Obviously very little/no alcohol burn with this one, but still pretty incredible how smooth it goes down.

O - Overall, wow, this is the first HF hoppy offering I've traded for, and I honestly couldn't be happier. What an exceptionally balanced pale ale, and one I hope to enjoy again!

poured from a 750 ml growler into a hill farmstead stemmed glass.

A - hazy pale gold with fluffly white head that bubbles off quickly but leaves good lacing on the glass.

S - somewhat faint and unmistakably citrusy. the hop aroma seems to develop over time in the glass, and i got more fresh pine towards the end. befitting its origins, it smells extremely fresh.

T - big citrus, transitioning into a bright, hoppy bitterness. serious citrus zing with each swallow. particularly lemony, in my opinion. finishes off with a pleasant yeast note before you're left with the hops as a lingering aftertaste. maintains some of the ultra-dry, hoppy qualities of west coast ipas but this is a more mellow pour.

M - light, crisp body and perfectly carbonated.

O - like some other hf beers, enlightenment is elegant to the point of confounding. rather than going after big flavors, this is a beer about balance and creating a completely unified sip, from smell to swallow. it took me most of a growler to realize that it's a stunner.

750mL growler filled at the brewery 6/5/13, poured into a HF stemmed snifter (oversized wine glass) just now, 6/9.

A. Golden orange. Dense haze. The brightness of the orange hue belies the thick haze, it really looks quite radiant. White bubbly head, fades quickly to a light cap. Lacing is minimal, but these head retention issues are my fault for saving this for last. I'm very sad I don't have more beer from this brewery. Well... there is prolegomena! I digress.

S. Piney bright citrus. Orange, grapefruit, resin, and something much softer, like a white wine grape aroma.

T. Ah the taste. What a nice taste. Light tangerine and grapefruit quality citrus up front, with a hearty citrusy bitterness in the middle to end, finalized with the lightness of a gooseberry paired with a hint of grapefruit.

M. Very light. Borders watery but doesn't cross the line, making it quite sessionable. The slightly lower crispness I am finding is likely due to the length of time in the growler, but it is still a fresh and bright feeling.

O. A beer to make a night out of. Very tasty, very light, but with just enough substance. Another fantastic offering from Hill Farmstead.
