Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: MichaelAllenVT on 04-03-2014

This lupulin-ladin India Pale Ale is packed with juicy tropical fruit character, bright floral aromas and delectable layers of hop flavor. Pour mindfully, inhale deeply and enjoy a tropical vacation in a glass. Always store cold, enjoy fresh and stay cool!
Sip of Sunshine IPA is brewed by Lawson’s Finest Liquids at Two Roads Brewing Co. in Stratford CT.

1 month old can, drinking very well

A - Hazy orange wheat body. Fine bone white head pours a finger high with nice retation and lace.

S - Sweet, fruity nose. Orange and grapefruit with a really nice soft nectarine center, with edges of sage, fresh bread crust, and a touch of booze.

T - Huge, straight up concentrated hop flavor. The hop character is very raw and tastes almost straight off the vine, which on net is great but I'm a little ambivalent about. The effect is big, herbal, and woody but at the same time almost astringent. A touch of white bread, orange pith, and honey serve as a back drop but for the most part stay out of the way.

M - Medium body for a DIPA, medium-full for an IPA. Pleasant carbonation.

O - A beer with a strong, almost aggressive, hop quality. Based on the body, flavor profile, and to some degree the ABV I'd be more inclined to call this a DIPA than an IPA.

pint can. poured into a spiegelau ipa glass. Dated 03.20.15. consumed 04.15.15.

A: Tarnished orange, generally good clarity, with a vibrant rush of macro-effervesce filling the vessel. Stoic 2 inch bone white head, soapy large air pockets along the vessel walls, with a mountainious rising centerpiece, rigid and firm. Stratified ring lacing.

S: Tropical freshness, wet mango, pineapple-orange with fresh lemon cream. Canned peach juice and a hint of fresh lime.

T: Flavor started out with some grassy tropical fruitiness, slightly tart pineapple and lemon, and citrusy peach. Big burst of lightly dry orange finishing with a dry floral hop burst or orange, pine, and honey suckle flower.

M: Light bready malt. Decent bite of crispness, medium bodie, lightly creamy textured.

O: Insane drinkability, tropical hopping perfect summer time quencher. Only a slight hint of the 8.0% which otherwise drinks like session material due to the light tropical fruity nature. Big wet tropical fruitiness that finishes with a warming of citrusy-pine hop flowers. Sip of sunshine is a sip of heaven. case worthy.

cpetrone84, Dec 30, 2014

Tall boy can poured into HF wine stem.

canned on 07/10/2015.

Appearance: Golden yellow body with an orange hue. 3 fingers of bone white head that has good head retention and leaves spotty lacing all over the glass. Eventually the beer drops down to a thin cap.

Smell: bright citrus and tropical fruits are the main theme in the aroma department. More specifically in the citrus profile you have a sweet tangerine and tart grapefruit; mango, papaya, and pineapple compromise the tropical fruit nose. This smells outstanding and I will admit that I had my nose in the glass the whole time I drank this beer.

Taste: Upfront moderate biterness level that just fades away pleasantly after each sip without the bitter bite becoming stronger which of course begs the drinker to take another sip immediately after swallowing the last. I prefer the aroma to the taste as the nose is more complex and interesting, but no question this IPA is quite delicious non the less. Hops are citrusy, tropical and even slightly floral. Lots of sweet orange and tropical fruits provide the bulk of the hoppy profile found in this beer. This beer is massively hoppy, but you are not blasted with crazy bitterness that is found in most IPA's. The floral flavors come in near the end of each sip and provide a slightly dry taste to the finish; mixed with some orange zest that makes this beer taste crisp and clean with juicy and sweet hop flavors on the front end and in the middle of the beer. Malt profile is bready with a little additional sweetness that balances out all those hops pretty nice.

Mouthfeel: medium bodied beer with moderate carbonation levels. The carbonation is dialed in pretty well and abv is well hidden when this beer is cold, but as it warms there is an ever so slight sweet and boozey sting. It is still pretty easy to crush the sixteen ounces from that tall boy can.

Overall: Sip of Sunshine is a great IPA. Lawson's make some fantastic hoppy beers. Double Sunshine still rules the roost as the very best hoppy beer that Lawson's brews. Bonus points for this beer getting packaged in tallboy cans; that is always great to see. I love the fact that I can't stop burping oranges after drinking this beer.

650ml bottle poured into a Lawson's snifter 9/3/2014.

A- Pours a cloudy lighter copper/dark orange body with a two finger foamy white head that briefly resembles freshly steamed milk that's just about to get poured to make a cappuccino. Very thick, frothy head, very impressive. Rings of lacing keep the glass from looking too clean. [4.25]

S- Tropical fruit, mango, grapefruit, floral notes, even a bit of pine. This IPA has everything, including a nice caramel malt backbone. This is very pleasing to inhale, and you don't have to inhale much to feel the full force of the hop aromas. Phenomenal nose. [4.75]

F- Plenty of tropical fruit and grapefruit up front and throughout; there isn't as much of the floral notes from the nose however. Sweet caramel and pale malt backbone that is clean with no graininess to be found; perfectly balances the hop aromas. It's very similar to Focal Banger in a lot of ways. Overall the flavour profile is very hop-forward but with a perfect amount of pale malt to back it up. [4.5]

M- That Vermont water is really doing some magic. Substantial body, on the fuller side of medium in body, with a nice soft feel that combines very well with what seems to be dialed in carbonation to make for a smooth but powerful drinking experience. A bitter resinous pine-caramel combination finishes things out, yet leaves the palate dry with tons of lingering juicy grapefruit flavours. Phenomenal. [4.75]

O- I don't get how Lawson's can dial in their IPAs so well. This is easily up there with The Alchemist's Focal Banger in terms of the most juicy, drinkable IPAs. This packs a massive aromatic right cross, and follows up with a left hook full of fruity flavours on the palate. Great stuff, I have to wonder if there's any recipe similarities between this one and Double Sunshine. [4.75]

Final score: 4.6125
