Pours a thick opaque black with minimal head. The nose starts with candied apple. Of all of the apple brandy aged beers I have had, this has the most pronounced apple character. The apples are melded with a nice caramel and brandy note and followed up by a thick and delicious fudge. The fudge is very heavy on the end and lingers nicely. The taste matches the progression of the nose. Apple, brandy, caramel, and fudge. The finish is where this beer shines... nice complex notes of barrel char, chocolate, brownie batter, and apple just dance on the palate long after the sip. This beer was a pleasant surprise. I've enjoyed some of the other Noir variants and none of them wowed me like this. Phenomenal.

The beer pours a deep black with a dull tan halo of head around the perimeter. The nose is a potent mixture of tart apples, caramel, wood, chocolate and alcohol - it seems to scream apple brandy as it warms. Similarly, the AB barrel cuts right the malt base, to the point of inundating the palate with bright apples and spicy wood, followed by some malty marshmallow-y sweetness in the finish. It's thick and chewy, saturated with barrel character, mildly carbonated, and does a reasonable job hiding the 12%abv. I prefer the wine barrel noir, but this beer's a very close second.

12oz bottle poured into a teku glass

A- pours midnight black with a small creamy dark tan head that retains long after the pour, leaving some thick patches of lacing behind and eventually settling to a think layer floating on top, beautiful looking beer

S- the aroma of a caramel candied apple pops out of the glass with a deep melted caramel sweetness, and a slight tart aroma of apple skin poking through. Beyond that, notes of tiramisu, brandy, vanilla, oak, butterscotch, and baked cinnamon apples come through. Just a hint of alcohol on the backend is the only thing deterring this from a perfect aroma

T- the fruity apple pops up immediately upon tasting with a flavor of red apple skin. This is quickly followed by sweet melted caramel with undertones of chocolate. The brandy kicks in mid-palate along with hints of coffee and whipping cream, the finish gives more caramel with a linger of apple & a hint of alcohol

M- medium thick body with low carbonation leads to a creamy mouthfeel that continues into a silky smooth finish, staying creamy and smooth throughout, some alcohol warmth on the linger

O- a sipper that is certainly a treat to drink, the taste isn't as complex as the aroma, but the barrel aging adds a big complexity to an already solid base beer, well worth seeking out

12 ounce bottle into a snifter.

I'm loving the frothiness on this beer's head. It's chewy looking and looks like something I could make a meal of. I also like the dark khaki color and the wall of lace the receding leaves behind. Pitch black body looks like it'll offer some flavor. 5

I wasn't expecting that nose. That's definitely apple brandy. Impressive how the apple is perceivable. Brandy notes are sticky and a little bit astringent. Rich dark chocolate layers over this. The combination reminds me slightly of root beer or cream soda. 4

Apple brandy and dark chocolate are just as prominent on the palate as on the nose. A strong current of vanilla adds some sweetness and smooths the flavor out a bit. Mid-palate tastes a bit like a toasted coconut donut. The brandy is so pronounced it seems like I poured some from a bottle myself into my stout rather than having it actually aged in a brandy barrel. Interestingly enough, as I get deeper in, the beer becomes a bit more smoky/peaty like it was whiskey-barrel aged. I like this dimension of the beer though and the beer overall is really growing on me. 4.25

It's a tad hot given that it does feel like the brandy's been blended in. Mouthfeel is thick, semi-viscous and a bit bitter and solventy on the finish. 4

There's a lot to like about this beer but it also is a bit rough around the edges.

12oz bottle from LambicPentameter (many thanks!). Bottled 1/20/15. Served in a Barrel and Barley teku.

Pours black with amlost a full finger of khaki head. The froth recedes to a thin halo than leaves only a few small spots of lace.

Big, rich nose of apple brandy. Cocoa, caramel, a fruity sweetness and hints of honey. I keep coming back to enjoy that terrific brandy aroma.

Again in the taste that apple brandy is way up front along with bold dark chocolate. Coffee, fruit skin, caramel, brown sugar, oak, subtle earth and a dollop of alcohol. This is all about the barrel and I love it. The booze is understated for its 12% abv and the base stout seems to provide a chocolatey playground for all of that oaky apple brandy goodness.

The body is on the fuller side of medium with carbonation that borders on overly rambunctious. Apple brandy dominance continues in the long aftertaste with complimentary notes of chocolate, oak and caramel.

Apple Brandy Noir is a beautiful showcase for the barrel. It comes across as a dark chocolate filled with brandy and a hint of coffee. This is far and away my favorite of Prairie's Noirs that I've tried. Outstanding and worth a little effort to obtain.

One of my favorite stouts (so far) from Prairie. Nose is warm and inviting, with notes of molasses, fudge, wet oak, and - surprisingly - a heavy amount of stewed apples. I didn't expect such a prominent fruit aroma from the calvados, but it's there in force. On the palate, the brandy is also front-and-center, but with less of that fresh apple character; the flavors come across more as a rounded, steady blend of ashy barrel wood, vanilla cream frosting, caramel, and baked, thick-skinned apples. Each sip is wrapped up in the sweet, sticky booziness you can only get with brandy, with the mouthfeel to match. Incredible; the barrel-aging really has changed this beer to its core. Now it's absolutely dripping with apple brandy flavor.

Indisputable evidence that the barrel can certainly make the beer - I've had Wine Barrel Noir and this is in a completely different ballpark. Bravo Prairie!

larryarms847, Feb 13, 2015
4.51/5  rDev +3.4%
look: 4.25 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Poured from a bottle into a Wicked Weed tulip. Pours out very black and opaque, a bit viscous, and fairly low in carbonation with about a finger of brown foamy head. Very interesting nose on this beer. Not sure I've come across another quite like it. Lots of dark semisweet chocolate, burnt oak, a hint of espresso, and a nice rich boozy, rummy note.

The beer has a full body, a very round, fat feel, and a roasty, boozy, complex flavor. It opens up with sweet, earthy molasses, brown bread, and burnt caramel flavors. Then, hints of booze, toasted oak, espresso, mocha, and a hint of smoke develop across the palate. Port wine flavors, dark cherries, and figs show up on the back end. It finishes well with a mild, warming bit of alcohol - just enough to volatilize the beer and evaporate all those wonderful subtle roasty, sugary, fruity, and oaky flavors, bringing them back around for a nice finale. The oak and brown sugar notes stick in your palate for a short while.

Wow, what a beer. Perhaps not one that I would choose to drink repeatedly, but definitely one that is worth visiting. The complexity of the flavor is wonderful. I can definitely see why some BAs really love it. I don't think I love it, but I do like it - a lot. The complexity really draws you in and challenges you. I like that in a beer and really savor those beers that do it, even if it's not one that suits my palate perfectly. I hope I get to revisit this one again someday. Highly recommended.
