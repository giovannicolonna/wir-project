This should be another fun one. I like the talk of some Citra action. Let's take a look-see. 16 oz. can into a tulip.

A - A beautiful deep, rich, hazy orange. Half a finger of off-white head, with small bubbles. Plenty of carbonation rising to the top of the glass. Pretty!

N - I could smell it even while holding the glass up to the light. TONS of orange complemented by grapefruit. I get virtually no hop bitterness at all. This is amazing.

T - There it is. Too bitter for me, but still very tasty. Lots of pine, not nearly as much citrus as I expected from the nose. Perhaps it's too much mosaic and not enough Citra for me. Very well-crafted, however.

M - Nice and frothy. From swirling it around for a second or two the whole thing becomes froth, and I always find that impressive. This is really a well-made beer.

O - Not my favorite from Surly, but extremely good stuff.

beersdmf17, Sep 24, 2015
4.5/5  rDev -0.4%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.5

Poured from a glorious pounder can into a Teku glass.

L: Pours a dark orangy yellow that is a breathtaking golden orange when held to the light. There was about a finger or 2 of white head that disappeared into a ring around the glass.

S: Smells pretty damn dank. Mosaic and Citra hops are an incredible combination and I've been waiting for a brewery to do this. Strong orange, peach, and papaya notes are what I smell immediately. Some grapefruit also comes through in addition to a little bit of pine and even some mango. Man the depth of smell is wonderful.

T: Pun intended, this is a bit abrasive, but not really (if that makes sense.) The taste follows the nose pretty well. The zesty orange and peach come through immediately with the grapefruit, mango and papaya notes coming through shortly after. This is damn good. There is a nice amount of bitterness, but it's not overbearing at all.

F: This has a supreme amount of flavor for a beer that is 7.2%. The carbonation is prickly, but it works with the beer very well. The alcohol is not present, but I don't think I could have more of one of these in a sitting. I can't say that for other IPAs that even go up to 8%.

Overall: This is a really nice beer. I am quite spoiled with all the wonderful IPAs that are available to me in New England, but I am glad that I was able to see what Minnesota has to offer. The bitterness is nice, as well as the flavor. I am a sucker for citra and mosaic hops and to see them combined in an IPA is something that I've been waiting for. This is my first beer from Surly and I'd love to try some other IPAs that they have. Especially if they're as good as this one.

markcp, Nov 22, 2014
4.54/5  rDev +0.4%
look: 3.25 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 4.75

Canned on 7/16/15.

Appearance: Awesome can art. The beer is medium caramel in color with some clarity and also hop sediment. Poured a dense off-white head that quickly dissipated. Looks quite different than when I've had it on tap, darker and tepid looking from the can.

Aroma: Resinous pine and that signature citra funky mango. Smelled the most explosive right after I cracked the can open.

Taste: Huge bitterness throughout paired with soft caramel notes from the malt and a bouquet of hop flavor. I'm not sure the ratio of mosaic to citra or additions but I'd bet on a large quantities of finishing hops. While the obvious comparison is to that of Abrasive, the main difference is less perceived sweetness and more raw bitterness. The hop flavor ranges from resiny to juicy fruit gum. The finish is smooth followed by a last burst of bitterness that fades in the aftertaste while bringing back some of the fruitier hop notes. If I didn't know that this was 7.2% ABV it would be difficult to guess. While it doesn't feel like a big beer there is a heat on the back end that pairs nicely with the dying bitterness. Another excellent beer by Surly.

Mouthfeel: Just above medium in body. I always find it to be over carbonated on tap, but it has about average carbonation for the style when given a healthy pour from the can. Good drinkability, but it's hard to resist the flavor so that's a given.

Final Thoughts: I was very surprised and happy to see this on the shelves. The $16.99 price tag is a bit much but I understand citra hops are not cheap. I will say that the appearance and mouthfeel leave a little to be desired but the flavor and aroma speak for themselves. I assume it will have rotating availability so I look forward to each new batch. Would I drink it again? Yep.
