Huge thanks to rudzud for the epic share.

Pours a mild fizzy darker colored head that fades quickly, nice long legs, almost pitch black color.

Nose is very dense and complex, dark choc almost fudgey, hint of nutty toffee and caramel, big vanilla bourbon, bourbon barrel, and hints of nougat, hint of candied raisin, the nutty aroma blends into a mild smooth coffee, nutty slight earthy smooth coffee, then suddenly a huge maple candy, back into cocoa fudge and coconut, then an earthy nutty Vietnamese coffee with a creamy iced coffee thing.

Taste brings big layered flavours again, different chocolates, creamy fudgey cocoa, with bakers choc, and creamy choc inside a lindor truffle. Into nice bourbon barrel juice, vanilla barrel, nice vanilla and some coconut as well. Hint bitter roast, touch earthy hop, creamy milk choc. Then coffee, mild smooth nutty coffee, Vietnamese like coffee I think. Then big maple flavor, maple cream. Nice caramel and nutty toffee. Hint bitter roast later, mild sweetness on the finish with tons of maple lingering, caramel, nice coconut and cocoa, vanilla barrel and bourbon lingering. Semi sweet.

Mouth is on the heavier side, fuller, little syrupy, hint of booze, decent but lighter carb.

Overall very nice, little sweet and syrupy, tons of nice maple, great bourbon barrel and caramel candy, etc, mild coffee wish it was a bit more, cocoa was nice but wish it was more too, but I'm nitpicking, it was an awesome beer.

12oz bottle poured into an oversized Funky Buddha stemmed goblet.

Pours the expected deep brown though the nice, wispy mocha-tan head that rings the glass was a nice touch, and an unexpected one. So...made with coconut eh? My favorite "dark beer" adjunct, so let's see what this nose is all about. Gadzooks! An overwhelmingly dominant coconut presence, they weren't lying! Liquid Almond Joy poured into a vanilla sugar cookie and then soaked overnight in bourbon barrels. Did I mention coconut? and vanilla? Gadzooks, I say again.

The taste is everything you want it to be, gloriously sugary milk chocolate with coconut flake, and more than a hint of marshmallow and vanilla. Excellent barrel presence! Chewy, thick mouthfeel, and thankfully despite the obvious sugary aspect to the beer, it never becomes cloying, even as it warms. Now, it is 15% ABV, so it's not surprising when the booze makes its presence known on the backend of each sip, providing a nice warming aspect. Certainly can't drink more than 1 in a sitting, but taking down the 12oz. solo isn't an issue at all. This beer is absolutely delicious, and I'm so happy I have 3 more.

650ml bottle. Served in a snifter glass.

Mouthfeel is medium bodied, a bit thin for a 15% beer, but still a solid body nevertheless. Fair amount of carbonation. Sticky, slick coating mouthfeel. Definitely a sipper because of that, but not because it's 15%. There isn't that much heat, just more bourbon flavouring than any kind of fusel. Overall, an interesting BA imperial porter with some adjunct flavours to it, decent execution. I'm not sure what adjuncts are actually used in this, but I definitely got bourbon barrels and cinnamon, perhaps vanilla bean. Not sure what else was used. Definitely a more than solid BA porter.

Stevedore, Aug 18, 2015
4.48/5  rDev -1.1%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

I picked up a bottle of Perrin's No Rules a few weeks ago at The Lager Mill. I'm very interested to see how this one turns out, so lets see how it goes. 2015 Vintage. Poured from a brown 22oz bottle into a Teku.

A- The label looks good, it's very obvious it's a Big Lebowski reference and it's very eye catching. It poured a very dark brown almost black color that takes on more of dark brown hue when held to the light with a little under a fingers worth of creamy tan head that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left a little bit of patchy lacing behind. This is a very good looking beer it's just how I expect an Imperial Porter to look.

S- The aroma starts off with a lower amount of high sweetness that imparts a big maple syrup and brown sugar like aromas that go right into some big caramel, toffee and light butterscotch aromas that come together to make for a rich and decadent aroma. Up next comes the barrel imparting some great coconut, oak, vanilla and bourbon like aromas without being overpowering. Towards the end comes a nice chocolate and cocoa like aroma. with a noticeable alcohol aroma that isn't too boozy for being 15&. This beer has a great aroma, it's pretty balanced.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts of with a higher amount of sweetness with those maple syrup and brown sugar flavors being the first to show up and the maple syrup sticks out the most this time. Up next comes that same mixture of caramel, toffee and light butterscotch that I got in the aroma and goes right into the barrels which seem to show up just a little more than they did in the aroma and they impart the same aspects. Towards the end comes some nice cocoa flavors and this times there's just a little bit of dark fruit flavors showing up. On the finish there's no bitterness with sweetness and alcohol taking it's place, it's for sure a little boozy, but not that bad for 15%. This is a great tasting beer, but I think it would truly be world class if it was a little less sweet.

M- A little smooth, a little cloying, on the very thick side of being medium bodied with a lower amount of medium carbonation. The mouthfeel is very nice, but I wish it was a little less cloying.

Overall I thought this was a great beer and great example of the style, I didn't hear much about this beer till I saw it at the beer store and knowing my history with Perrin I knew there was a better chance that it would be a hit than a miss, thankfully I was right. This beer had nice drinkability, but one glass would be my limit anymore than that would be overkill and because of the sweetness I don't think I would want to drink a full bottle. What I like the most about this beer is how well all the different aspects came together, it just made for some great aromas and flavors. What I like the lest about this beer was the sweetness, I think it needs to be toned down just a little. I would for sure buy this beer again, I would be interested to see how it ages, but I think it's about as good as it's going to get right now. I would for sure recommend this beer, if you like big bourbon barrel aged beers I think it's a must try. All in all I was a little impressed with this beer, I thought it was going to be good, but it exceeded my expcatations it left me with a pretty tasty beer that I really enjoyed, but I can't help but think this beer would be perfect for a winter/ Holiday season release. IT's my new favorite Bourbon barrel aged Imperial Porter, Arcadia's Shipwreck Porter held the crown for many years, but this beer takes the style to a whole new level and it's now my favorite of the style and my favorite beer from Perrin. Great job guys, this beer can hold it's own against some of the better bourbon barrel aged beers, keep up the great work.

Ri0, Sep 27, 2015
4.15/5  rDev -8.4%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

Enjoyed out of a 22 oz brown bomber with no bottling date that I noticed, but given the style and the fact that this was only released a few weeks ago, I'm not terribly concerned. Time to see if this one lives up to the crazy buzz that's been surrounding it--poured into a large brandy-style snifter.

Appearance (4.25) - Pours a dark cola color from the bottle that becomes a fairly matte black-brown once it is all collected in the glass. If I hold it up to the light, I can see those same cola colored notes on the edges of the darker body. Atop sits a finger toasted marshmallow colored head that looks somewhat pillowy and only retains for a minute or so before turning into a ringlet around the edges of the glass. No lacing to be seen whatsoever, but some faint legs are present for a bit when the glass is tipped.

Smell (4.25) - There is a considerable sweetness on the smell that contains therein pleasant notes of coconut, brown sugar, and faint cinnamon. I'm really enjoying the gentle intertwining of the coconut and cinnamon, which both come through in a subtle and balanced manner, but I must admit a little trepidation at the fact that I can't really detect any alcohol (for a 15% beer no less!) or the contrapuntal notes of roast and bittersweet chocolate that are quintessential to the porter style and might serve to offset the sweeter elements. But for now, I'll put such worries aside and enjoy the delicious smell of this dessert beer.

Taste (4.0) - Initially, the wonderful balance of coconut and cinnamon that were previewed by the smell makes for a great first impression. Coconut and cinnamon are two of my favorite ingredients in a beer, but I think this is the first time I'd ever had them side by side in one beer. However, as I work my way through the glass I begin to get fatigued by the increasingly cloying sweetness that I can only assume comes from the use of the turbinado sugar. It seems my initial reservations about the smell lacking any elements of booziness or roasted char flavors have been realized, as I really want another flavor profile to cut through the confectionary melange from the coconut, sugar and cinnamon. The sweeter profiles all but drown out the barrel-aging; I'm sure there are some notes of vanilla and caramel, but it's hard to pick them out of the crowd. With some warmth, the sweetness is tempered a bit as the booze comes through more, but it's still sweet enough that I decide to cap my bottle to finish the second half the following night.

Mouthfeel (4.25) - Carbonation is on the low side of moderate and the body is medium-heavy in thickness. I wish it were a bit thicker, because I can't help but wonder if it would support the sweetness a bit better. The end result is a nice feel that gives the impression of slickness at times.

Overall (4.25), a beer that I enjoyed quite a bit while simultaneously coming away disappointed. It starts off so well, but becomes maddeningly monotonous as you work through the bottle. 22 oz was simply too much for me to finish in one sitting, but I can imagine that this would be a huge hit at settings where you only get 5-6 oz at a time. There are some excellent things going on in this beer, but it certainly was a lesson for me in Too Much Of A Good Thing. I will definitely be sharing my other bottles rather than taking them to the face like I tried to do with this one.
