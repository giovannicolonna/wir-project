Bottle 335 of 300....mmmm...math

Bottle split blindly with Kyle

Morning Delight is nothing if not a very unique and interesting beer. Super sugary sweet, overloaded molasses and maple upon a massive wave of fresh roasted coffee. Mouthfeel is thick, rich, and coating. Pancakes in a glass is more than appropriate...drinks batter-like with a big cup of coffee tossed on top.

What Morning Delight gives in a very interesting and huge take on a breakfast style stout, it takes in the cloying, rather difficult beer to work through. Blasphemous perhaps, but this beer is really too much and could use a little reigning in. A beer to be shared for certain. Would I like to try this beer again in four packs that may be more readily accessible? Sure. Is this worth trading away a bunch of great beer just to try? If you're a glutton for punishment, over-trading, or instant cavities, then by all means.

c-rum, Sep 18, 2015
4.5/5  rDev -5.5%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

I had this on-tap at the Toppling Goliath tap room. It was served in a chalice tulip. Mornin' Delight was reviewed from notes.

Appearance- There was only a slight ring of sandy colored head for a cap.The body is completely black, just like your morning cup o' joe.

Smell- Oh yeah, tons of iced coffee smell in this one without being overly roasty. There are notes of vanilla and even maple syrup! It also has an oat-like character to it as well. This is going to be great!

Taste- Just absolute gobs of maple and vanilla hitting the palate right away. The iced coffee then starts coming in waves. There is also a slightly sour and tangy feel that is most like dark cherries.

The finish is really strong on maple and iced coffee filling up the mouth. Man, this is an absolute blast to drink!

Mouth feel- Yep, this is how Imperial Stouts should feel. It has great coating and is definitely chewy. The body is pretty big as well.

Overall- The coffee and maple character of this beer is excellent. I would drink this all day, but at 9%, which you cannot tell by the way, I would probably regret doing that fairly quickly.

There are really no significant flaws in this stout. If you ever make it to the TG taproom, this one HAS to be in your top two on the list of beers to try.
