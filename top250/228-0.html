Appearance: Arrives very cloudy, with an impressive and perky head capping the amber colored liquid; nice sheets of lacing

Smell: Tropical fruit aromatics, with mango, apricot, peach and papaya

Taste: Just a hint of cookie dough malts and then the fruit flavors arrive, bringing all the elements detected in the aroma; tangerine builds, after the swallow to add a slight bitter element to the fray; the finish is a fruit salad of Florida flavor

Mouthfeel: Medium to full body, a bit of a tongue coater; low to moderate carbonation

Overall: A terrific IPA; if the availability were broader it would easily make the Top 250

Poured into a Haw River Farmhouse Ales teku glass. Crowler filled 2-9-15. Thanks a bunch, colt3ninja!

A- Pours a hazy medium amber color with a 1/2 inch beige head that retains well before becoming a thin ring around the edge of the glass and a large center patch of surface foam. resilient lacing trickles dlowly down the glass.

S- Pungent, juicy hop aroma reminds me of a homebrewed IPA. Resinous notes of pineapple, mango and orange. Also a bit grassy plus I get this hint of green pepper or poblano pepper that's surprising but nice.

T- Smooth citrus hop flavor that's moderately bitter but doesn't linger long. Malt is there just enough to provide some support. Ah and, now that I'm looking for it, there's that same green pepper element in the flavor that I found in the aroma.

M- Tangy, bright and crisp with medium carbonation and a body that leans toward the lighter side.

O- Great stuff. A very unique approach to a single IPA. Very drinkable and refreshing with a dynamite aroma. For the record, I love green peppers.

dar482, Feb 18, 2015
3.99/5  rDev -10.5%

Crowler brought back from Tampa. So many duplicate listings on here, this one has more ratings. Full notes taken to IPad during consumption.

A- Semi cloudy golden yellow beer out of the 32oz can to a few wine glasses. A creamy white head in all the glasses to just above a finger. Huge sheets of lace with ample spots.

S- Hops dominate the aroma with bright fruits. Mostly citrus and stone fruits but not a juicy nectar type profile. Oranges, grapes, tangerines and some mango.

T- Feels a bit fresher here somehow with once again a fruity but not pulpy or juicy hop profile. Clementine, bitter citrus pith, oranges, mango, apricot and tangerines make up most of the flavor. Grapes, berries, light cracked grains and bread round it out.

MF- Light to medium bodied overall with a crisp and fizzy foamy consistency. Slight creaminess from the high carbonation. Stone fruits lead to a medium clean bitterness. A bit seltzer like.

Stone fruits like mango and apricot lead to big tangerines in a fruity but crisp and not quite nectar hop profile. Lacks a certain fresh edge that other fruity IPAs have but still a refreshing and tasty brew.

Pours gold. GIANT mango pervades every inch of the beer's flavor and aroma. Mango up front, mango in the middle, mango, passion fruit, and grapefruit in the back end. Big, bold, bright, but never too sweet, never gimmicky.

I look at single-hopped Citra IPAs as existing on a continuum between two poles. Previously, those poles were marked "Zombie Dust," which is very bitter, and "Psuedosue," which is very fruity. Crank destroys that old continuum, blasting so far beyond Sue's fruitiness I could have sworn I was drinking fruit juice. Amazing.
