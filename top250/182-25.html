22oz bomber
L - pours a murky turbid rust brown. Lots of fine particulates. Opaque with no real head or lacing at all.
S - Oxidized sherry and dried fruit notes with a harsh sharpness and sour tones
T - Sweet, oxidized and sharp. Vanilla and oak with a hint of barrel and no booze much.
F - medium bodied, barely any carb

Overall a particularly challenging old ale / barleywine. Over-oxidized and sharp. Rapidly heading toward sour, and not something I would be keen to ever try again. This is what I imagine a barleywine turns into when you forget about it in the cellar for 20 years.
Stomach turning eventually due to the soured notes - made it through about 8-9 ounces over the course of an hour but couldn't finish a serve.

Once on tap at the release, once more from bottle two weeks after, into tulip glass.

A: This is dark! Like the color of the gluttonous soul that is always on the search for that perfect (English) barleywine. Yes. I'm talking about me. It is murky, it is maroon, it is mahogany.

N: Sweet raisins, slightly woody, like sticking your head into the bottom of your tank and smelling the spent grains and settled yeast.

T: Your standard taste profile of a well-brewed English: Big malts, barrel bits, dried fruits, slightly buttery with some oak and barrel coming from the top of your mouth to the back of your nose.

M: Creamy, smooth, right amount of burn. Makes you feel like you need to chew it a little before you down that sip and take another.

O: It's definitely worth that 20 minutes of waiting in the queue. Oh, you didn't bother to go down to Hickory and now had to pay premium price for a bottle? Too bad for you. Me and my case are laughing at you.

This is a great beer. The various vintages, up to 7 years in the waiting, were blended into this barleywine.

The barrel aging is.... perfect. It's balanced, and complete. The beer is huge without being cloying, sweet, or too boozy. The body is the perfect amount for drinkability. There is a slightly tannic finish from the oak and bourbon, but the finish and the body is so much drier than some barleywines, or appears so, that it makes it really drinkable.

World class BA barleywine for sure, much, much better than BCBBW in so many ways.

Deep, dark mahogany poured into a snifter. Very minimal foam that disappears quickly. Smells of leather, molasses, brown sugar, coconut, and dates, hovering over undertones of cured meats and tobacco; the barrel is strong in this one. The flavors are quite rich and decadent, with a cascade of raisins, cocoa, and sherry, followed by a long finish of figs and fudge. There is a tremendous amount of bourbon character, but without being hot or overly boozy; this drinks very smooth and is truly delightful.

With seven years of patiently barrel-aged beer involved in the making of Omega Point, I can't wait to see what Olde Hickory will churn out for their 25th anniversary.

AppaZappa, Mar 17, 2015
4.94/5  rDev +10.5%

LOOK - Dark murky brown, little to no head if poured smooth. I prefer a dark garnet, not beautiful but definitely acceptable for style.

SMELL - Oh lord, I could smell and try and dissect this beer for days. Alcohol is up front along with the following that swirl in and out of prominence: sweet oaky caramel, toasted marshmallow, leather, tobacco, bourbon-y vanilla, and also a touch of red wine and honey in the background.

TASTE - Wow. Ron Swanson would love this beer. Wood absolutely all over the place! Caramel, charred oak, big tobacco, big raisin, with some dark chocolate on the finish. The molasses sweetness increases as it warms. Insanely complex.

FEEL - Perfectly carbonated, will be slicker with age. A bit chewy. Definitely coats the tongue.

OVERALL - Not the prettiest beer ever made, but one of the damn finest.

This is my first ever beer review, but I had to do it, this beer blew me away.

Poured into a matching Omega Point snifter, slightly just below room temp on 3/28/15. Bottled 1/25/14 I think. Very strong, sweet aroma, plenty of bourbon and barrel on the nose, pours a beautiful caramel color with a thin tan head that just sits a second and then dissipates. It's wonderful to just smell it. Taste matches the aroma, sweet, caramel, a little toffee and vanilla, then bourbon. It's got some heat but not offputting, and the raisin/plum component comes in quickly giving you a full sensation of a perfect barrel aged barleywine. No flavor is too much and they all blend in your mouth without harshness. One of the best beers I've ever had, and one I need a ton more of. On par with King Henry easily, better than BCBBW in my opinion.
