Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: ThatsThatDude on 03-10-2015

We hear you guys are digging our hop forward beers...so we busted out another double IPA to celebrate our 2nd anniversary. UPPER CASE has a delicate, dry pilsner malt character with a smooth, soft, doughy mouthfeel from the raw wheat, oily hop resin which all serves as a canvas for this twice dry hopped 9% double IPA. Overipe mango, pineapple and passion fruit aromas leap out as the beer is poured. The impression of tropical fruit also takes the lead in the flavor which is layered further by white wine, pine resin and grapefruit zest. Hopped primarily with Mosaic with supporting roles played by Galaxy, Citra and Columbus. We figured there will be considerable interest, so brewed 3 batches!

Bottle. Pours a cloudy, marmalade color. Thin, soupy head. Random, patchy lacing. Whoa. The aroma is absolute hop juice. The Mosaic hops pummel my nose. Some people don't enjoy the almost oniony quality of Mosaic, but I love it. It's also incredibly tropical-fruity. A previous reviewer cited descriptors such as "cilantro and plant stems" and they nailed it. Still, the tropical fruit medley is impressive. I get a ton of guava, mango and pineapple. The flavor explodes once it hits my tongue. Such a concentrated hoppiness. Elixir, extract, nectar. The green onion, cilantro and stem-spice of the Mosaic is in elite form. Galaxy, Citra and Columbus hops also add plenty of hoppy depth. Mango, papaya, pineapple and guava hop flavors. I get signature Citra flavor as well as a passion fruit/moss flavor combo that I equate with Galaxy. It's half tropical and half green. I really love when a hoppy brew can balance between those elements. The malt is signature Trillium dense pastry and sugar. It really props up the lupulin. Body is creamy and fairly full. Bitterness isn't necessarily searing. It lingers but doesn't obliterate. I enjoyed this beer a ton. Up there with Heavy Mettle and Fort Point in my book. It really reminds me of Dinner by Maine Beer. Maybe not for everyone but definitely fits my tastes.

Flounder57, Apr 15, 2015
4.73/5  rDev +3.5%
look: 4.25 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.75

Drank from a 25.4 fl oz (750 ml) bottle purchased at Craft Beer Cellar, Belmont, MA
032415
Served in a snifter

Slightly hazed, golden yellow appearance with amber accents. The beer was much more transparent than your average hoppy offering from Trillium. A small, bubbly, off-white head topped the liquid but it quickly faded to a thick, lasting ring. Streaky, sticky lacing trickled along the glass.

The nose was made up of simply everything you could possibly want to find in a double IPA. Incredibly hoppy and dank with intense tropical fruit notes. There was an equal balance between marijuana and bright, lively passionfruit. Mango also came through strong. Rich and fruity. Moderately sweet. Super juicy with loads of peach and tangerine. A subtle, honey-like quality appeared as well.

The flavor profile was super hoppy and intense yet remarkably well balanced. Tangerine notes were most apparent. Perhaps some canned mandarin slices. Uber juicy and fruity. Nice pale malt backbone. Just enough for balance, but not too much to get in the way of the hops. Peachy with tropical passionfruit notes. Touch of candied orange peel on the finish but absolutely no bitterness. The 9% ABV was nowhere to be found. After the swallow, a hint of rind-like pithiness appeared.

On the palate, the beer was frothy and foamy. A bit pickly. The liquid contained a lively, spritzy effervescence. A bit thinner and more carbonated than Trillium's other DIPAs.

Upper Case is a cleaner, thinner and more balanced double IPA than what I am used to from the guys at Trillium. Although I loved the incredibly complex, intense and rounded flavor profile, I felt it lacked a bit of the intensity and brashness found in Vicinity and the Mettle variants.
