Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: American_Hawk on 11-13-2014

First brewed in celebration of the first anniversary of Row 34, our favorite Fort Point oyster bar and craft beer purveyor, Vicinity highlights a neighborhood of passion and vision. Plenty of spicy, earthy, tropical fruit aromas followed by complex flavors of grape juice, light citrus and lychee on the palate. The pilsner malt provides a crisp, clean, minerally finish. Soft mouthfeel, a restrained bitterness on the finish.

Pours a hazy, golden yellow in color with 2 fingers of pillowy off white head that leaves moderate lacing and leaves just a thin cap. Aroma is predominantly hoppy with lots of grapefruit, tangerine, mango and lemon. There are also some "dank" notes that are present throughout but linger in the background. Taste is much like the aroma with a strong hoppy profile. Good mouthfeel with medium body and medium+carbonation. There is a bit of alcohol here but nothing overly hot. OWhile hops were the dominant aroma and flavor they were somewhat muted for a DIPA. I'm a fan of Trillium, especially their IPAs but I was a bit underwhelmed here. This is a solid DIPA and while I won't go as far as saying that I was disappointed I will say that I expected more.

tywhite, Sep 26, 2015
4.66/5  rDev +5.2%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.75

Poured from bottle dated 1/20 into tulip glass.

Appearance: Hazy, cloudy orange-yellow with a THICK, pillowy head. Spirals of carbonation throughout.

Smell: This beer absolutely floored me with regard to smell. Blasted with citrus rind, tangerine, and mango. Very tropical, with some maltiness hidden behind the juice blast that you first smell.

Taste: An absolute juice-bomb......starts piney and finishes with some of the most powerful tropical flavors I've ever experienced from an IIPA. The taste lived up to the smell and more. I expected fruit and citrusy flavors from the look and smell, but the taste was mind-blowing.

Feel: not as thick and sticky as some of the other Trillium brews, but the feel fits the beer. Smooth and medium-thick.

Overall: The best IIPA I've had from Trillium, and one of the better brews I've had in a long, long time. Delicious.
