32 oz. growler (filled today) poured into a tulip

Pours a hazy golden-orange in color with a solid finger+ of fluffy white head that fades rather quickly to a thin coating leaving behind minimal lacing. Aroma is quite nice, strong hop character. Taste follows the nose and is excellent, lots of citrus and tropical fruits with slight dank notes. Pineapple, grapefruit, orange, lemon, lime, pine. A touch of malt and a dry, clean finish. solid but not overwhelming bitterness most prevalent in the finish. Medium body and mouthfeel; moderate carbonation, alcohol is well masked. Overall this is excellent beer; incredibly complex aroma and taste, this is unabashedly hoppy but not overwhelming bitter.

Fresh 750ml bottle poured into a tulip

Look - Typical Trillium - New England look. A gorgeous light golden orange color. Super hazy. Fluffy white head that leaves awesome lacing almost immediately. Sexy looking beer.
Smell - The nose blew me away! Papaya, mangos and oranges all jump out of the glass at you. Hints of pineapple, citrus and pine thrown in there as well. If I could bottle this stuff up I'd wear it as cologne.
Taste - A wonderful wave of grapefruit, grapefruit pith and pine hit the taste buds. Get some tropical fruits in there mid palate and a slight pop of bitterness to round things out. Start to get more pineapple and mangos as the beer warms a little. A more aggressive tasting beer than the Congress Street I drank the night before. AWESOME none-the-less.

Overall - Trillium has quickly become my new favorite brewery. Wish I had access all the time to their beers! Their IPAs are truly something special.
