pseudoSue pours a light orange color with a good amount of white foamy head. Nose is tropical and has some notes of earthy, piney hops. The nose is actually lighter than I expected. But this is a pale ale, not an IPA, so I guess that makes sense. However, the taste is definitely hops galore! Super tropical fruity goodness. I personally love the Citra hop as is, and Toppling Goliath has done something truly wonderful with it here. This one has a very nice soft, carbonated mouthfeel. This is my first beer from TG, and if the others are anywhere near as good as Sue, I look forward to trying more.

Beer pours a rich gold with a little bit of haze and red highlights. Very good looking and inviting with a about 1.5 fingers of off-white head. Lacing seems to stick a little.

Citra punch to the face. I heard the nose was a little overrated with this beer, but I have to disagree. Reminds me of Zombie Dust for sure. Some spice and an overload of juicy citrus (tangerine, grapefruit, lemon peel). Fantastic.

The flavor is quite pleasant, but it is honestly bit of a let down after such a huge and promising smell. There isn't really the depth ZD has. Pseudo seems a little watered down in comparison. The aftertaste is especially disappointing, as it lacks a the unique aftereffect that many citras seem to impart. Still, the flavor matches the smell in character and overall the flavor is well above average.

Mouthfeel is pretty spot on, besides the watered-down nature of the beer--which I believe derives from the flavor. Medium carb. Creamy and extremely drinkable.

Overall this is a fantastic pale ale. Not the best, but still high on my favorite list. IMO, this is pretty apples and oranges when compared with ZD. The nose is the only thing that really reminds me of it. The malt bill is clearly different though, and not as refined. I also think the citra character, whill amazing, is a little muted.

22oz bottle from IAmJacksHopTongue (freakin awesome extra, thanks man!). No bottling info. Served in a Terrapin snifter.

Pours a soft pale orange with just under two fingers of white head. The healthy layer of froth that remains leaves some sizeable sheets of lace.

Vibrant citrusy aromas of oranges, clementines, mango, pineapple and just a hint of malt sweetness.

Tastes just as bright as the nose promised. Clementines, pineapple, mango, mandarins, grapefruit peel, some cracker and mild lemon drops. Layer upon layer of Citra hop goodness without a hint of alcohol. Delicious and ridiculously easy to drink.

Fairly light body but not too much so. Moderately sharp carbonation. Fairly bitter citrus zest finish with oranges, grapefruit and kumquat.

One of the best American pale ales that I've tried. Certainly among the upper crust of Citra single hopped brews out there. Very worthy. This is one of the two or three truly special APAs that I'd actually drink regularly if I had access to it.

22oz. bomber bottle with a super cool fossil gray label with a bright neon green border. The label looks like a rock with "TG" in the middle and the mouth of a T-Rex in the lower left hand corner. Written over the label in pencil etching is the name of the beer with the "S" in Sue being highlighted in neon green. To the far left of the label is all the mandatory US information. To the far right of the label is where you'll find a small paragraph that briefly describes the beer. Pint glass? No! This special beer deserves to be poured into a Snifter. I poured this mid west beer into my Florida born Peg's Cantina Snifter.

From behind the T-Rex came a super hazy bright kumquat orange colored beer. It had that type haziness where I could briefly see my fingers on the other side of the glass. It was all of the beer's hop particles that made it so hazy. The initial head of foam was somewhere between 1/4 to 1/2 an inch in height and pearl white in color. It had good retention that left behind a tiny string of rings and just a spot or two of lace around the glass. Beer eye candy all the way!

Damn! As soon as the cap came off, out comes a strong aroma of bitter citrus hops and citrus fruits. When the beer settled in the glass, the beer was still hugely hopped. Nothing could calm down the hops. The aroma was basically one big giant citrus smell with a semi sweet undertone. The aroma contained bitter citrus hops, lemon, tart grapefruit, oranges, kumquats, mango, and even a little pineapple. Despite this aroma being hugely hoppy and bitter, my nose picked up a faint whiff of bready malts in the finish. My nose couldn't get enough of this beer.

At least my tastebuds weren't jealous of my sense of smell. My tastebuds got to taste the same hugely bitter citrus hops and citrus fruits that my nose got to smell in the aroma. The taste was dry and hoppy with a ton of bitter citrus hops. These hops danced on my tongue for a long time before it let through the citrus/exotic fruits. This giant fruit punch had the taste of lemon, tart grapefruit, a gentle touch of orange, and a drop of mango and pineapple. The fruits were more powerful in the aroma. After the fruits had their fun, the beer then ends with a little pine and a brief taste of bready malts. Hop Bomb? Oh yeah!

The label said that this beer has a 6% ABV. Really? Well anyway, this beer was medium bodied with its fair share of carbonation and a hugely bitter aftertaste. The aftertaste was one dimensional. There wasn't much of a variety of tastes in this beer which is fine with me. The aftertaste contained a large amount of bitter citrus hops, grapefruit, lemon, and mango. This aftertaste will linger on the roof of your mouth for a long time.

First of all, I have to thank my new BA friend mnbuffalo for sending me this beer. This is one of those beers that disappears right in front of my eyes. It's so good that I'll take a sip here, another sip there, and before I knew it, the bottle was emptied out. Would I drink it again? Oh yeah!! More! More!

Poured from a bomber into a tasting glass.

A: Hazy, medium golden color with one finger of diminishing white foam head. Light lacing remains on the glass.

S: Tropical fruit of moderate intensity really expands in the nose given time. Grapefruit can also be identified as separate but complimentary.

T: Begins barely off-dry, with refined pale malt and sticky tropical fruit hops aromatics that match the smell closely. Acidity is bright and lively early, preventing the hops flavors from turning oppressive. The finish is clean and moderately bitter for the style, and also a little soapy.

M: Thin to medium viscosity, balanced on the palate, with low carbonation.

D/O: This beer is representative of Toppling Goliath's talent for channeling aggressive dry-hopped qualities, while also looking like one of their pale ales (unfortunately). A bit edgier than Three Floyds Zombie Dust, Citra hops border on bold here which is saying a lot for the delicious but docile cultivar. It is also true to the form of the brewer in that is shows no yeast character, which seems to be one of the most unifying features across the Toppling Goliath portfolio.

Received this from BA BottleCaps80 as part of a small trade, as I've been curious to try it for a long time. Enjoyed out of a 22 oz brown bomber with no discernible dating on the label or bottle. That said, I know this was bottled within the last couple weeks, so it's pretty fresh. Poured into a nonic style tumbler glass.

Appearance - Pours a crystal clear copper orange color that allows me to see right through to the other side of the glass. EDIT: after reading some of the other reviews that all seem to mention opacity from hazy to murky, I can't help but wonder if mind is so translucent because of how close to bottling it is. Anyway, back to the review. Not much variation of hue except for a slight amber highlight around the bottom of the liquid. Atop the body sits a frothy, towering three finger cream-colored head with a slightly orange tint. Retention is moderate, with sudsy lace that eventually dries leaving faint sticky ring impressions around the side of the glass. I've seen more beautiful beers, but this one hits all the basics.

Smell - Such an intoxicatingly delicious aroma that I'm conflicted over whether I want to dive in or sit here inhaling for several minutes. A bouquet of citrus that reminds me of that aroma you get right when you cut into a fresh fruit. Tangerine, mango, sweet pineapple, and even just a hint of grapefruit pith, but without any of the pungency that you get from an IPA. There is just enough pale malt presence provide a water cracker base for the wonderful Citra topping.

Taste - The promise of the smell is delivered on with interest, with the citrus and tropical fruits from before now succulent and juicy with again hints of mango and clementine with accents of grapefruit zest, pineapple and guava. Again there is just enough malt to give this one some nice body, and the fruit profile is so prevalent that it tricks the palate into thinking there is a lot of sweetness when in reality, the only sweetness comes from the grain base. Bitterness is low to nonexistent.

Mouthfeel - Medium-to-light bodied and moderate carbonation, with an incredible juiciness that makes this beer infinitely drinkable.

Overall, just an outstanding beer. It reminds me of ZD in the way the fruit is so aggressive without kicking into that level of pungency and bitterness that you get from an IPA. It's one of those beers for which the taste is so refreshing that it's easy to take big draws out of the glass because it almost gives the impression that it's quenching your thirst. I'm halfway tempted to sit on my second bottle for a month or so to see if the opacity, nose and taste change significantly, as it's not often that I've gotten a hop-forward ale that was this fresh. I say halfway tempted because it's so good that I doubt I'm going to have that kind of willpower.
