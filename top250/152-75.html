Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: jhartley on 12-24-2014

Up until this brew, none of Lagunitas’ barrel-aged adventures ever made it to shelves. They’ve been super-limited draft-only releases. This time we’ve made enough to actually bottle some … The 100% barrel-aged 2015 High West-ified Coffee Stout. Ale brewed with Coffee from our friends at Petaluma Coffee & Tea, then aged in Rye Whiskey barrels from our friends at High West Distillery in Park City, Utah … barrels that held High West’s Double Rye and American Prairie Bourbon, to be exact. Cheers!

zimm421, Sep 20, 2015
4.16/5  rDev -5.2%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4.25

650ml bottle poured into a Lagunitas mason jar. Enjoyed on 3.31.2015, reviewed from Tasting Journal.


The beer pours a nice, jet black color, very opaque and viscous with a thick, finger or so of khaki head, foamy and fluffy that has nice retention and leaves a wall of spotted lace behind. The aroma is great, rich bittersweet bakers chocolate, lightly roasted coffee, smooth oaky barrel presence with just a hint of warm bourbon and rye spirit, earthy that gives way to some smooth malt profile. The flavors open with plenty of chocolate, rich and decadent, but also with some underlying bitterness that emphasizes the coffee here, with is tame, but balanced and integrated, light bourbon but more of a rye character, spicy and warm, chewy oak, very earthy with smooth grain and a bit of sweet vanilla at the end. The mouthfeel is nice, it coats the mouth very well, medium body, higher than expected carbonation that is still quite gentle with a simple and easy finish, a bit light.

Verdict: A very nice barrel aged coffee stout from Lagunitas, a very impressive offering for their first bottled barrel aged beer. Balance is the name of the game, it's not overly boisterous or intense with any of the characteristics and can come across as somewhat tame in that regard, but it's the whole package.

Pours a silky, semi-oily tar black with a rather thick finger of frothy and creamy dark brown foam.

Big and bold coffee and roast aroma melds well with the slightly spicy rye bourbon notes.

At first sip, I was in love. Fresh coffee, dark chocolate, vanilla, cream, and some rye bourbon heat all compliment each other wonderfully.

Heavy-bodied, medium carbonation, roasty, silky smooth, but lacking thicker body I was looking for in a brew this heavy.

Overall, for $8.99/22 oz bottle, you cannot pass this up. I'm glad I stocked up on these as the beer isn't the best thing to happen to beer, but it is solid and just outstanding.

Not sure what to expect here. More barrel and less coffee or vice versa. I hope it's the former. Let's find out. 22 oz. bomber into a teku glass.

T - Oh wow, this is really excellent. Extremely similar to KBS just a way better price ;). I get coffee, but not too much. I get barrel, but not too much. I get roasted malt, and just enough. A really well-made beer.

M - Full and frothy, exactly the way I want my stouts to be. It's like spraying whipped cream into my mouth, except the whipped cream is chocolate and coffee flavored.

O - Wow, I'm blown away. Granted, if I didn't only use whole number ratings this would definitely NOT get a 5, but everything is closer to a 5 than a 4. I should really pick up a few more of these as they're just sitting there on the shelves of my local store for $9.99 a pop.
