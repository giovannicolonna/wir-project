Notes & Commercial Description:
Beer added by: jplopez21 on 10-04-2013

Aged in the third-use barrels that were once home to Kentucky bourbon and then our renowned Bourbon County Stout, this traditional English-style barleywine possesses the subtlety of flavor that only comes from a barrel that’s gone through many seasons of ritual care. The intricacies of the previous barrel denizens – oak, charcoal, hints of tobacco and vanilla, and that signature bourbon heat – are all present in this beer. Hearty and complex, Bourbon County Brand Barleywine is a titan and a timeline; a bold, flavorful journey through the craft of barrel aging.

I got a bottle of Goose Island's Bourbon County Brand Barleywine Ale that I got in a trade, thanks a lot Kevin. I've been wanting to try this beer ever since I first heard about it and since it never showed up at any of my local beer stores I had to trade for it, I'm expecting great things, so lets see how it goes. 2013 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a snifter.

A- The label looks nice, it's got a good clean and classy look to it. It poured a nice dark brown color that took on more of a caramel color when held to the light with just a thin ring of tan head that quickly faded to pretty much nothing and it didn't leave and lacing behind. This was a nice looking beer, I like the color, but the head and lacing were pretty much non existent.

S- The aroma starts off with a higher amount of sweetness that has a lighter brown sugar like aroma and it's followed by the bourbon barrels being the first to show up and they impart some really nice, bourbon, oaky, and vanilla like aromas that remind me of an expensive bottle of Bourbon. Up next comes the malts which impart a some great caramel, light toffee, slight roast, very light cocoa, sweet malts and caramel malt like aromas with some nice dark fruit aroma showing up in the background with raisins and figs seeming to stick out the most. Towards the end comes a little more sweetness along with a light pipe tobacco like aroma and there's a noticeable alcohol aroma, but it isn't too strong. I thought this beer had a very good aroma, it was on the sweet side but i really liked how the different aspects worked so well with each other.

T- The taste seems to be really similar to the aroma and it starts off with a higher amount of sweetness that still has a brown sugar like aspect to it and it's followed by the bourbon barrel still being the first to show up and it imparts all those great aspects that it did in the aroma and they are at just the right level. Up next comes the malts that impart the exact same malt aspect that I got in the aroma and this time it seems like the caramel flavors stick out just a little more and I like that. Up next comes the dark fruits with some nice raisin and fig flavors coming through along with a really light pipe tobacco flavor. Towards the end comes some noticeable alcohol that's not too boozy and it's a little warming. On the finish there's a little more brown sugar sweetness with pretty much no bitterness and it has a caramel, bourbon barrel and dark fruit like aftertaste. This was a great tasting beer, it's everything I would want in a bourbon barrel aged Barleywine.

M- Fairly smooth, rich, a little sticky, almost slightly cloying, on the slightly lighter side of being full bodied with a slightly lower amount of medium carbonation. The mouthfeel was really great and it worked really well with this beer.

Overall I thought this was a great beer and I thought it was a great example of the style, it had everything that I look for in the style plus a little more and it didn't leave me disappointed in the slightest. This beer had good drinkability, it was fairly smooth, rich, a little sticky, almost slightly cloying, a little filling and the taste did a great job at holding my attention, I could drink a bottle by myself with no problem and it tastes so good that I would be pretty tempted to drink a couple of these in a row and If I had a lot I might do that. I think it makes for a great fall and winter time beer, it feels like it would keep me warm in the cold weather. What I like the most about this beer was how good the taste and aroma were and the great mouthfeel helped take this beer to a whole new level. What I like the least about this beer was appearance, it wasn't bad and I liked the color, but the head, lacing and retention were disappointing. I would for sure buy this beer again if I ever saw it and I hope it shows up at some of my local beer stores next year. I would recommend this beer to just about anyone, it's just one of those beers that you just have to try. All in all I was very happy with this beer and I was also a little impressed, I was already expecting this beer to be great and it ended up exceeding my expectations. I am really happy I decided to seek this beer out, this Bourbon County series has all been great. This is now my favorite Bourbon Barrel Barleywine, it's that damn good and it's up there as one of the better goose Island beers I've had, I still think I liked the regular and the coffee version slightly more, but that doesn't take away how great of beer this is and as it stands it's pretty much my ideal Bourbon Barrel Barleywine. Goose Island is really bringing the heat when it comes to these limited releases. Awesome job guys, this was one great beer, keep up the great work.

Edit I got to try a few samples of the 2014 batch, it's very nice, but I still prefer 13.

Fantastic beer and worth effort to obtain.

12 oz bottle into a tulip.

A: Pours a murky dark brown color. Practically no head is formed - there isn't even much of a collar. The 'head' is off-white color, I guess. It doesn't look like much at all, but given what this beer is, it's a moot point.

S: And guess what, sweet bourbon character hits the nostrils immediately. Vanilla, coconut, oak; you name it, it's here. Even better, it's backed by a wonderful dark fruit aroma and (slightly) lesser chocolate notes. This smells like it will be one of the best dessert beers ever.

T: Holy frig. The opening flavors are heavy in vanilla, coconut, and bourbon. But that finish...dear lord, dat finish - what a vibrant explosion of dark fruit goodness. The finish becomes more caramel-forward as it gradually opens up. There's a slight booze note post-swallow and it's hardly a distraction. Some slight barrel comes through here, too. This brew has a lot to offer in terms of complexity and flavor, with everything easily distinct yet harmonious and well balanced (if rather sweet - hey, it's a barleywine). In many ways, it's even better than regular BCBS.

M: The secret here is how much smoother it is than most in the style. Even with a faint alcoholic bite, it's wonderfully soft yet with a cake-like density. It goes down as easily as a 12% barrel-aged monster can go down.

O: I've had a few barrel-aged barleywines before (Sucaba and Insanity spring to mind), but this is so far above those two it's not even funny. Everything here is brilliantly defined without being moronic. Best barleywine I've ever had? More like one of the best beers I've ever had.

Curse you, InBev.
