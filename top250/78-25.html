Bottle from Gabriel, thanks a bunch! Pour a near black, dark brown hues, medium two-finer brown head emerges then fades to a ring. Aromas are of an initial sweet sticky bourbon, followed by sweet black strap molasses, treacle, light coffee, light vanilla and a hefty dose of cinnamon. Taste has lots of cinnamon. Seems to overwhelm the other flavors. Not as much coffee as I expected either. Maybe it was my expectations that came with this beer but it fell short of being world class for me in the mid-palate and finish. I was hoping for a bit more body in the mouthfeel....a bit more creaminess...and a bit more balance among the profile. Given the ingredients, I don't get a lot of depth but quick flashes and undertones of coffee/earth/cocoa. Finishes sweet with doses of cinnamon and molasses, wet oak and bourbon. While this certainly was a tasty beer, it was not quite on par with the other Top 50 Imperial Stouts that I’ve had the pleasure of trying.

Bomber poured into snifter...shared with the wife. She really liked this one
A: Black. Dark brown at the edges against light. Minimal dark brown head with slight oil slick on snifter
S: Chocolate bread pudding. The smell of this beer could be mistaken for a cinnamon roll
T: Wow. Sweet chocolate, molasses, bread pudding, cinnamon, subtle pepper with a touch of vanilla and maybe even some coconut. Aftertaste is incredible. Lingering chocolate, cinnamon, coffee, and a bit of bourbon
M: Minimal carbonation. Thick/slightly sticky. Coats the tongue. You can chew on this one a bit
O: Man this beer is good. Not sure it would improve with age and it's drinking great. Very well balanced. Crack this beer, it's delicious
