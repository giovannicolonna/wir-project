4.25/5  rDev -8%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

Dark brown / black, tan head that fades to a ring on the edges. Not intensely dense; no real sheeting or legs ... par for the course with Cycle's adjunct stouts. They keep it a bit lighter to allow the adjuncts to be a bit brighter and to tone down the sweetness. Makes them much more drinkable IMHO. Smell is heavy coffee, heavy cinnamon, a bit of ashy char / roast, a bit of caramel / vanilla bourbon barrel notes. The taste comes across as a bit bitter with dark coffee and cinnamon. Not quite acrid, but it's close to heavy handed. Char, roast, coffee / cinnamon gives the impression of drying out the base. More in the ashy / roasty vein that sweet and decadent. Well done and I would happily drink this frequently ... though scarcity and hype make it tough to deliver on cost of entry. There are comparable or superior beers that are considerably easier to acquire.

4.58/5  rDev -0.9%
look: 4 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.5

Bottle shared by Duck at Zwanze Day, thanks man.

This pours a deep, oily looking black, with a small head and no lacing.

Smells like sweet cinnamon- almost like sugary cinnamon coated frosting from a cinnamon bun, with chocolate, vanilla and coconut.

Interestingly, there's really no barrel character here, so I don't really have any indicators for what barrels were used- though I believe it's whiskey of some sort. The cinnamon is on point though- super forward in the taste but far from overpowering or fatiguing. There's just a small amount of coffee. There's way more coconut and vanilla. Those flavors could certainly be barrel derived. There's some chocolate and a little bit of roast, reminding you that this is indeed a beer.

This is medium bodied, thick but not chewy, with a lower level of carbonation.

A really excellent stout, great use of cinnamon.
