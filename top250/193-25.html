pagriley, Nov 08, 2014
4.55/5  rDev +2.7%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 5 | overall: 4.75

Canned on 10/07/14, consumed 10/31/14.

APPEARANCE: The bright orange color is muted, slightly, by a bit of a haze. The head is egg shell white and almost exactly two of my fat fingers thick. That said, it dies rather quickly, leaving some spotty lacing on the back side of the glass. A handful of slow moving bubble can be seen making their way towards the top of the glass as well.

SMELL: The fresh cut grass on the nose is huge and wonderful, however, there is a also a good dose of pine and orange. Some mango, and maybe pineapple, lurk beneath it all.

TASTE: Although it is not the most dominant flavor, the first flavor to stand out is the orange. It is just heavier on the orange than most other DIPAs I’ve had, which is a good thing. The grassiness from the nose is still there and it is absolutely beautiful. The resinous pine is also fairly big on this. There are simply a lot of very aggressive flavors here making this complex, but exceptionally drinkable. Some tropical fruit and a hint of grapefruit dance around the palate as well…that grapefruit really comes in towards the finish. The last DIPA I remember being this much of a roller coaster ride was Three Floyds Dreadnaught.

MOUTHFEEL: A little too good. This beer is gulpable…and at 10% that is a problem. Medium to slightly big bodied, the Unsessionable is semi-sweet to start with a finish that dries ever so slightly.

OVERALL: This is a supremely drinkable beer. I feel so privileged to have been able to drink this fresh out here in Colorado. I hope Rev decides to brew more in the future and I’ll buy a case next time.

JohnG190, Oct 28, 2014
4.5/5  rDev +1.6%

This seems like Chicago's answer to Hopslam to me. Brassy golden appearance with a decent head (in a pint glass, when poured at a slightly higher temp into a snifter it had an impressively thick, ivory-colored mane on it). Nose highlights the Australian hops (think Sucks or DirtWolf), but the taste is sweeter and the mouthfeel stickier -- hence the Hopslam comp. I hate to just review a great beer like this by only comparing it to other brews of its ilk, but to be honest it's an overdone style at this point and Unsessionable is a fantastic homage to its predecessors. The clever "anti-trend" name/marketing, and the fact that it's an elite Midwest DIPA in 6packs of cans are the differentiators, and I can see a spot for this in my beer fridge for the fall. Good stuff.
